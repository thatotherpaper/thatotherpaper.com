I’m officially sold on the Austin Ice Bats

26 March 2007 | Chris Trew

I’ve been putting it off for months now, but today… I did it. I shredded all to-do lists, ignored all party invitations, and cruised myself to the Chaparral Ice Arena to see the Austin Ice Bats take on the Rio Grande Valley Bees in the final game of the regular season. It’s six hours later and I’m still beaming.
Somehow, someway, I got everything that I wanted out of that hockey game, including:
- Two fights. One right in front of me. (danger!)
- An ejection. (scandalous!)
- A high scoring game. (oh la la!)
- A body check so hard that an entire pane of glass fell. (glee!)
- A close-up conversation with a player on the opposing team about how his team was not good and my team was very good. (intense!)
- Many opportunities to beat on the glass. (cheer!)
Hockey is an amazing sport to watch live and, IMO, the best sporting event to take a non-sports person to as long as you don’t try to explain what “icing” is. Unfortunately, since the Ice Bats missed out on post-season action, you’re going to have to wait until October to see Austin hockey.
