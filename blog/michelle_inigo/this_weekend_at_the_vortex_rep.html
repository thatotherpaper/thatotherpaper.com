This weekend at the Vortex: Reppy returns

11 October 2007 | Michelle Inigo

New York native and legendary Austin entertainer Reppy is returning to the Vortex Theater to perform her one-woman comedy show ReppyToire: A Tour de Farce this weekend, with a special performance happening tonight. This debut is the first of a trilogy – the next installments will happen in 2008. ReppyToire promises to be unique and sophisticated, free of hard-biting satire and risqué humor. Reppy’s old-fashioned humor creates an all-inclusive environment that breaks down social barriers.

courtesy Reppy 

“There’s an intimacy created,” explained Reppy. “My show goes beyond demographic and socio-economic backgrounds. It bypasses all those things and goes straight to the heart. It’s like we’re all in on a joke together.”

During the two-hour show, Reppy will have alternate conversations with seven of her 20 characters, using minimal props and relying mostly on facial expressions, voice alterations, and body language to differentiate between the characters. Among them will be six-year-old Rachel Caroline Ainsley, whom Reppy describes as a precocious little tyrant that has her mother wrapped around her finger; Carla Ana Maria Franchesca Gabriella Rigolleti, a tough-talking Catholic-Italian broad from the Bronx; and Mazis Randall, a 92-year old stand-up comedian who claims she still has her wits about her. Isn’t it easy to get lost between all the personalities?

“The characters are so real, but I’m actually a very normal person,” said Reppy.

Reppy began performing at the Chicago House located on Sixth Street nearly 20 years ago, and she packed the house every month. With the birth of her daughter, however, Reppy decided to take an extended break to be a mom. Meanwhile, the singer/songwriter/comedian/actress/playwright began teaching at the Austin Waldorf High School and founded its summer theater program. Her camps focus on learning and improving acting skills and self-esteem. They start in June and run through August; each one lasts two weeks.

In the fall of 2006, Reppy staged her comeback with two-sold out shows at the VORTEX, followed by her children’s show, ROMP. “Both shows were sold out – they had more people there than ever before.” Not able to follow the momentum that proceeded, Reppy once again stopped performing because it was interfering with her teaching. This time, the separation was short-lived. She resigned from teaching in June and now says, “I’m ready to do my thing.”

Tonight’s show will be a special benefit performance that will raise money for the Austin Waldorf School. For tickets, call 512-478-5282.

8pm October 11–13 | Pay what you can: $15–$30

The Vortex
2307 Manor Road • 478-5282
