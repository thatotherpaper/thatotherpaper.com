There sure are a lot of mosquitoes here

10 June 2007 | Kristin Hillery
photo / sookie 

I knew it.

A few days ago I was sitting outside on the patio at Flipnotics, and as soon as the sun went down, they came for me. I’d smack one mosquito off my arm, only to see another six feasting on my ankle. I left the place covered in tiny red bumps. And tears of shame.

So I wasn’t surprised when I saw this story from KVUE (“Mosquito population booms”), which points out that all the rain we’ve gotten has left us with literally bajillions of mosquitoes. Apparently mosquitoes love standing water – puddles and retention ponds, for example – and that’s the perfect place for lady mosquitoes to lay their eggs (and they hatch about 48 hours later).

What’s that you say? You want to know more about mosquitoes, like what that elevated bump that forms after they bite you is called? Read more here and here.
