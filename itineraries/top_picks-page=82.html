Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Jan 10-16

10 January 2007 | That Other Paper

Wednesday | January 10

photo / jenn tx 

Idiocracy

If you didn’t get a chance to see Mike Judge’s Idiocracy when it was in theaters for, like, two seconds (and playing in about, like, negative one billion cities besides Austin), do yourself a favor and watch it tonight at Beerland. Or just rent it and watch it at home, hermit.

7pm | Free

Beerland
711 Red River St • 479-7625

Thursday | January 11

Monkeywrench Books Benefit

Monkeywrench Books does some cool stuff for our community, so why not show up to a circus-themed bar to show ’em a little support? Starting at 7pm, Bright Light Social Hour will take the Carousel Lounge stage. They’ll be followed by Night Viking, Perennial Tuesday, Follow That Bird, Attic Ted, and This Dog’s a Lion. Don’t forget you can bring your own liquor to the Carousel Lounge, so pick out that top-shelf bottle of Everclear you’ve been saving – this is a special occasion.

Monkeywrench Books Benefit
7pm | BYOL | $5 suggested donation

512-Go!» Carousel Lounge
1110 E 52nd St • 452-6790

Friday | January 12

Kerri Lendo and her broken PowerBook at last week’s Punchline 

Punchline, presented by That Other Paper

Yep, it’s Punchline again. We’re not going to let you forget about the stand-up comedy show we sponsor at ColdTowne Theater every Friday night. At 10pm. To which you may BYOB. Come!

Punchline
10pm | BYOB | $5

512-Go!» ColdTowne Theater
4803 Airport Blvd • 524-2807

The festival runs Jan. 12-14 

Star of Texas Tattoo Art Revival

Do you like tattoos? Do you want to see a lot of tattoos? Palmer Events Center is housing a three-day celebration for the timeless art of defacing human skin. The event features top tattoo artists from around the world, the ever lustable Suicide Girls, Wizzard’s Legends of Pinstriping, art galleries, car exhibits, unique vendors, and tattoo contests. Weekend pass holders will also gain free admission (and shuttle rides) to Emo’s for shows on Friday and Saturday night.

Star of Texas Tattoo Art Revival
January 12-14
Weekend pass $40 | Day pass $15

Palmer Events Center
900 Barton Springs Rd • 404-4500

Saturday | January 13

photo / s m 

Frank Warren, author of The Secret Lives of Men and Women

Over three million people visit Frank Warren’s PostSecret website each month to view the “Sunday Secrets,” postcards mailed anonymously that contain illustrated confessions. As the second PostSecret book, The Secret Lives of Men and Women is a collection of postcards – some shocking, some upsetting, all beautiful – that have never been posted on the website that’s become therapy for so many. Tonight at BookPeople, Warren will be talking about the book, signing copies, and revealing his own secret.

7pm | Free

BookPeople
603 N Lamar Blvd • 472-5050

Sunday | January 14

photo / Kimberly Mead 

Trickster

This musical comedy is dedicated to asshol– tricksters. You know the type: They’re always pulling rabbits out of their pants and dancing and juggling shoehorns and stealing your money. Bonnie Cullum’s production celebrates mythological tricksters from a variety of cultural backgrounds that you have probably never heard of. Trickster aims to be experimental, funny, and very different than what you’d normally see at The Vortex.

8pm | $10-$30 (pick your price)

The Vortex
2307 Manor Rd • 478-5282

Monday | January 15

Martin Luther King March

Celebrate Martin Luther King Day and multiculturalism in Austin today by heading over to the MLK Statue on the East Mall of the University of Texas campus. Led by Grand Marshal State Representative Dawnna Dukes, the crowd will then march all the way to Huston-Tillotson University, where there will be a cultural festival with music, art, and food.

9am | Free

MLK Celebration

Tuesday | January 16

FronteraFest 2007: Short Fringe

FronteraFest kicks off tonight at Hyde Park Theatre. In its fourteenth year, FronteraFest features at least four plays under 25 minutes tonight that could be just about any format, from a single monologue to avant-garde dance. You never know what you’ll get, but it’s guaranteed to always be interesting.

8pm | $12

Hyde Park Theatre
511 West 43rd St • 479-PLAY
