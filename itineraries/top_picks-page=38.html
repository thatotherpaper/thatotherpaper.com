Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Nov 12 - 18

12 November 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | November 12

courtesy E.A.S.T 

East Austin Studio Tours preview / KOOP Radio Orientation

Have you ever been interested in participating in Austin’s community-run radio station, 91.7 KOOP? If so, you have two different opportunities each year – one in November and one in May. November 12 marks the beginning of this year’s fall training session. People interested in volunteering at KOOP or learning about radio programming can come to this orientation session where you can get general information or sign up for programmer training sessions. The orientation will start at 7pm in the AFL-CIO Building (1106 Lavaca). Afterwards, you can go to Emo’s to talk with people involved with KOOP and East Austin Studio Tours artists and organizers, with performances from Dracula’s Weed Smuggling Jam Engine, BASIC, and Zookeeper. Here you can pick up studio maps and get all the details you’ll need concerning East Austin Studio Tours Weekend coming up this weekend.

8pm | $2 suggested donation

Emo’s
603 Red River • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • $2 Music Monday: Punk’s Not Dead at Alamo Drafthouse Downtown | 9:45pm
  • Beer and Cheese at North by Northwest Restaurant and Brewery | 7pm

Tuesday | November 13

German Theater Abroad

Five actors comprise the GTA (German Theater Abroad), three Germans and two Americans; they are accompanied by two Austrian video artists who are tracking their journey across America. They started in New York with the goal of traveling across 16 states to 24 cities over the course of seven weeks, eventually ending up in LA. Each show plays for one night only, so this is your only chance to catch GTA for a while. And it should be worth it. Though fairly unknown in America, Roland Schimmelpfennig, 38, is a contemporary German playwright who has published 16 plays – all of which have been translated into many languages. This particular play is an ever-changing comic-farce, juxtaposing onstage performance with the constantly evolving video footage of GTA’s current tour, playfully exposing both German and American stereotypes.

8pm | $10–$30

The Vortex
2307 Manor Rd • Austin, TX 78722

Other options:

  • Zappa Plays Zappa at Hogg Auditorium | 8pm
  • White Denim, Golden Boys, Hugs at The Mohawk | 9pm

Wednesday | November 14

Weird Wednesday: Mister Scarface

This show every Wednesday at 11:55pm is never a letdown. And it’s free! If you haven’t seen a show at the new Drafthouse, tonight’s a great time to do it. Tonight’s flick is Mr. Scarface, an Italian crime movie.

From the promoter:

Jack Palance plays a bad, bad man who runs a neighborhood crime syndicate in Rome. But when a series of small outrages escalates to an all-out war he proves no match for a couple of slacker kids (Harry Baer and Al Cliver) with a dune buggy and an over-the-hill consigliere (the great Vittorio Caprioli) who still remembers the old days and the old ways. While not a comedy as such, this film contains more laugh out loud scenes than all but the very best comedies. But it also delivers shocking, intense violence and a pungent ambience redolent of sleazy pool halls and greasy hair tonic.

11:55pm | FREE

Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
320 E 6th St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Architecture in Helsinki at Emo’s | 10pm
  • Austin Poetry Slam and Open Mic Night at Ego’s | 7:30pm

Downtown Art Night

  • Austin Museum of Art (823 Congress Ave)
  • Arthouse (700 Congress Ave)
  • Fielding Lecht (708 Congress)
  • Else Madsen (411 Brazos, #99)
  • Mexic-Arte (419 Congress Ave)
  • Studio 107 (200 block of E 5th #107)
  • Volitant (320 Congress Ave)
  • 4 walls fine art (100 block of E 5th)

6–9pm | Free

Downtown Art Night
Downtown • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • Thursday Night Awesome at Coldtowne Theater | 10pm
  • Free Music Happy Hour at Ruta Maya | 6:30pm

Saturday | November 17

photo / roxannejomitchell 

Barack Obama: Texas Countdown to Change

Presidential hopeful Barack Obama sure loves Austin – this Saturday is the second time in a year that he’s been here for his campaign, Countdown to Change. (Read our story about his last visit here.) This visit will be somewhat more low-key – instead of speaking at Auditorium Shores, Obama will be speaking at The Backyard. Get your tickets here.

4:30pm | $25

The Backyard
3101 Highway 71 West • Austin, TX 78738

Other options:

  • Aesop Rock, Rob Sonic, Blockhead, Black Moth Super Rainbow at Emo’s | 10pm
