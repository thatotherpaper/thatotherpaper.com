Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Feb 18 - 24

18 February 2008 | That Other Paper

Monday | February 18

Audio Inversions presents Brian Sacawa

This concert series features contemporary avant-garde composers. At this particular concert Brian Sacawa will be performing pieces written by the Audio Inversions resident composers. Sacawa is critically acclaimed for his saxophone playing (he’s won a Grammy) – he plays internationally as a recitalist, chamber musician, and soloist. To see him performing pieces composed by contemporary composers is as fresh as it gets.

8pm | $10 general admission; $7 for students

Austin Museum of Art
23 Congress Ave #100 • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Bob Beardon Book Signing at BookPeople | 7pm
  • Thelonious Monk: Straight No Chaser at Alamo Ritz | 10pm

Tuesday | February 19

Old Time Music Collective

From the promoter:

Have you ever wished you and your friends could just sit around and play tunes with each other? I’m not talking about electric jams or drum circle jam bands but old traditional tunes that livened up many a kitchen and house dance. Well now is your chance to come start the journey on a road that is longer than all of our lifetimes combined! Get together with other folks of ALL skill levels to learn and play old standards of Irish Folk and Ole Timey music. We are all students and all teachers. Tim, Michael, Beth, Spot, John, Elizabeth, Jerry, Damon, Lisa, Westen, Randall, Donya etc…are the seed planters. Let’s hope this grows into a strong collective. Please leave your egos, congas, and dijimbes at the door!

8pm | FREE

Rio Rita
1308 E 6th St • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • 90s Night at Elysium | 8pm
  • Hilary York at Scoot Inn | 8pm

Wednesday | February 20

photo / Kristin Hillery Lucas Molandes 

Farewell, Lucas Molandes (he’ll be back)

Local stand-up comedian Lucas Molandes is headed up to New York to shoot his television debut for Comedy Central’s Live at Gotham; he’ll then embark on his first national tour. Before his splits for the Big Apple, however, Molandes is going to get together with other local favorites – Matt Bearden, Brendon Walsh, Lisa Delarios, Kerri Lendo, Seth Cockfield, and Bryan Gutmann – for a farewell show.

9:30pm | FREE $donations accepted

ColdTowne Theatre
803-B Airport Blvd (next to I Luv Video) • Austin, TX 78751

Other options:

  • Total lunar eclipse | 7pm
  • American songwriter tour: Jason Eady at Momo’s | 7:30pm

Thursday | February 21

Hangin’ with Mr. The P! Company

Local comedy troupe The P! Company bills themselves as “Sketch Comedy for Nerdz,” and that description is right on. Their shows are a combination of improv comedy and video sketches parodying everything from Battlestar Galactica and Harry Potter to The Forbidden Zone. So if you’re a nerd, or if you just want to pretend you’re one for an hour, head on out to ColdTowne Theater for some funnies. Read more about The P! Company show here.

11:39pm | $5

ColdTowne Theater
4803-B Airport Blvd. (next to I Luv Video) • Austin, TX 78751

Other options:

  • North Mississippi Allstars w/ Alvin Youngblood Hart at Antone’s | 8pm
  • Romeo and Juliet at The Velvet Rut Theater | 8pm

Friday | February 22

Grand Opening: Merlin Works Institute for Improvisation

From the promoter:

Some of Austin’s most experienced and respected improvisers are joining forces to launch the next improv school in town and an ongoing revue of improvised theater. Audiences can come see top-notch comedy and compelling stories and students can study with some of our city’s best teachers. Celebrate with live improv comedy at 8pm from Get Up and Parallelogramophonograph and stick around at 10pm for a party with drinks, dancing, and drama.

8, 10pm | $10 | Tickets

Salvage Vanguard Theatre
2803 Manor Rd • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Punchline at ColdTowne Theater | 10pm
  • Roy Wood Jr at Cap City | 8, 10:30pm

Saturday | February 23

Black Lips, White Denim, Strange Boys

The Black Lips are an interesting band – self-described as “Seriously slurred swashbucklers living out the ultimate teenage dream, yet breaking through another layer of remarkable idiocy, transcribed through hallucinations, exasperation, and perfectly casual dick fumbling.” Their sense of humor is displayed during their live performances, which often incorporate vomit, urine, nudity, making out, and live chickens. Last year they were the hardest working band at SXSW, playing over 12 shows in three days.

10pm

Emo’s
603 Red River • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Art Night Austin 08: A night in the galleries at All around town | After Dark??pm
  • Independent Spirit Awards Viewing Part at Austin Studios Screening Room | 4–7pm

Sunday | February 24

Overwhelming Underdogs

Overwhelming Underdogs 

One man, seven characters, and material drawn from a former life in Los Angeles form the play Overwhelming Underdogs by five-time B. Iden Payne award-nominated actor Joe Hartman.

From the Arts on Real website:

Watch in delight as an inappropriate kids’- show host, a neurotic meditation guide, a struggling 1950s night club chanteuse, and four other unlikely heroes come together to challenge the definition of what it means to be a winner.

Buy your tickets in advance here.

5:30pm | $15

Arts on Real
2826 Real St • Austin, TX 78722

Other options:

  • Curt Finch, author of All Your Money Won’t Another Minute Buy at BookPeople | 2pm
  • Fourth Annual Capital Confectioners Cake and Sugar Art Show and Competition at Texas Star Meeting and Event Center | 9am–4 pm
