Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: January 31 - February 6

31 January 2007 | That Other Paper

Wednesday | January 31

Campfire Songs

photo / courtesy Irie Bean Campfire Songs at Irie Bean 

Looks like tonight’s temperature will be in the 40s – sounds like perfect campfire weather to us. But instead of going to all that trouble of pitching a tent (tee hee), packing a cooler, and shitting in a hole, why not go to Irie Bean Coffee Bar’s delightfully fantastic patio to sip a latte, listen to live music, and warm up by the fire? Good news for your four-legged friends, too: Pets are welcome on the patio.

8pm | FREE for customers

Irie Bean Coffee Bar
2310 S Lamar • 326-4636

Thursday | February 1

photo / Kirk R. Tuck 

Present Laughter

Tonight at Zach Scott, there’s a special performance of Noel Coward’s semi-autobiographical and fully-hilarious play Present Laughter. Come early for the pre-show mixer, and be sure to enjoy the faaabulous cabaret performance during intermission.

7:30pm | $28

Zach Scott Theater
1510 Toomey Rd • 476-0541

Friday | February 2

IkkiCon

photo / little computer eerk 

Oh snap, it’s an anime convention all up in The Woodward Hotel and Conference Center. Some events planned for the weekend include a pool party, karaoke, 24-hour gaming, how-to demonstrations, and (what we’re most intrigued by) Anime Idol. Regardless of whether you’re into anime, IkkiCon should be a pretty interesting… experience.

IkkiCon official site

All day | $15 for a day pass

The Woodward Hotel and Conference Center
3401 S IH-35 • Austin, TX 78741

Saturday | February 3

Happy Birthday, Bob Marley!

photo / Chad Johnson 

Every year, Flamingo Cantina goes all out to celebrate Bob Marley’s birthday. This year’s bash features Shantytown Underground, a 10-piece reggae band that’ll be performing both classic reggae tunes as well as songs from throughout Marley’s career. The Mau Mau Chaplains will also be on hand to play Marley covers, and word has it that there will be some Caribbean snacks available.

8:30pm

512-Go!» Flamingo Cantina
515 E Sixth St • 494-9336

Sunday | February 4

photo / courtesy Getalong Gang 

Dolly Would

The Getalong Gang knows how to do it. And by “it,” we mean entertaining, funny theater. This afternoon, the Gang’s at FronteraFest Long Fringe with Dolly Would, a new show that will answer that question you’ve always been wondering: What would Dolly do?

The Getalong Gang
4pm | $10

The Blue Theatre
916 Springdale Rd • 479-7529

Monday | February 5

photo / courtesy Sex Workers’ Art Show C. Snatch Z 

Sex Workers’ Art Show

What do a stripper historian, a smut purveyor, and an award-winning author have in common? They’re all coming to Austin this Monday and Tuesday! And these women aren’t characters – these are the real women behind Sex Workers’ Art Show, a nationally touring cabaret show that breaks down the myths behind working in the sex industry. Expect tassels, expect truth – but don’t expect to sleep with these ladies just because you paid to see their show.

Sex Workers’ Art Show
7:30pm | $24

Arts on Real
2826 Real St • 472-2787

Tuesday | February 6

photo / Andy Todd 

Air Guitar Championships

Were you born to be a guitar god – only to tragically inherit the finger dexterity of your 90-year-old arthritic granny? Then here’s the chance to live out your dream, despite your complete lack of talent. The Alamo Drafthouse hosts this city-wide air guitar contest – no playing of actual instruments involved – so throw on your leather pants, flex those fingers, and prepare to show all of Austin just how rock star you really are, baby.

9:45pm | $7 | Buy tickets

512-Go!» Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
409 Colorado St. • 476-1320
