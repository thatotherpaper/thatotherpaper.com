Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Jan 14 - 20

14 January 2008 | That Other Paper

Monday | January 14

Love and a 45 at Rio Rita

From the Rita’s mouth: “It’s a record player party and you’re the DJ! Bring in three of your coolest 45’s and rule the dancefloor. Sign-up opens at 8pm. Music starts at 9pm. Dare to impress us.” And, folks, that means come hip. Hope you’ve still got that obscure Afrobeat collection you found in a bargain bin in Dusseldorf.

8pm | FREE

Rio Rita
1308 E. Sixth St. • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Rock ’n’ Roll Karaoke at Beerland | 9pm
  • David Ohlerking at Flipnotics Coffeespace | 10pm

Tuesday | January 15

Dale’s Swing Thing

Local music legend Dale Watson, the founder of the Ameripolitan music movement, is playing at the Belmont tonight with his Lone Stars. Watson is the subject of the documentary Crazy Again, directed by Zalman King, which chronicles his battle with grief when his girlfriend died. Watson will also be in King’s next movie, Austin Angel, due out soon. Check out his music on his MySpace page and his official website, dalewatson.com.

7pm | Free

The Belmont
305 W. Sixth St. • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Roy Lichtenstein Prints at Austin Museum of Art | 10am–6pm
  • As I Lay Dying with August Burns Red, Evergreen Terrace, and Still Remains at La Zona Rosa | 7:30pm

Wednesday | January 16

Jason Bateman’s Birthday Party

Celebrate Jason Bateman’s special day with a midnight screening of 80s classic Teen Wolf Too.

11:59pm | $8.25

Alamo Downtown
320 E 6th St • Austin, TX 78701

Thursday | January 17

Installation 4: It’s a Beautiful World 

Installation 4: It’s a Beautiful World

Artists Andrew Pommier, Blaine Fontana, Michael Sieben, and Tim McCormick will be in attendance tonight for the opening party of Installation 4: It’s a Beautiful World. Each of the artists interpreted the theme of “It’s a beautiful world” in their own way – and they’ve expressed that interpretation using painting, photography, sculpture, and collage. This is the fourth year of this art tour, and music will be provided by Austin’s own DJ Mel.

6–10pm | FREE

Gallery Lombardi
602 W 7th St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Moustache Party at Beauty Bar | 10pm
  • 90s Grunge Take on Romeo and Juliet at The Velvet Rut Theatre | 8pm

Friday | January 18

DJ Shadow and Cut Chemist

It’s DJ Shadow spinning with Cut Chemist – need I say more? They’ll be spinning 45s all night on 8 turntables with two guitar loop pedals. Crazy complexity!

9pm | $30

La Zona Rosa
612 W. 4th Street • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Austin Boat Show at Austin Convention Center | 12pm
  • MEGON FEST! M.O.T.O., Manikin, The Young at Beerland

Saturday | January 19

It’s Gonna Be Everything

This art opening will feature collaborative works by Sterling Allen, Tim Brown, Peat Duggins, Justin Goldwater, Nathan Green, Ryan Hennessee, Josh Rios, Michael Sieben, and Corkey Sinks – the founders of Okay Mountain.

6–9pm | FREE

Creative Research Laboratory
2832 East Martin Luther King • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Carnaval Brasileiro Poster Exhibit at South Austin Museum of Popular Culture | 7pm
  • MC Overlord at The Dirty Dog | 9pm

Sunday | January 20

courtesy of Big Sam’s Funky Nation 

Big Sam’s Funky Nation at Continental Club

You may have seen them at ACL, or at least read about them here. If you didn’t see them then, now’s your chance to catch Big Sam’s Funky Nation, the brassiest, funkiest band you’ll see all year. Sam said to us, “With New Orleans as our home base, we’ll be bringing our heavy dose of funk across the country. Get ready to dance!”

12am | $10

Continental Club
1315 S Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Dead Confederate at Mohawk | 10pm
  • Waterloo Video presents Moonlight Cinema at The Belmont | 8pm
