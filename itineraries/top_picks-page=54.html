Tuesday | July 24

I always gagged on the silver spoon. 

Citizen Kane at the Paramount

At only 25 years old Orson Wells transformed American Cinema with his film Citizen Kane, based on the life of newspaper mogul William Randolph Hearst. This is generally acknowledged as one of the best movies of all time. So we should all see it once on a big screen before we go. You’ll also get to see The Shadow: Chapter 13.

9:20pm | $5–$7

Paramount Theatre
713 Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78701

FAM Fest 2007

FAM = Film, art, and music. FAM Fest is all about artists of all kinds getting together, sharing work, and having a good time. Oh, and they also like to do good: This year’s FAM Fest will benefit the Big Brothers and Big Sisters of Central Texas. Friday will even feature a full evening of short films from around the world. On Friday and Saturday there will be an art gallery, and on Saturday bands will hit the stage.

Check famaustin.com for a complete listing of events.

Friday, 9:30pm–1am and Saturday, 1pm–??? | $15 for the Film Festival on Friday | $20 for both days

The Pump Project Artists Complex
702 Shady Lane • 78702

Saturday | July 28

The Last Days of Judas Iscariot

From the promoter:
What do you get when you put on trial the most controversial “betrayer” in history? Mix in his sexy defense attorney, a womanizing prosecutor, and an ill-tempered judge – then add a motley crew of witnesses including a not so saintly Mother Theresa, an alcoholic Pontius Pilate, and a silver-tongued Satan? The Last Days of Judas Iscariot – an imagined, time-bending, politically incorrect court case between “God and the Kingdom of Heaven vs. Judas Iscariot.” Playwright Stephen Adly Guirgis deftly combines chaotic farce and contemporary hip-hop street talk with “that old time religion” to ask big questions about “the ultimate betrayal,” the political and spiritual disorder of our world today, and the universal nature of forgiveness. Is our future created by the choices we make, or are we bound by a predestine result? This wonderful ensemble cast includes Beth Burroughs, Samantha Brewer, Stacey Glazer, Steve Campos, Trent Tinnin, Andrew Cowen, Jesse Alba, Wray Crawford, and features Travis Tinnin, Aaron Weisinger, Gina Houston, Michelle Keffer, and Charles Stites.

8pm | $15 ($10 for students)

The City Theatre
3823 Airport Blvd, Suite D • 524-2870

Sunday | July 29

photo / weblogsky 

Blue Lapis Light: Aerial Workshop

Months before the old Intel Shell was finally destroyed, Blue Lapis Light dancers could be seen twirling and jumping around the concrete mess – frightened passersby even called 911, thinking they were about to jump to their deaths. But what they were doing was rehearsing for “Requiem,” a show at which the dancers turned the hideous abandoned building into something beautiful. Daredevil dancers were connected to the concrete by bungee cord and harnesses; dramatic lighting and music left audiences in awe. Now Blue Lapis Light has a new studio in South Austin, and they’re offering an aerial dance workshop for those intrigued by this kind of dance – no experience necessary.

Visit bluelapislight.org for more info.

Noon–4pm | $125 (includes lunch)

Blue Lapis Light Studio
6701 Thomas Springs Rd, Ste A • 736-9700
