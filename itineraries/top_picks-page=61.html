Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: June 4–10

3 June 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | June 4

courtesy Paramount Theatre The Big Sleep 

Paramount Theatre Summer Classics: The Big Sleep

In this sexy noir classic, Bogart plays private eye Philip Marlow. Lauren Bacall struts it hard as the femme fatale. This version of the movie is not the same edit as the 1946 version that became so popular. Rather, this is an extended version of the movie that had been shown to troops abroad in 1945. The movie makes much more sense in the extended version – it’s a rare opportunity to see this on the big screen.

7:15pm | $7/$5 students & seniors

Paramount Theatre
713 Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78701

Tuesday | June 5

photo / Anima Free food alert! 

Hunger Awareness Day: Free burritos at Chipotle

Today is Hunger Awareness Day, so all Austin locations of Chipotle are giving out one free burrito, salad, taco, etc. if you bring in one nonperishable food item. What a steal!

All day | FREE

Austin-area Chipotles

Wednesday | June 6

Austin Poetry Slam and Open Mike Night

Everyone is welcome to take the stage at the Austin Poetry Slam and Open Mike Night, whether you’re planning to perform stand-up comedy, spoken word, or a monologue. Three performers will go home with a cash prize.

Austin Poetry Slam

7:30pm | $3

Ego’s
510 S Congress Ave • 78704

Thursday | June 7

 

First Thursdaze: Save Our Springs Benefit

Every first Thursday at Ruta Maya is First Thursdaze, a show sponsored by the folks at Austin Daze. Tonight’s show will benefit the Save Our Springs Alliance, so be sure to donate anything you can, even if it means just buying a beer or a couple of empanadas. Plenty of entertainment will be on hand, including a puppet show and live music.

9:30pm | NO COVER

512-Go!» Ruta Maya
3601 South Congress • 707-9637

Friday | June 8

Portraits

First they did Romeo and Juliet, then they did Kitty Kitty, and now Mother of Invention Productions is back with Portraits. This show is actually seven plays by seven local artists – all were written and produced by these talented Austinites (they will also be performing in them). At tonight’s show, see Portraits plays by Jodi Jinks, Renei Sims, and Maggie Gallant (who is also an excellent stand-up comedian).

Mother of Invention Productions

8pm | $10

The City Theatre
3823 Airport Blvd, Suite D • 512.524.2870

Saturday | June 9

courtesy Alamo Drafthouse 

Raiders of the Lost Ark: The Reenactment

The Alamo Drafthouse sums it up best: “In 1982, Eric Zala, Jayson Lamb and Chris Strompolis began filming a shot-for-shot remake of RAIDERS OF THE LOST ARK. They were twelve years old. Six years later, the film was in the can. Nineteen years later still, the Alamo Drafthouse Cinema is proud to announce RAIDERS OF THE LOST ARK: THE ADAPTATION with the filmmakers live in person, telling the amazing story of this legendary movie.”

7pm, 9:45pm | $12.50

512-Go!» Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
409 Colorado St. • 476-1320

Sunday | June 10

Janeane Garofalo and Patton Oswalt

Emo’s has been doing a bunch of comedy shows lately, and we’re lovin’ it. Tonight’s show features big-timers Patton Oswalt and Janeane Garofalo.

Patton Oswalt clip from the Comedians of Comedy Tour (NSFW)

9pm | $27–$30 | Tickets

512-Go!» Emo’s
6th & Red River • 477-3667
