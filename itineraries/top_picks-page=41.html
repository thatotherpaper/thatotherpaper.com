Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Oct 22 - 28

22 October 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | October 22

courtesy Gogol Bordello 

Gogol Bordello, Dub Trio, DJ Dubta

Gogol Bordello’s front man, Eugene Hutz, once claimed in an interview that as a gypsy one of the first things you’re taught is how to get attention on the street so that you can entertain people for money. He acquired the skill set with fervor and soon left his native Ukraine. First deejaying in Bavarian night clubs, he then moved on to the lower east side of New York City where he formed Gorgol Bordello and cut a record that has won them international fame. This show should be highly entertaining, as the group is known for their dramatic, cabaret-style stage performances.

10pm | $15

Emo’s
603 Red River • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • House of Torment at Highland Mall | 7–12am
  • An Evening with Calvin Russell at the Scoot Inn | 9pm

Tuesday | October 23

courtesy Cap City Comedy Club 

Chris Porter

You may know Chris Porter from season four of NBC’s disappointing reality show Last Comic Standing. He’s in town this week performing at Cap City Comedy Club.

From Cap City:

There’s plenty of evidence – onstage and off – to suggest that Chris Porter is indeed funny. For one thing, the Kansas City native appeared on season four of Last Comic Standing and not only held his own with such veteran laugh-getting luminaries as Gabriel Iglesias and Bil Dwyer, but Chris was one of the three last comics standing before that year’s champ Josh Blue took the crown. His other TV credits include Comedy Central’s Live Tour and Live At Gotham.

8pm | $5.50–$8.50 | Get tickets

Cap City Comedy Club
8120 Research Blvd • Austin, TX 78758

Other options:

  • Fall Out Boy at the Frank Erwin Center | 7pm
  • Hot Hot Heat at Emo’s | 7pm

Wednesday | October 24

Earl Greyhound and Ten Benson

New York rockers Earl Greyhound are putting on “an aggressive hour of rock from start to finish, reigning down on its audience with a barrage of ripping guitar solos and wild, thunderous beats.” Check out their MySpace page to listen to the songs before you go.

9pm | Free

The Mohawk
912 Red River • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Austin Farmer’s Market at Triangle Park | 4pm
  • Meet the Sculptor: David Everett at Umlauf Sculpture Garden and Museum | 6:30pm

Thursday | October 25

Ladies Night at Birds Barbershop

photo / Veronica Hansen 

Live music, vodka drinks, discounts on hair-dos, and FREE chair massages – all this is happening tonight at Birds Barbershop on Burnet. So if you’re interested, call 452-1200 to make an appointment.

6–9pm | varies

Birds Barbershop
6800 Burnet Rd. • Austin, TX 78757

Other options:

  • Dr. Dog, Apollo Sunshine, The Delta Spirit at The Parish Room | 8pm
  • Ice Bats vs The New Mexico Scorpions at Chaparral Ice | 7:35pm

Friday | October 26

courtesy Do512 

Do512’s First Anniversary Party

Our friends over at Do512, an Austin events website, are celebrating their one-year anniversary with live music, Halloween costumes, and, maybe best of all, free booze. Make sure you RSVP and wear a costume.

7:30pm–12:30am | FREE | RSVP

The Graveyard
2208 S Lamar Blvd (Behind Strut) • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • Games and Dames at The Mohawk | 9pm
  • The Fiery Furnaces at Emo’s | 10pm

Saturday | October 27

courtesy Rock the Casbah 

Rock the Casbah 28

DJ Mel spins; you rock out in your finest ’80s attire. Tonight’s Rock the Casbah is a Halloween Throwdown – costumes are encouraged. Dance, mingle, and play in the Atari 2600 Battle.

From Rock the Casbah’s MySpace page:

born in 2001. it’s the freshest 80’s jumpoff anywhere. the music is cut, scratched and blended. it’s all about a good time. it’s a fucking 80’s party. lighten up. wear flat shoes. accept no imitations.

10pm | $8

The Parish
214B E 6th St • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Ararat Spooky Supper and Show at Hot Mama’s Espresso Bar | 7pm
  • Evolution: A Grand Opening at Pump Project | 6pm

Sunday | October 28

Sinead O’Connor

This close to Halloween, you’re probably looking for something to scare the shit out of you. And you got it: Sinead O’Connor is playing tonight at UT’s Hogg Auditorium. She’ll be playing songs off her new album, Theology – her first album in seven years.

8pm | $35–$45

Hogg Auditorium
24th St and Whitis • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Fire and Brimstone Awards at the Vortex Theater | 7pm
  • Illuminations at Seaholm Power Plant | 8pm
