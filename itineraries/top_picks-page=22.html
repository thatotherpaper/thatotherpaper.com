TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: March 3 - 9

3 March 2008 | That Other Paper

Monday | March 3

Justice

While often compared to Daft Punk – simply because they create electronic house music and are from Paris – Gaspard Augé and Xavier de Rosnay are their own men. Oh, there’s one more similarity: It’s impossible not to shake your ass when they hit the speakers. There really isn’t a better way to start your week. So if you like to dance in any shape or form, this event is for you.

6pm | $25

Stubb’s Bar-B-Q
801 Red River St • Austin, TX 78701

Other option:

  • Austin Rodeo at Travis County Expo Center | 10am–10pm

Polls close at 7pm | FREE!

Wednesday | March 5

They Might Be Giants

If you’re reading this page, then chances are that you’ve had at least one formative experience listening to a They Might Be Giants album. The Johns Linnell and Flansburgh have come a long way from their quirky drum-machined ’80s geek-pop – and by a long way, I mean they’re making children’s albums. But don’t expect any guest appearances by Raffi (the show is ages 14+ only, so you might not bank on hearing any kiddie tunes, either). These guys are one of the most clever and energetic duos you’ll ever see perform on stage, and they’ll be supported by their full band for a Big Rawk Experience. If you ask nicely, they might even play you the theme song from Malcolm in the Middle.

7pm | $20 advance/$22 door

Stubb’s Bar-B-Q
801 Red River St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Poetry on the Plaza: On the Road at Harry Ransom Center | 12pm
  • Burning Hotels at Emo’s | 10pm
  • Austin Museum of Digital Art performance: Carl Stone at Ballet Austin at the Firehouse | 8pm

Bones Thugs-n-Harmony

Are you at a crossroads about what to do tonight? Go see Bones Thugs-n-Harmony. The group won a Grammy in 1997 for Best Rap Performance and now they’re bringing their harmony to your backyard. And if you’re really bored, watch the BTNH movie, I Tried, about what their lives would have been like if they hadn’t have become rappers. Maybe an Oscar is next!

Friday March 7

Jack Kerouac’s Scroll Manuscript of On The Road

The Harry Ransom Center has been displaying all their Beat effects since February 5. But Today they will put Jack Kerouac’s scroll manuscript of On The Road out on display, where it will remain until June 1. The scroll is 120 feet long – HRC will display the first 48 feet. There are no paragraph breaks and characters are all referred to by their real names. Kerouac wrote the entire thing in three weeks. Though he had kept constant journals over the seven year period of traveling that he distilled into a single novel.

In 2001 the scroll was purchased by Jim Irsay – the owner of the Indianapolis Colts – for $2.43 milion; since then it has stayed on the road, traveling all over the US.

Free tours of On the Road with the Beats are offered Tuesdays at noon and Saturdays at 2pm. Call 512-475-8086 to make arrangements for groups of 10 or more.

Tue 10am–5pm, Wed 10am–5pm, Thurs 10am–7pm, Friday 10am–5pm, Sat Noon–5pm, Sun Noon–5pm | FREE

Harry Ransom Center
21st and Guadalupe 78712 • Austin, TX 78704

Saturday | March 8

BarCamp Austin III 

BarCampAustinIII, Karaoke Apocalypse, and Soulhat reunion show

BarCampAustin is one of the largest BarCamps held worldwide every year:

BarCamp is an ad-hoc unconference born from the desire for people to share and learn in an open environment. It is an intense event with discussions, demos and interaction from attendees.

Anyone with something to contribute or with the desire to learn is welcome and invited to join.

Here’s a schedule of BarCampAustinIII sessions, discussions, and presentations. Got an idea for a session? Sign up! Everyone is welcome – no exceptions

At night, BarCampAustin becomes a party. Piss off your parents by singing punk and metal karaoke with a live band: Karaoke Apocalypse. Legendary funk group Soulhat will close the event with a rare reunion performance.

Free food, drinks, and beer will be supplied by Wahoo’s Fish Tacos, Sweet Leaf Tea, Vitamin Water, and Independence Brewery.

10am–2am | FREE | RSVP on the wiki or Upcoming.org

GSD&M idea city
828 W 6th Street • Austin, TX 78703

Other option:

  • Stool Pigeon at ColdTowne Theater | 8pm
