Monday | September 3

Labor Day happenings

Austin Triathlon

If you’re spontaneously adventurous or have been ritualistically training for the past two months, the Austin Triathlon near Auditorium Shores is a great opportunity to wake up early and run, swim and bike with a number attached to your chest. The course features a 1500 meter swim across Lady Bird Lake, a 40K bike trail through South Congress and a 10K run. Participants and spectators can enjoy Austin’s Pizza and live music along Auditorium Shores after the event.

7am | FREE to watch; $110 to race

Auditorium Shores
900 West Riverside Dr • Austin, TX 78704

Tuesday | September 4

Rehearsing the American Dream: Arthur Miller’s Theater

From The Harry Ransom Humanities Research Center website:

The American playwright Arthur Miller (1915-2005) articulated an unparalleled engagement with his historical moment through such plays as Death of a Salesman and The Crucible. He remained committed throughout his life to a politics of freedom. This engagement was never simplistic or topical, one of the reasons that his work has remained such a significant part of the staged canon. He compellingly married the emotional and psychological elements of character with concerns about public and political responsibility. The idea of theater providing the conscience for a nation—Miller’s intellect and artistry were forged by his US citizenship—pulls his disparate works together into a whole. The exhibition uses Miller’s plays to explore conscience in its theatrical expression: as an intertwined and interdependent political and emotional life.

10am–5pm | FREE

Harry Ransom Center
21st and Guadalupe • 471-8944

Wednesday | September 5

AMOA Extra-Ordinary: The Everyday Object in American Art

courtesy AMOA 

This exhibit started on August 25 and will be running until November 4. Extra-Ordinary consists of thirty photographs, prints, paintings, and sculptures that, either in form or content, toy with the idea of art as something elevated beyond that of everyday experience. Featuring art from the past four decades, this exhibit seeks to draw out the extraordinary within the quotidian. You’ll see works from Alexander Calder, Vija Celmins, Tony Feher, Robert Gober, Marisol, Claes Oldenburg, James Rosenquist, Jessica Stockholder, Andy Warhol, and others.

10am–6pm | FREE for kids under 12; $4 students and seniors; $5 adults

Thursday | September 6

Crawford, USA

courtesy Austin Film Society 

Tonight the Austin Film Society will premiere an intriguing film by producer/director David Modigliani. Crawford, USA answers the question, “What happens to the 705 residents of Crawford, Texas when George W. Bush decides to call it home?”

Thrust into the media spotlight for the past seven years, this quaint country town has been reeling from the impact of tourists, press, and political divide. Modigliani’s film covers the town newspaper’s 2004 endorsement of Kerry, the 20,000 protesters who swarmed the streets in 2005, and how individual residents’ daily lives have been forever changed.

7pm–10pm | FREE with registration

Viva Las Manitas

photo / Kristin Hillery
