Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: June 11 - 17

11 June 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | June 11

photo / CNT-Sevilla Manu Chao rocks out 

Manu Chao

Manu Chao, anti-establishment musician and the premier innovator of alternative Latin music, plays tonight at Stubb’s. A Frenchman of Spanish decent, Manu Chao currently resides in Barcelona. His musical style is a multilingual, multicultural agglomeration of rock, ska, punk, and found sounds, all with a Latin slant. His live performances are notoriously badass.

8pm | $30 SOLD OUT

Stubb’s Bar-B-Q
801 Red River • Austin, TX 78701

Tuesday | June 12

photo / notariety 

Air Sex Competition: Round 1

Air Sex is super hot right now in Japan, and it’s just hitting Austin. You know how air guitar competitions are? Well, instead of playing invisible guitars, people are going to fuck invisible people. The judges will assess costume, foreplay, and climax, keeping in mind believability, sexability, and overall entertainment value. You could be the Air Sex King/Queen of Austin. Get awkward! Yeah!

9pm | $7

512-Go!» Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
409 Colorado St. • 476-1320

Wednesday | June 13

Billy D. Washington w/ Doug Mellard

A former Houston cop, Billy D. Washington is now a hilarious stand-up comic who’s bringing his unique act to Cap City a few nights this week. Because he’s also a musician – he’s opened for Aretha Franklin – expect Billy to bust out a keyboard during the show. Doug Mellard, 2006’s Funniest Person in Austin, will open.

8pm | $9 | Tickets

512-Go!» Cap City Comedy Club
8120 Research Blvd • 512.467.2333

Thursday | June 14

Unwritten Law w/ Zebrahead

Southern California rock band Unwritten Law is joined by rapcore group Zebrahead tonight at Emo’s. Other bands scheduled to play include Bullets and Octane, Neurosonic, and Junior.

Doors at 6pm | $16 | Tickets

512-Go!» Emo’s
6th & Red River • 477-3667

Friday | June 15

We Have Separation 

We Have Separation

This weekend will be your last chance to catch this wacky, wacky hip-hop narrated play by Spencer Drigger. Roy not only has amnesia, he also has a brother lost in outer space and a sister who has been kidnapped. Oh, and Roy is stuck in his chair. The play also features a mad scientist and a roller coaster.

8pm | $10

The Blue Theater
916 Springdale Rd • 927-1118

Saturday | June 16

courtesy The Vortex 

College Freshman Year Sucks

Rob Nash has done it again: College Freshman Year Sucks, which he wrote and is performing in, has been getting rave reviews. The show is funny, smart, and Nash is brilliant. See it while you can – the show’s run at the Vortex ends on June 16.

8pm | $10–$30 (pay what you can)

The Vortex
2307 Manor Road • 478-5282

Sunday | June 17

Teen Witch w/ songwriters in person

From the Alamo Drafthouse: 80s teen comedies definitely captured the hearts and minds of America, but none of them were as lightheartedly wacko as this late-era cult classic. Louise is unpopular at school, but she learns that being the direct descendant of the original Salem witches has its advantages. What follows is a helium-infused romp through the post-pubescent black arts, bolstered by eye-popping unexpected musical sequences and a tour-de-force performance by the one and only Zelda “that short lady from POLTERGEIST” Rubinstein! The most supernaturally delicious slice of ’80s cheese, presented by the guys responsible for its zaniest moments. Magic!!! Also stars Bewitched alumnus Dick Sargeant as Dad, and the always-amazing and thoroughly irritating Joshua Miller as Louise’s little brother Richie.

9:45pm | $7–$9 | Tickets

512-Go!» Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
409 Colorado St. • 476-1320
