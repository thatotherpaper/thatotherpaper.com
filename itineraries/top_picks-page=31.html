Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Dec 31 - Jan 6

31 December 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | December 31

New Year’s Eve

It’s the last night of 2007, and just about everybody has tomorrow off, so it’s time to par-tay! Or stay in with champagne and your honey, or put the kids to bed and fall asleep watching Dick Clark and Ryan Seacrest trying to appeal to the masses… or whatever it is you kids do these days. If you’re in the mood to celebrate the new year downtown, you pretty much have endless options. Everywhere downtown is having a party, but you might want to call ahead to see if there’s a cover since places that normally don’t have a cover may charge for entry on New Year’s Eve. Here are a few we like:

  • 80s party at The Parish | Tickets $35
  • Bling in the New Year at Beauty Bar | $20 cover, $15 if you email ahead to bart@beautybar.com
  • Bob Schneider, Two Guy Trio (yeah, the same group that always drew their name in chalk on the Drag), and T-Bird and the Breaks at Antone’s | Tickets $36
  • Sock Hop at Scoot Inn | $10 cover
  • New Year’s Improv Extravaganza at The Hideout | $20

For the non-downtown inclined, First Night Austin promises to be a great time. As Austin’s annual New Year’s Eve festival, First Night Austin has events beginning at 3pm and continuing until midnight. The city has commissioned over 60 artists to create art in and on the city itself – for instance, on outdoor plazas, sidewalks, balconies, building facades, etc. The festival takes place in Downtown Austin, from Congress and Cesar Chavez to Auditorium Shores including City Hall and the Second Street District, and at 6:30pm, everyone can join in the Grand Procession down Congress Avenue. Mayor Will Wynn and Congressman Lloyd Doggett will speak at 7:45pm, and at midnight, the city will watch fireworks over Lady Bird Lake. Pretty cool. The event is alcohol-free and family-friendly, so if you’re sick of getting trashed on Sixth on New Year’s, head out to First Night Austin. Get more info at firstnightaustin.org.

Tuesday | January 1

Kathleen Edwards

She’s a little bit piano, a little bit drums, a little bit bitch, and a little bit sweet. With three albums to her name and a fourth coming out in March, she’s got the experience to back up the gritty lyrics and voice (which contrast with her sweet face). Check out her songs on her MySpace page, then go see the real thing at The Continental Club. We like her song “A Face in the Crowd.”

10pm | $7

Continental Club
1315 S Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • DJ Aryn J at Beauty Bar | 10:30pm
  • Bill Carter & Stephen Doster at Threadgill’s World Headquarters | 8pm

Wednesday | January 2

Emo’s Free Week

photo / jerone2  

Five great Austin bands kick off Emo’s Free Week. Wednesday night features: Jets Under Fire, Hundred Year Storm, Fulton Read, Golden Bear & Built By Snow. Bring a friend, or three – it’s free!

9pm | FREE

Emo’s
603 Red River; Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Jazz Pharoahs at The Elephant Room | 6pm
  • Austin Poetry Slam at Scoot Inn | 7pm

Thursday | January 3

When More Pigs Fly!

This ridiculous production at Arts on Real is a blast, and time’s running out to see it. The Austin Circle of Theaters awarded them Best Costume Design, so expect some damn good costumes. It’s a “musical revue of love and acceptance spooned in healthy heapings of feathers, rhinestones, twinkle lights and glitter!” and we can’t think of a better or more fun way to spend tonight.

8pm | $25–$33

Arts on Real
2826 Real St • Austin, TX 78722

Other options:

  • Blade Runner at The Paramount | 7:30pm
  • The Latino Comedy Project at Esther’s Follies | 8pm

Friday | January 4

B Scene at the Blanton

On the first Friday of each month, the Blanton holds a little party where you can come have some cocktails and eat hors d’oeuvres. It’s a fun event for art lovers – but beware the Blantinis, as they will knock you on your ass. While you’re there, you can check out Latin American artist Jorge Macchi’s internationally renown collection: The Anatomy of Melancholy. The title references a book of the same name by Robert Burton, written in 1612. You can read The Anatomy of Melancholy thanks to Google. The exhibit features over 40 of Macchi’s works from 1990 to present – his conceptual art is clever, thought provoking, and not without a sense of humor. In addition to great art, food, and drinks (cash bar), there will also be a scavenger hunt and complimentary massages. Woot.

6pm | $5 for members; $10 for non-members

The Blanton Museum of Art
MLK at Congress • Austin, TX 78712

Other options:

  • 6th Annual Tattoo Art Revival at Palmer Events Center | 10am
  • Latin French Fusion Lounge with DJ Grey at Bess Bistro | 9pm

Saturday | January 5

Macbeth

The Velvet Rut Theatre specializes in Shakespeare plays. Right now they’re preoccupied with one of Willie’s darkest works, Macbeth. And tonight is your last night to catch this production. Now that the holidays are over, we can return to our normal lives: omens, greed, infidelity, murder, insanity.

8pm | FREE (Donations accepted)

The Velvet Rut Theatre
1612 East 7 • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Masonic at Emo’s FREE week – no cover Emo’s | 10pm
  • Reahanna Parker Art Opening at Austin Figurative Gallery | 7pm

Sunday | January 6

Gospel Brunch

It’s the first Sunday of the new year, and there’s a good chance you’re either nursing a hangover this morning or really dreading going back to work tomorrow. Or both. But what might make your day is some awesome food – including migas, brisket, and grits – and amazing music at Stubb’s. This morning they’re doing their wildly popular Gospel Brunch. And don’t miss out on the Make Your Own Bloody Mary Bar. Call ahead to reserve a table (480-8341 ext. 306).

11am and 1pm

http://stubbsaustin.com
801 Red River • Austin, TX 78701
