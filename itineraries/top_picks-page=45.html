Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Sept 24-30

24 September 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | September 24

Nikkatsu Crime Retro: A Colt is My Passport

courtesy Alamo Drafthouse 

This retrospective series aims to expose westerners to the edgy noir action of Japanese genre films, chock-full of hitmen, gang-bosses, sidekicks, and hot babes. This is the only place you can see these films outside of Japan, as they are not distributed on DVD (or VHS, for those of you still living in the Pleistocene Epoch). Set in the streets of Tokyo, but borrowing values of American gangster movies, these films inhabit a liminal world between east and west. Mark Shilling, author of NO BORDERS, NO LIMITS: NIKKATSU ACTION CINEMA, will attend the screening to answer questions and give you some perspective on the genre.

9:45pm | $8.25

Alamo Drafthouse South
1120 South Lamar • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Savory Baking From The Mediterranean with Anissa Helou at Central Market | 6:30–9pm
  • New and Noteworthy Book Club (discussing The Looming Tower) at BookPeople | 7pm

Tuesday | September 25

Dan Deacon

Part of a seven-week tour with the Video Hippos, this show at the Mohawk tonight features Dan Deacon, a composer and performance artist from Baltimore.

9pm | $6 | Get tickets

The Mohawk
912 Red River St • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • BAM! Black Arts Movement Performing Arts Festival at George Washington Carver Museum | See website

Wednesday | September 26

James McMurtry

photo / janet_dancer 

James McMurtry, son of legendary novelist Larry McMurtry, first started playing music at the University of Arizona in the early ’80s before moving back to Texas to tend bar at a friend’s nightclub. In 1989 he gained critical acclaim for his album Too Long in the Wasteland and has kept a hold of it ever since.

10pm | $7

Continental Club
1315 S. Congress • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Magic Jewels and Megazilla The Parlor | 10pm
  • New Disaster, The Last Vegas, Blackholicus Emo’s | 10pm

Thursday | September 27

 

Austin Art Garage Opening Party

From the promoter:

Ann Kelso Salon and City Spa is hosting an art party with an exclusive selection of art works from emerging local artists Dan Grissom, Joel Ganucheau, Tim Raines, Luis Abreux, Rachelle Diaz, Julie Isaacson, John Kachmar, Forrest Elliot, Annie Bradley, Gabriela LeBaron, and more. Food provided by Carmelo’s Italian Restaurant, and beverages by Cissi’s Market and Sweet Leaf Tea. The first 25 attendees to arrive will receive complimentary petite spa experiences from Ann Kelso Salon as well.

7pm | FREE | Email art@austinartgarage.com to RSVP

Ann Kelso Salon and City Spa
1400 S Congress Ave • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • The Black Irish, Flatfoot 56, Saint Street Brawlers, Capital Scam at Red 7 | 8pm

Friday | September 28

courtesy Chris Trew 

One AM

Chris Trew and Tami Nelson, both members of improv troupe ColdTowne, formed a side project titled One AM over a year ago. The duo has sold out shows at Frontera Fest and are espoused by many as their favorite show ever. Come ready to experience awkward situations that offer brief moments of relief.

8pm | $7–$10

ColdTowne Theater
803 Airport Blvd (behind I Luv Video) • Austin, TX 78751

Other options:

  • Krum Bums, Monster Squad, SS Kaliert (Germany), Unit 21, Career Criminal at Emo’s | 10pm
  • Dale Watson, Walk Wilkins at the Continental Club | 8pm

Saturday | September 29

Texas Shootout 

Texas Shootout: Women’s Flat Track Derby Association National Championships

Just when you thought it couldn’t get more intense, Women’s Flat Track Derby Association National Championships weekend came around. The roller derby girls are out for blood – there are four games today as well as autograph signings, live music, and after parties. If you can’t get enough, take a look at the schedule and see what’s going on all weekend.

All day: view the schedule | $28 | Get tickets

Austin Convention Center
500 E Cesar Chavez St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Do512.com Presents at The Parish | 9pm
  • Austin Farmer’s Market at Republic Square Park | 9am

Sunday | September 30

Austin Yam Jam

photo / joni Ruthie Foster 

From AustinYamJam.com:

The Austin Yam Jam is an annual music festival that brings some of Austin’s best musicians together with Operation Turkey – a non-profit that feeds and clothes the homeless – to raise awareness, volunteers and donations. 100% of each admission goes directly toward feeding and clothing the homeless.

  • Ruthie Foster
  • Bruce Robison
  • porterdavis
  • Carolyn Wonderland
  • Patrice Pike

2–8pm | $10-$15; children under 12 free

Threadgill’s
301 W Riverside Dr.; Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Miss Lauren Marie at Flipnotics | 8pm
  • Riley Osbourn’s Fundraising Extravaganza at Antone’s | 4pm
