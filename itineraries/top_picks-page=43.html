Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Oct 8 - 14

8 October 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | October 8

$1 night at Dart Bowl

photo / smason 

Every Monday from 9pm to midnight, Dart bowl provides the cheapest bowling experience in town. Each game is $1 per person, shoe rental is $1 for the whole night, and even nachos, hot dogs, and sodas are only $1. The money you save here can be spend towards bowling pin shaped beer bottles and Lebowski-inspired white Russians. Be sure to show up at least 45 minutes early to reserve a lane.

9pm to midnight | $1 per person, per game

Dart Bowl
5700 Grover Ave • Austin, TX 78756

Other options:

  • Slaraffenland, Benko, Silver Pines at The Mohawk | 9pm
  • Sunny Sweeny, Mark Jungers and The Mules at The Continental Club | 10pm

Tuesday | October 9

Doug Benson

photo / Kristin Hillery Oh, hello. 

Stand-up comic, documentary filmmaker, writer, TV personality, and stoner Doug Benson performs stand-up comedy tonight at Cap City. You may know Doug from VH1 or from the Marijuana-Logues, a show that’s been a big hit in both New York and LA, where people also smoke pot. And Austin should be no different, except we smoke better pot than the people in those other cities. If you’re interested in gaining more insight into the world of Doug Benson, check out our interview with him. Local comedian and Funniest Person in Austin finalist Lisa Delarios will open for Doug.

8pm (with shows at 8pm and 10:30 Fri and Sat |Tues: $5.50; Wed/Thurs: $9; Fri/Sat: $13

Cap City Comedy Club
8120 Research Blvd • Austin, TX 78758

Other options:

  • The Loneliness of the Long Distance Runner at Alamo Drafthouse South | 7pm
  • The Godfather II at The Off Center | 7pm

Wednesday | October 10

Weird Wednesday: The Baron

Here’s what the folks at Alamo Drafthouse have cooked up for tonight’s Weird Wednesday:

The late Calvin Lockhart plays the Baron, a struggling film-maker trying to get his movie made in this very strange blaxploitation obscurity. His financier, a ghetto drug pusher named Cokeman pulls out his funding under pressure from the supernaturally scuzzy mob enforcer Richard Lynch, who wants his money back NOW. So the Baron is forced to get his hustle on with a quickness if he’s going to get his movie into theaters. Not surprisingly he does what most of us would do in that situation - he peddles his body to wealthy women (including Joan Blondell and Marlene Clark). Hey, we’ve all done it. The movie within the movie, also called THE BARON and starring Lockhart as an ermine-clad race car driver named Baron Von Tripp looks pretty awesome. With a booming original soundtrack by Gil-Scot Heron and Brian Jackson that will knock your dick in the dirt!

11:55pm | FREE

Alamo Drafthouse South Lamar
1120 South Lamar • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Great Lake Swimmers at Stubb’s | 8pm
  • Rallying the Wolves Pt. 1 at The Off Center | 7pm

Thursday | October 11

photo / snenad 

The City Theatre presents Death of a Salesman

Tonight is the opening night of Arthur Miller’s Death of a Salesman at The City Theatre. Willie Loman has put all his faith in the American dream and yet he has nothing to show for it other than his own self-deprecation, the unrelenting pressure he foists on his sons who wish to have their own lives, and his wife who’s miserably caught in the middle. Death of a Salesman is a play that becomes more relevant with time, as consumption and materialism spiral increasingly out of control and the rift between expectations and reality deepens. The play will run Thurs–Sat at 8pm and Sunday at 5:30pm until November 4.

8pm | $15 - 20

The City Theatre
3823 Airport Suite D • Austin, TX 78722

Other options:

  • Haunting Oboe Music at The Mohawk | 10pm

Friday | October 12

Darjeeling Limited Feast

Chow down and enjoy a fine Indian feast while you watch Wes Anderson’s latest comedy The Darjeeling Limited, starring Owen Wilson, Jason Schwartzman, and Adrian Brody. Entertainment Weekly says this about Anderson’s latest: “There’s a startling new maturity in Darjeeling, a compassion for the larger world that busts the confines of the filmmaker’s miniaturist instincts.” See the menu for the feast here.

7:30pm | $60

Alamo Drafthouse South Lamar
1120 S Lamar • 476-1320

Other options:

  • The Scabs at La Zona Rosa | 9pm
  • Texas Burlesque Festival at The Parish | 9pm

Saturday | October 13

Texas Burlesque Festival

courtesy Kitty Kitty Bang Bang 

This is day two of the Texas Burlesque Festival, a “hoedown and showdown celebrating the raucous revival of burlesque, vaudeville and the accompanying et cetera.” Mary J and Whatamelon and Doryan and Adam are your MCs tonight at Antone’s – performing are male solo acts, female solo acts, ensembles, and vaudeville artists, including local fave Schave and Reilly.

From the promoter:

Over 50 performers and 9 troupes from around the west and beyond are heading to the capital city, Austin, Texas, to bring you two rowdy evenings with more wall-to-wall wigglin’, jigglin’, and gigglin’ burlesque action than Texas has ever seen!

9pm | $15–$25 | Get tickets

Antone’s
213 W Fifth St • 320-8424

Other options:

  • Sounds Under Radio at Stubb’s | 9pm
  • Buddy Miles’ All-Star Blues Jam at La Zona Rosa | 9pm

Sunday | October 14

Sock Hop Sunday

courtesy Dakota State University Archives 

Every second Sunday of the month, a group of lively people tear up the dance floor like it’s sometime between 1950 and 1983. This time the beloved Sock Hop will be held at Scoot Inn. As always, however, retro costumes are encouraged. Expect a jukebox, pool table, and music by DJ DR.

9pm | Cheap

The Scoot Inn
1308 E. Fourth • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Choose Austin First Fest at The Triangle | 1-6 pm
  • Gospel Brunch at Stubb’s | 11am
