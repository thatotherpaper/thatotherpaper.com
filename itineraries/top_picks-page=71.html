Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: March 26 - April 1

26 March 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | March 26

Michael Mordecai’s Jazz Jam

Whether you’re looking for a hot place to take a date or just a laid back venue to relax at after work, Michael Mordecai’s Jazz Jam at the Elephant Room is a good bet.

6pm | NO COVER

512-Go!» The Elephant Room
315 Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78701

Tuesday | March 27

photo / Daquella Manera 

Robert De Niro in Costume: Selections from the Archive

The Harry Ransom Center acquired several memorable Robert De Niro costumes last fall. They are now on display on the first floor of the University Co-op until April 1. See if you can keep track of how many people at the exhibition say, “You talkin’ to me?” We lost count at 500 jillion.

More information

8am–7:30pm | FREE

University Co-op
2246 Guadalupe St • Austin, TX 78705

Wednesday | March 28

Grindhouse premiere at The Paramount

From Grindhousemovie.net: Planet Terror plus Death Proof together in one explosive show! Appearances by Grindhouse directors Quentin Tarantino and Robert Rodriguez, plus special guests.

7pm | $100 VIP; $25-$35 general admission

Paramount Theatre
713 Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78701

Thursday | March 29

White and Nerdy: The Weird Al Singalong

From the Alamo Drafthouse: We’ll be showing classic Weird Al videos like: Eat It, Like a Surgeon, Amish Paradise, and White and Nerdy, along with favorite moments from UHF and the short-lived The Weird Al Show. If you’ve only heard Weird Al and haven’t seen his videos, then this night is a must-see. Be ready to perform your own original parody song for big prizes!

7pm; 9:45pm | $10 | Tickets

512-Go!» Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
409 Colorado St. • 476-1320

Friday | March 30

photo courtesy Dana Gould 

Dana Gould

Dana Gould of The Simpsons and The Ben Stiller Show performs at Cap City this weekend.

Read our interview with Dana Gould

8pm and 10:30pm | $16

512-Go!» Cap City Comedy Club
8120 Research Blvd • 512.467.2333

Saturday | March 31

Peeping Tom (Mike Patton & Dan the Automator) w/ Dub Trio

From Wikipedia: Peeping Tom is an experimental collaboration album by Mike Patton. It was released May 30, 2006 on Patton’s own record label, Ipecac Recordings. The set was pieced together by swapping song files through the mail with collaborators like Norah Jones, Kool Keith, and Massive Attack.

9pm | $16

512-Go!» Emo’s (outdoor stage)
6th & Red River • 477-3667

Sunday | April 1

Sunday Fair: Texas Hill Country Wine and Food Festival

There’s nothing quite like being drunk in public on a Sunday afternoon. And if you can afford the $45 price tag to this annual culinary event, that is just what you will get. Over 100 exhibitors, including Texas wineries, breweries, and restaurants, will gather in Georgetown’s San Gabriel Park to offer samples of their delicious wares. The event also features cooking demonstrations, seminars, and live music. Needless to say, this event usually sells out, so advance tickets are recommended.

Noon–5pm | $45

San Gabriel Park
445 E Morrow St • Georgetown, TX 78626
