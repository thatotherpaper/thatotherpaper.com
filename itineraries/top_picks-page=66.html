Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: April 30 - May 6

29 April 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | April 30

The Wind That Shakes the Barley 

The Wind That Shakes the Barley

The Wind That Shakes the Barley features Batman Begins star Cillian Murphy as an Irish medical student who must join his brother (Padraic Delaney) in the fight for freedom against the British in the early 1920s. With overblown special effects movies starring spidermen, pirate men, or ogre men soon to be invading every theater in the nation, we recommend letting one last character drama engage your brain for two hours before the Hollywood sequel machine takes over.

7pm, 9:40pm

Dobie Theater
2021 Guadalupe St • 472-3456

Tuesday | May 1

Toots and the Maytals

How’d reggae get its name? How very convenient that you asked: Toots and the Maytals’ 1968 single “Do the Raggay” is why the genre is called reggae. Interesting. See this legendary band play tonight at La Zona Rosa.

8pm| $26 | Tickets

La Zona Rosa
612 W Fourth St • Austin, TX 78701

Wednesday | May 2

Blonde Redhead

From Stubb’s:
Blonde Redhead’s noisy, dissonant guitars, alternate tunings, and quiet, stilted lyrics have often been compared to early Sonic Youth.

Doors at 7pm | $17–$20

Stubb’s Bar-B-Q
801 Red River • Austin, TX 78701

Thursday | May 3

photo / fortes 

B-Boy City Art Show

Tonight’s event at Gallery Lombardi will kick off the 14th annual B-Boy City Convention, which is happening all weekend. Featuring graffiti by Nathan Nordstrom and photographs of b-boys and past B-Boy City Conventions, the art show will also have DJs on hand.

7pm | FREE

Gallery Lombardi
602 W Seventh St Suite A • Austin, TX 78701

Friday | May 4

photo / Kristin Hillery Eric Clymer 

Punchline

Every Friday, That Other Paper is proud to present Punchline at ColdTowne Theater. This stand-up comedy open mic features 12–15 local comics who each get four minutes onstage. With only about 30 seats available, you’d better get there early. Tonight’s show will be hosted by local comedian Jimi Tolliver.

Punchline

10pm | $5 | BYOB

512-Go!» ColdTowne Theater
48 1/2 St and Airport Blvd • Behind I Luv Video

Saturday | May 5

Cinco de Mayo Music Festival

Celebrate Cinco de Mayo at the Austin Cinco de Mayo Music Festival. There will be a carnival, music, food, and various contests like a jalapeno-eating contest. It’s located in east Austin at the Fiesta Gardens. The best part is that this event benefits the Greater East Austin Youth League. Charity, carnival rides, and carnival food. What more do you need?

More info

Noon–midnight | $5; $8 after 8pm

Fiesta Gardens
1901 Bergman Ave • Austin, TX 78702

Sunday | May 6

photo / Matt Hislope 

A Thought in Three Parts

From the promoter:
Rubber Repertory is proud to present the U.S. premiere of Wallace Shawn’s A Thought in Three Parts, a trio of plays exploring the lonely underside of sex, love and all that falls between. As the evening progresses from a hotel room in a foreign country to a dimly lit youth hostel to the breakfast nook of a man named Mr. Frivolous, questions of coupling are unraveled. Where do loving and loathing intersect? Will personalities ever mix as conveniently as anatomies? If everyone’s so tired, why can’t anyone sleep? Alternately elegant and excessive, unsettling and wildly funny, A Thought in Three Parts has been called “a tour de force of gratification denied.”

7pm | Pick your price: $10–$30 | Tickets

The Vortex
2307 Manor Road • 478-5282
