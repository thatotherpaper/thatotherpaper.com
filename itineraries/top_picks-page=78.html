Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Feb 7-13

7 February 2007 | That Other Paper

Wednesday | February 7

Introduction to Chocolate

photo / Mayra Chiachia My diet starts tomorrow LOL 

Calling all chocolate lovers: Terry Wakefield, the chief chocolatier of Bissenger Chocolate, heads this informative yet oh-so-tasty class on the fine art of candy-making. You’ll learn Wakefield’s tricks of the trade, then try your own hand at making traditional chocolate truffles. Who knows? You might even end up with a homemade Valentine’s Day gift for that someone special… if you don’t eat all the candy first.

6:30pm | $35

Culinary Center at Whole Foods downtown
525 N Lamar Blvd • Austin, TX 78703

Thursday | February 8

photo / courtesy Matt Bearden How dreamy! 

GameShow!

What would happen if The Daily Show and The Price is Right got together and made sweet, sweet love? A convenient segue into the explanation of local comic Matt Bearden’s live-action game show (which That Other Paper happens to be sponsoring), that’s what! GameShow! is back – this time at the upstairs showroom of Buffalo Billiards – with host E. Murray McKinley and an audience eager to play such classic games as “Grow a Beard on the Emo Kid” and “Lick My Pie, Eat My Slice” to win prizes and booze.

10pm | $5

Buffalo Billiards
201 E 6th St • Austin, TX 78701

Friday | February 9

Hoover’s Blanket: Rivals

photo courtesy of Hoover’s Blanket 

Born from the mind of sketch comedy writer Chris Allen and directed by Jeremy Lamb, Rivals promises to be twisted, hilarious, and a little confusing. A full-hour show is a first for Hoover’s Blanket, and this is the second week into their month-long run at ColdTowne Theater. Check out the teaser.

Hoover’s Blanket

8pm | $7

512-Go!» ColdTowne Theater
4803 Airport Blvd • 524-2807

Saturday | February 10

Big Star Burlesque

From top left: Blue Valentine (Director), Ruby Darling, Krakatoa Katie, Anna Maejnuna 

We’ve been excited about this show for months. The ladies of Big Star Burlesque, one of only two plus-size burlesque troupes in the nation, will no doubt be rockin’ the pasties full force tonight at The Hideout – it’s opening night. With fabulous ladies like Ruby Darling, Krakatoa Katie, Blue Valentine, and Anna Maejnuna – not to mention vaudeville act Schave and Reilly, charming host Gil Sinatra, and a live band – why would you miss this?

Big Star Burlesque story in That Other Paper

8pm | $12 | Tickets

512-Go!» The Hideout Theatre
617 Congress Ave • 443-3688

Sunday | February 11

photo / courtesy Les McGehee 

Plays Well With Others

Les McGehee, author of the improvisational comedy book Plays Well with Others, has teamed up with Owen Egerton (formally of The Sinus Show) to bring his book to the Zach Scott stage. Just make sure you bring 15 bucks, or else you won’t be paying with the others.

2:30pm | $15

Zach Scott Theater
1510 Toomey Rd • 476-0541

Monday | February 12

Beethoven, meet Roland 

Eclectacoustic

Much more than your everyday piano recital, the Audio Inversions know how to rock out classically. This is their second year of electric compositions, and this Monday you’re invited to come downtown to the Austin Museum of Art (which will remain open during the show) to see these punks bite the proverbial head off the classical dove.

Audio Inversions
8pm | $10

512-Go!» AMoA
823 Congress Ave • 495-9224

Tuesday | February 13

photo / Eva Powell 

The Hardest (Working) Man in Showbiz

You’d feel like a giant dick if you missed this one. Ron Jeremy will be discussing his new book The Hardest (Working) Man in Showbiz. That’s right: The star of nearly 2,000… movies… is coming to Austin to get everyone in the mood for V-Day.

7pm | FREE

BookPeople
603 N Lamar Blvd • 472-5050
