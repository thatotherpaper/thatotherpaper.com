Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Jan 24-30

23 January 2007 | That Other Paper

Wednesday | January 24

photo / Marco Annunziata 

Jeff Tweedy w/ Scott McCaughey

Jeff Tweedy (lead singer of Wilco) makes a two-night stop in Austin during his solo tour. Tweedy is known to never play the same set twice, pulling from a library of over 200 songs from all of his former bands. Wilco’s sixth album, Sky Blue Sky is scheduled to be released May 15th, 2007.

8pm | $25 | Buy tickets

Hogg Memorial Auditorium
Guadalupe St & 24th • UT Campus

Thursday | January 25

Zeale 32 w/ The Word Association, Passenger, and Paradox

Zeale 32 

We just recently discovered Zeale 32, and his sound is solid. It’s awesome to discover new local talent, especially when it diverges from the overcrowded indie rock scene. Although he’s not a native to Austin, he’s earned his keep by battling at freestyle competitions along the way. Right now he’s working on his first national release under the label BabyGrande Records, due to drop in 2007. Check out a few of Zeale’s songs on his MySpace profile.

9pm | $5-7

Red 7
7th & Red River • 512.476.8100

Friday | January 26

Tagline: May the best hit man win. 

Smokin’ Aces

The weather sucks – go see a movie. Written and produced by Joe Carnahan (NARC), Smokin’ Aces is a story of desperation, pissed-off mobsters, and eccentric, blood-thirsty hit men (watch the trailer to see what we mean).

IMDB page | Trailer

Saturday | January 27

photo / Peyri Leigh 

Le Garage Sale

The “Le” means you shouldn’t come to this garage sale looking for broken lamps, headless Barbies, or skid-marked panties. Sorry! But what you will find at Le Garage Sale are some pretty sweet deals on clothing from Big Red Sun, Girl Next Door, Gomi, and Adlante, to name just a few. Heads up: They accept cash only.

legaragesale.net

Jan 26 & 27 | 11pm-6pm
$10 ($15 two-day pass)

Palmer Events Center
900 Barton Springs Rd • 404-4020

Sunday | January 28

Mojo Kickball Returns

photo / Jill Morris 

After nearly a two-month hiatus, Austin’s unique flavor of kickball–meets–dodgeball–meets–tag resumes play this Sunday. The weather will no doubt be cool, but so far there’s no rain in sight. Still haven’t heard of Mojo Kickball? Check out mojokickball.com to sign up and study the rules (though, we assure you, they won’t make any sense until you actually play).

Mojo Kickball
3pm | Free (donations welcome)
Drinks and snacks provided.

Lorraine Camacho Activity Center (The Big Field) map
34 Robert T Martinez Jr St • 512.391.1863

* * *

The Cost is Correct, Part Deux

Come on down. The Heart of Texas Tournament (Austin’s chapter of the International Gay Bowling Organization) will be hosting its version of the game show that proves who has the sharpest capitalist skills. It’s the closest chance most of us will have to the real thing without having to bask in Bob Barker’s aftershave. A $10 donation gets your name in the hat for a chance to “be the next contestant.” Don’t miss out on this opportunity to win over $1,000 in fabulous prizes. Hmmm, we’re assuming that doesn’t include a brand new car. Oh well.

For more info, email txgameshowfan [at] austin.rr.com

7pm | $10 donation

The Cockpit Club
113 San Jacinto • 350-3241

Monday | January 29

photo / Steven Kreuzer 

The Bridge

San Francisco’s Golden Gate Bridge has captured the hearts of visitors for decades. However, there is a little known fact that it is also the place where someone commits suicide by jumping off the bridge approximately every 15 days. Under controversial circumstances, director Eric Steel and his crew spent 2004 exploring what draws people to this notorious site. During their stakeout, they witnessed 19 jumps and talked to grieving family members of successful jumpers.

Showing, Jan 26 - Feb 1
Buy tickets | IMDB page

512-Go!» Alamo Drafthouse South Lamar
1120 South Lamar • 512.476.1320

Tuesday | January 30

C’mon man, where’s Okro? 

One-Man He-Man Show

Break out the Underoos and Pop Tarts – we’re going back in time to a twisted animated past. John Erler (of Master Pancake Theater) cowboys up to the task of voicing-over the entire animated movie He-Man and the Secret of the Sword – by himself. To give you a taste of the script changes that have transpired: Skeletor now enjoys bubble baths and Julia Roberts movies and Beast Man carries an insatiable lust for pudding and butter. This is your last chance to see the show before it disappears into Eternia!

9:45pm | $10 | Buy tickets

512-Go!» Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
409 Colorado St. • 476-1320
