Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Dec 17 - 23

17 December 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | December 17

The War on Democracy

Since 1958 Australian journalist John Pilger has been a critic of the western world’s foreign policy – in particular America’s foreign policy and what he sees as this countries imperialist agenda. His newest documentary, The War On Democracy, explores America’s role in human rights violations throughout South America. Over the course of his career Pilger has written several books and made documentaries about Vietnam, Cambodia, India, and other countries. Among his fans you will find Noam Chomsky, one of the world’s most radical intellectuals.

7pm | $8.25/$6 for students

Alamo Ritz
320 E 6 • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • America’s Top Model Christmas Special and Audience Sing-along at The Hideout Theater | 8pm
  • Book Swap at Irie Bean Coffee Bar | 7:30pm

Tuesday | December 18

Air Hockey Tournament

If you’ve ever dreamed of getting really serious about the game of air hockey, here’s your chance: The Austin Air Hockey Association is sponsoring a weekly air hockey tournament on Tuesday nights, and it’s just $5 to enter. The tournament starts at 9:30, but show up a little earlier if you want to sign up. They’ll have free air hockey going on before and after the tournament.

9:30pm | $5 to enter

Dirty Dog Bar
505 E 6th St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Beauty Bar Holiday Party at Beauty Bar | 10pm
  • ’90s Night at Elysium | 10pm

Wednesday | December 19

Rehearsing the American Dream: Arthur Miller’s Theater

Not only was Arthur Miller a playwright, he was also a carpenter, and for a brief time he was married to Marylin Monroe – before Joe Dimaggio. He pointed out once in an interview that Marylin Monroe had an ugly nose – a putty nose that didn’t really fit her face. And if you look hard you’ll see that he was right. But most importantly Arthur Miller challenged the status quo with his writing, most notably with his plays Death of a Salesman and The Crucible, the latter he wrote at great personal and political risk. Miller was one of the first American writers to put his characters’ emotional lives and conscience in conflict with an American capitalist system with its own ends. This exhibit explores Miller’s life and work.

Tuesday, Wednesday, Friday: 10am–5pm; Thursday: 10am–7pm; Saturday and Sunday: 12pm–5pm | FREE

Harry Ransom Center
21 and Guadalupe • Austin, TX 78705

Other options:

  • Evel Knievel film at Alamo Ritz | 11:55pm
  • Polar Express IMAX 3D experience at The Bob Bullock Texas History Museum | 7pm

Thursday | December 20

courtesy Jimmie Roulette (all rights reserved) Jimmie Roulette loves nature 

Jimmie Roulette w/ Bryan Gutmann

Jimmie Roulette, you’re thinking, I think he’s that greasy comic who stunk like cigarettes and shame we saw do a set at the Circus Circus buffet in Vegas last summer. Nope – Austin’s own Jimmie Roulette is brilliant comic with an impressive stage presence who tours the world performing stand-up. You may have seen him on Comedy Central’s Premium Blend, at the HBO Comedy Festival, or hosting one of the Funniest Person in Austin rounds this year. With Bryan Gutmann opening, this is definitely one of the best shows to end the year.

8pm | $9–$12 | Get tickets

Cap City Comedy Club
8120 Research Blvd • Austin, TX 78758

Other options:

  • KOOP’s 13th Anniversary at Ruta Maya | 8pm
  • Country dance lessons at The Broken Spoke | 7pm

Friday | December 21

Rosencrantz and Guildenstern Are Dead

Born in 1937, Tom Stoppard had an international upbringing, leaving Czechoslovakia with his family for Singapore with many other Jews in 1939. From there they moved to India and eventually settled in Britain. Stoppard worked hard in his studies, and his first play, A Walk on the Water, won him modest success. He went on to write Rosencrantz and Guildenstern Are Dead for which he won a Tony. The play is an absurdist tragicomedy in which two minor characters from Shakespeare’s Hamlet are the main characters acting out an inverse version of Hamlet. The play alludes not only to Hamlet but also to Beckett’s Waiting for Gadot in terms of literary techniques and themes – though stands wholly on its own as a work of art. They play is highly entertaining and will keep you laughing – and this is the last week for you to catch this production.

8pm | $15–$20

City Theater
3823 Airport Blvd. Suite D • Austin, TX 78722

Other options:

  • The Skunks Holiday Reunion at Continental Club | 10pm
  • Cafe Libro at Resistencia Bookstore | 7pm

Saturday | December 22

Winter Solstice Party

Celebrate Winter Solstice at this free, all-ages party that features live performances from Gahar, Guymon Adams, and Sullen Serenade. Since the party’s at Secret Oktober, you’ll have a chance to shop for last-minute holiday gifts in the store and at the surrounding stores like End of an Ear.

4–8pm | FREE

Secret Oktober
1905-B S 1st St • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Lisa DeLarios at The Velveeta Room | 9:30 and 11:30pm

Sunday | December 23

The Nutcracker

courtesy Ballet Austin Nice, brah 

Ballet Austin’s production of The Nutcracker runs through today, and it’s so popular that just about all of the shows this year have sold out. Such is the case with tonight’s show, though if you don’t mind an obstructed view seat, those tickets are still available. They’ve been doing the show for 45 years, so it’s no wonder tickets are so hard to come by. Treat yourself and your loved ones to this awesome holiday tradition.

2pm | $15–$59 | Get tickets

The Paramount Theater
713 Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78701
