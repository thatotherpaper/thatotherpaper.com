Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Dec 10 - 16

10 December 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | December 10

courtesy Women and Their Work 

Pattern Pattern Pattern

Though these works of art vary widely in the nature of their construction and aesthetic each work is rooted in derives it’s meaning from pattern. Featured artists (all Austin-based) include: Germaine Keller, Laura Paulini, Amanda Barr, Rosemarie Fiore, Lisa Solomon, and Jana Swek with Shea Little. If you go later in the afternoon and stay until 6pm, you might want to go check out the Christmas lights on 37th street – that’s free, too.

Mon–Fri: 9am–6am; Sat, Sun: 12pm–5pm | FREE

Women and Their Work
1710 Lavaca St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Vegetarian/Vegan Speed Dating at Dhaba Joy | 6pm
  • Casablanca French Moroccan Feast at Alamo Ritz | 7pm

Tuesday | December 11

photo / [http://www.flickr.com/photos/grilledcheese/1912089761/ Duran Duran 

Duran Duran

Have you been hungry like the wolf for some Duran Duran lately? Even if the band became famous before you were born, you still know the songs and secretly rock out to them on Bob FM on your way to work. Well now you can rock out to Duran Duran in person! The Brits are coming to the Austin Music Hall today.

8pm | $52 | Get tickets

Austin Music Hall
208 Nueces St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • The Tossers at Emo’s | 10pm
  • The Consciousness Group at BookPeople | 7pm

Wednesday | December 12

courtesy Donald Pitschel 

PowerPoint Slam

Conceived by our friend Donald Pitschel, PowerPoint Slam is an interesting concept – it gives you the opportunity to subvert the PowerPoint presentation, break it out of the office, and use it as a tool to express yourself. It’s all about defying the oversimplified corporate medium. Anyone is welcome to come watch the presentations. If you think you want to participate by making a presentation yourself, you can send a five-minute presentation to submissions@powerpointslam.com. The event is held at Salvage Vanguard Theater, which may be a little hard to find. If you’re coming from west of I35, drive east on Manor; you’ll pass Larry Lane on your left, then (soon) Curtis Avenue on your right – SVT is on your right, across the street from Merrie Lynn Avenue. Check out the map.

8pm | $5

Salvage Vanguard Theater
902 E 5th St • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • A Child’s Christmas in Whales: a Jazz Performance at Harry Ransom Center | 7:30pm
  • Weird Wednesday: Sex Demons at Alamo Ritz | 11:55pm

Thursday | December 13

X-Mas: UnWrapped! A Holiday Burlesque

All year, Shrewd Productions brings you award-winning shows full of thought-provoking material, mature content, and real-life nudity. But each December, they raise money by putting on pasties. X-Mas UnWrapped is running for its last week Thursday through Saturday, and you don’t want to miss the deluge of flesh. They’ve got whips, dancing man-elves, alcohol galore, and of course the lovely Jingle Belles. Call 479-PLAY for reservations, because the tickets are disappearing faster than the clothing.

Thurs–Sat: 10pm | $15–$30, sliding scale

Hyde Park Theatre
511 W 43rd St • Austin, TX 78751

Other options:

  • Johnny Goudie at Lamberts | 10:30pm
  • The Method Gun at The Off Center | 8pm

Friday | December 14

photo / Kristin Hillery DJ Jester 

DJ Jester, Dance Like Robots, DJ Gibb

DJ Jester the Filipino Fist spins with some of Austin’s finest. He’s been featured in many publications, including Spin, GQ, and The Village Voice, among many others. Touring extensively, he’s joined the bill with acts such as Kid Koala, Of Montreal, Grand Buffet, MGMT, and Lederhosen Luci. And he will bring the fist. He specializes in combining beats and tracks you never thought possible.

10pm | Call venue for price info

Emo’s
603 Red River • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • The Toadies at Austin Music Hall | 8pm
  • Prince Klassen at Beauty Bar | 10pm

Saturday | December 15

Abuelita’s Christmas Carol 

Abuelita’s Christmas Carol

We can always count on writer/actor/comedian Alex Garza to help put on some awesomely good productions. And this is no exception – Abuelita’s Christmas Carol is about Rosita, a 70-year-old grandma with a cooking show who, when the holidays come around, starts thinking her life is meaningless. She winds up being visited by a few ghosts, including the ghosts of two of her dead husbands, before finally realizing she does have a wonderful life. The story is familiar, but with Alex Garza playing the title role and cast of characters in the show, it will certainly be a hilarious take on a traditional Christmas story.

8pm | $5–$8

Mexican American Cultural Center
600 Red River St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • The Walkmen at Emo’s | 10pm
  • Ararat Holiday Dinner at Hot Mama’s Espresso Bar | 7pm

Sunday | December 16

Keep Austin Bizarre Gift Show & Christmas Bazaar

photo / senor codo 

Forget the mall – want to get your Christmas shopping done in one day and support local artists and businesses while you’re at it? The Keep Austin Bizarre Gift Show & Christmas Bazaar is a locally created 10-day shopping event that showcases local artists, designers, small businesses. You can buy everything from jewelry to clothes to crafts while eating food and listening to live music. Check out the website for lists of exhibitors and musicians, as well as information on parking and tickets. If you can’t make it today, don’t sweat it – the bazaar is happening from December 14–23.

11am–11pm | $5

Palmer Events Center
900 Barton Springs Rd • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • A Festive Feminist Soiree benefiting BookWoman at The Old School | 6pm
  • Pillow Fight Club at Beauty Bar | 10pm
