Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: August 27 - September 2

27 August 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | August 27

101X Guitar Hero 2 Happy Hour

photo / Jimmy Prescott 

Head over to Stubb’s after work to challenge a buddy or a stranger to Guitar Hero 2. Presented by 101X, the Guitar Hero 2 happy hour at Stubb’s is pretty much the best idea ever. Sign up between 5pm and 5:45pm – the first 40 people to sign up will compete, and the winner gets tickets to a Stubb’s show and the Sunday Gospel Brunch.

6pm–10pm | NO COVER

Stubb’s Bar-B-Q
801 Red River • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • The Roller, Iron Age, Black Cobra at Emo’s | 10pm
  • Rose’s Pawn Shop, Miss Leslie and her Juke-Jointers at the Continental Club | 9:30pm.

Tuesday | August 21

Total lunar eclipse

photo / foxypar4 

It’s early but definitely worth it – the second full lunar eclipse in three years happens early this morning. Count yourself lucky, because Africa, Europe, and the Middle East don’t get to see this one. The next full lunar eclipse happens February 21, 2008. Check out this great MSNBC article for a more detailed explanation of the eclipse.

Partial eclipse at 4:52am; total eclipse at 5:52am | FREE

Your backyard

Other options:

  • Out of Bounds Improv Festival at the Hideout

Wednesday | August 29

The Hudsons at The Cactus Cafe

The Hudsons were voted best folk band at this year’s Austin Music Awards. Deft musical stylings accompanied by crisp harmonies and a clever sense of humor always make for a fun show. This show at the Cactus Cafe marks the release of their much anticipated third album, Before I Sleep. The Cactus Cafe (as cozy as it is) will most likely be crowded, so get there early to ensure you get a good spot.

9pm | $6

Cactus Cafe
24th and Guadalupe • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • Prom Nite, Peel, Alexander’s Dark Band, The Hunnies at Emo’s | 10p
  • Subrosa Union, Don Chani at the Flamingo Cantina | 10p.
  • Out of Bounds Improv Festival at the Hideout

Thursday | August 30

Silver Lining flat lining: Michael Wutz

courtesy Volitant Gallery 

Volitant Gallery will be showcasing German artist Michael Wutz’s nihilistic view of the world’s future through September 22. Michael Wutz’s exhibit utilizes different mediums, specifically including three large-scale paintings, one large-scale etching, and multiple series involving graphite paper, Polaroid-format and hand-painted etchings, to visually illustrate how the world is “flat lining.” The artist also summarizes these traditional forms of art, which draw on modern-day media for inspiration, into a video installation.

11am–9pm | FREE

512-Go!» Volitant Gallery
320 Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • J. Price w/ The Vehicle Reason at Stubb’s | 10pm
  • Music happy hour with Meagan Tubb and the Shady People at Ruta Maya | 7pm–9pm
  • Out of Bounds Improv Festival at the Hideout

Friday | August 31

DJ NU-Mark of Jurassic Five

photo / [www.flickr.com/photos/ninjaneil902 ninjaneil902] 

DJ NU-Mark has produced over half of Jurassic Five’s body of work (Jurassic Five EP, Quality Control, Power in Numbers, and Feedback), and has toured with them all over the globe. Onstage NU-Mark is known for his innovative spinning, high energy, and use of found instruments, like children’s toys that make sounds. A master at multi-tasking, he operates his six-piece DJ set-up like turntable Jedi. He’ll be joined by Austinite DJ Manny of DJ Dojo. Additionally Flyjack, a band specializing in rare funk hooks, will play.

9pm | $10

The Parish
214 E 6th St • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • Black Angels, The Strange Boys, and Cavedweller at Emo’s | 10pm
  • Future Clouds and Radar, The White Ghost Shivers at The Mohawk | 9pm
  • Out of Bounds Improv Festival at the Hideout
  • Black Sheep premier at Alamo Drafthouse South Lamar | 7:30pm

Saturday | September 1

Self Portrait Painting Show

courtesy Austin Figurative Gallery 

Local artists’ 2-D self portraits will be on display today at the Austin Figurative Gallery. Seeing these pieces will be like looking into the artists’ tortured souls or something. The gallery is accepting work up until August 31, so if you’d like to make a last-minute entry, go here for more info. If you love what you see, come back tomorrow for the Self Portrait Photography Show.

7pm–11pm | FREE

Austin Figurative Gallery
301 Chicon • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • First Annual Austin Sound Off Festival at the Scoot Inn | 3pm–2am
  • Out of Bounds Improv Festival at the Hideout
  • Artist’s Market at Mother Egan’s Irish Pub | 10pm
  • Indie Night at Jovita’s | 7pm

Sunday | September 2

Pop Stars Brunch

Nurse that hangover with live music, food, and a spot on Opal Divine’s patio (which is kept cool by water mist fans). Beaver Nelson, Michael Fracasso, and Matt the Electrician are scheduled to rock you as you stuff your face.

10am–3pm

Opal Divine’s Penn Field
3601 S Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Ultimate Beatles Jam at Antone’s | 7:30pm
  • Out of Bounds Improv Festival at the Hideout
  • First Annual Austin Kids’ Day at The Glenn at the Backyard | 1:30pm
