Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Nov 26 - Dec 2

26 November 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | November 26

GWAR

photo / Jason White 

If you thought boredom was the only thing you had to fear on Monday night, you were wrong. GWAR is coming to rock the fuck out of your sorry fleshbag! For you pathetic, weak humans who don’t know about GWAR, here’s a quick tutorial: After terrorizing the galaxy with unspeakable crimes against all living creatures on all planets for eons and eons, the cosmic warriors that comprise the crossover thrash-metal band GWAR were banished to the most insignificant shithole of a planet in the universe: Earth. Here they were frozen in a coma of pure hatred until the toxic waste created by humans thawed them out. Newly reanimated, GWAR formed a metal band dedicated to the destruction of the human race, and eventually the destruction of existence itself. They are going to rock you to DEATH!!!

8pm | $15

Emo’s
603 Red River • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Manic Panic Monday at Red Scoot Inn | 8pm
  • New and Noteworthy Book Club at BookPeople | 7pm

Tuesday | November 27

Jitterbug Vipers and fancy barbecue

The Jitterbug Vipers sound like “a speakeasy in the ’40s… smokey, sexy, unfettered fun,” according to their MySpace page. And that sounds just perfect to us, especially if you’re looking for a way to chill out after a long day at work. Grab a booth, table, or seat at the bar upstairs at Lamberts a bit earlier to take advantage of their happy hour specials. Fancy barbecue is what they do there, and they do it well – be sure to sample the Frito pie and save room for dessert.

7pm | No cover; happy hour specials

Lamberts
401 W 2nd St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Silverchair at La Zona Rosa | 8pm
  • Tai Chi with Guy Forsyth at Ruta Maya | 1pm

Wednesday | November 28

DJ Manny, Adon, Exceed

photo / Todd Ross Nienkerk DJ Manny 

Tonight is the first night of Wax on Wednesday at the Parish Room. From here on out (for a while at least) the Parish will be staging a weekly Wednesday event featuring local, national, and international DJs. And for this week our friend DJ Manny, of DJ Dojo, will be headlining. There will be art sponsored by Austin Art Garage and a wicked light show assembled by synaesthesiax. It’s free for the 21+ crowd.

8pm | FREE; $10 for under 21

The Parish
214 E Sixth St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Weird Wednesday: Office Girls at Alamo Ritz | 11:55pm
  • An Evening with the Rude Mechanicals at The Off Center | 8pm

Thursday | November 29

photo courtesy Electric Six Electric Six 

Electric Six

Detroit’s Electric Six play at Emo’s tonight with Willowz and We Are The Fury. With the recent release of Electric Six’s I Shall Exterminate Everything Around Me That Restricts Me From Being The Master, you can expect to hear songs from the album as well as classics like “Gay Bar.”

Don’t miss our interview with Electric Six’s Dick Valentine

10pm | $10 | Get tickets

 

Emo’s
603 Red River • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Parallelogramophonograph Presents: Improvised French Farce at ColdTowne Theater | 8pm
  • Arts and Drafts at Rio Rita | 7pm

Friday | November 30

John Dee Graham

John Dee Graham has long been a force in the Austin music scene. He’s lived a road-hard life and his music is the better for it. In 2006 he was named Musician of the Year at SXSW. His discography is prolific and there’s no denying the man has style, both music and fashion-wise. He still only charges $10 to get in the door. He will be celebrating the release of yet another album, Swept Away – a live recording.

10pm | $10

Continental Club
1315 S Congress • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Austin Ice Bats vs Corpus Christi Rays at Chaparral Ice | 7:35pm
  • Handel’s Massiah at St. Martin’s Lutheran Church | 7pm

Saturday | December 1

Acknowledge Me

Acknowledge Me! 

I Love Mike Litt and Raji World present Acknowledge Me, a celebration of all things indie in Austin. Paying the $5 cover at the door – which benefits Habitat on Wheels – gets you access to local media (including TOP), a DIY crafts market, free vegan fare, live music, and documentary screenings. Bands scheduled to perform: The Mysterious H, Canopy, Screamin Baby Heads, Billy Harvey, Brazos, Toko-Ri Get High, and Omega Monster Patrol.

8pm–2am | $5

The Scoot Inn
1308 E 4th St • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • Sunset Valley Farmers Market at Tony Burger Center | 9am
  • Eric Krug w/ Carey Moore and Kerri Lendo at The Velveeta Room | 9:30 and 11:30pm

Sunday | December 2

photo / Veronica Hansen 

Zilker Park Christmas Tree Lighting

The 155-foot tall Zilker Christmas Tree is an Austin tradition. It consists of 3,309 bulbs on 39 streamers strung from a Moonlight Tower. If you can, bring cash or a new, unwrapped toy to donate to Operation Blue Santa.

6pm | FREE

Zilker Park
2100 Barton Springs Rd • Austin, TX 78746

Other options:

  • Famecast Fenom Finals at Stubb’s | 7pm
  • Dress Up: Portrait and Performance in Victorian Photography at Harry Ransom Center | Noon–5pm
