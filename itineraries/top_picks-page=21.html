TOP picks: March 10-16

10 March 2008 | That Other Paper

Monday | March 10

Nuclear Taco Night

What makes nuclear tacos so hot? Well, it’s not the habañeros puree – that’s just for the flavor. Nuclear tacos are made with the feared red savina pepper, which packs a walloping 580,000 Scovilles. (By comparison, those jalapeños you put on your nachos weigh in at a measly 8,000 Scovilles.) These tacos aren’t for the faint of heart or stomach.

Nuclear Taco Night at SXSW 2005

Join your fellow masochistic gourmets for a night of free tacos (and ice cream to soothe your tastebuds). But be careful how much you eat: Nuclear tacos will get you in the end.

6pm | FREE

Nuclear Taco Night on Upcoming

Brush Square Park | Nuclear Taco Night on Upcoming
409 East 5th St. (5th and Neches) • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • The Great British Booze-up
  • South by Northwest 3rd Annual Geek Fest Party
  • Frrvrr Launch Party: Monday Night Frrvrr!

Tuesday | March 11

Southern Drama Circus Party 

Southern Drama Circus Party

From the Circus Party press release:

Self proclaimed “Transylvanian Violin Rock” outfit Southern Drama has just announced plans for their upcoming album release and film premiere party. Southern Drama’s Circus Party will take place on the eve of the South By Southwest Music Festival at Austin’s infamous, circus-themed, dive bar The Carousel Lounge. This free event will be a carnival of sorts featuring live circus entertainment, palm reading, music, film, cabaret, a live emcee, and an art show with work by DawnStarArt (glam-pop oil-on-canvas) and the Painton Gallery (burlesque carnival art). Attendees will be able to relive their chilhood with free confectionary delights provided by sponsors Tiff’s Treats Cookie Delivery and South Congress’ own Big Top Candy Shop and Hey Cupcake! For those who wish, The Carousel Lounge conveniently allows patrons to BYO liquor and wine.

Warning: If you’ve never been to the Carousel, brace yourself for a truly Lynchian experience.

7pm–12am | FREE

Carousel Lounge
1110 E. 52nd St. • Austin, TX 78752

Other option:

  • Art Disaster No. 5

Wednesday | March 12

The Upright Citizens: Matt Walsh, Matt Besser, Ian Roberts, and Amy Poehler 

The Upright Citizens Brigade in ASSSSSCAT Improv

The (original) Upright Citizens Brigade – Matt Besser, Matt Walsh, and Ian Roberts – are performing two shows with local improv troupe ColdTowne at the USArt Authority. From ColdTowne’s press release:

It begins with a suggestion of a single word from the audience. From there, one performer improvises a monologue, which plants the seeds for the cast to build a series of interlocking, improvised scenes. The show follows a loose structure where character and story are instantly created, resulting in a surprisingly fresh, mysteriously interconnected and hilarious piece of improv comedy that can never be performed the same way twice. Local nerdcore Rap sensation Terp2It opens. Presented by UCBcomedy.com.

8pm and 10pm| $10

USArt Authority
510 W 29th St (next to Spider House) • Austin, TX 78705

Other options:

  • Sustainable Food Center’s Picnic in the Park
  • Creative Arts Society Visual Art Exhibit

Thursday | March 20

Grammar for the Soul 

Lawrence Weinstein – Grammar for the Soul

If you’re as big fans of grammar as we are, you won’t want to miss Lawrence Weinsten, author of Grammar for the Soul, at BookPeople. Weinstein’s goal is to show how grammar can make you better as a person. From Amazon.com:

Weinstein demonstrates numerous surprising connections between elements of grammar and human attributes we aspire to – connections, for example, between use of the “double-barreled” punctuation mark, the colon, and assertiveness; between use of passive constructions like “my hand is guided” and creativity; and between use of the first-person pronoun “I” and heightened awareness.

Grammar lovers and the self-aware unite!

7pm | Free

BookPeople
603 N Lamar • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Blue October at La Zona Rosa | 8pm
  • Night of the Living ’80s at Light Bar | 10pm

Friday | March 14

The Vagina Monologues 

The Vagina Monologues

Eve Ensler’s play The Vagina Monologues will play at the Enchanted Forest today. The play has won an Obie and played internationally. In 1998 Ensler founded a non-profit called V-Day. Those who participate in V-Day put on benefit performances of the play – that money in turn goes to various organizations that help end violence against females. This particular performance of The Vagina Monologues will help out Service Women’s Action Network and Austin Settlement Home. As if that’s not cool enough, Eve Ensler will be at the performance.

6pm | $15

Enchanted Forest
1412 W Oltorf • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Make Funny Not War at Esther’s Follies | 8pm
  • Lorin Friesen paintings at Austin Figurative Gallery | 7 pm

Saturday | March 15

Mess With Texas 2 

Mess with Texas Party 2

Last year’s Mess with Texas party was insanely fun, so it’s great to hear that this event is likely turning into an annual thing. Featuring a ton of great comics, Mess with Texas is a free, all-ages show that’s going to take over Waterloo Park.

1pm | FREE

Waterloo Park
1301 Trinity St • Austin, TX

Sunday | March 16

South Austin Psychedelic Sunday

A party at the Cathedral of Junk? Count us in. Scheduled to perform: Lead Sister II, Diamondhead, The Space Invader Orchestra, X+X, and Book of Shadows. Even if you don’t know much about these bands, it’s a great opportunity to come check out the Cathedral.

2pm | FREE

Cathedral of Junk
4422 Lareina Dr • Austin, TX 78745
