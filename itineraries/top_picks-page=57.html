TOP picks: July 2 - 8

2 July 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | July 2

Transformers w/ Robosaurus LIVE

Alamo Drafthouse, as usual, is doing it right: They’ll be showing Transformers on every screen in the house. But before that, they’ll have Robosaurus, a 60-foot metal beast, in the parking lot eating cars, spitting fire, and just destroying. Destroying! If you purchase the highest priced tickets, you will be considered VIP and are guaranteed a front row seat as well as some party favors.

7pm, 8:45pm | $25–$75 | Tickets Both shows are SOLD OUT

Alamo Drafthouse South Lamar
1120 S Lamar • 476-1320

Tuesday | July 3

photo / Matthew Simoneau 

An Evening with Lez Zeppelin

New York–based all-female Led Zeppelin tribute band Lez Zeppelin plays tonight at La Zona Rosa. The band formed in 2004 and has already garnered rave reviews.

From lazonarosa.com:

LEZ ZEPPELIN stays true to the musically audacious spirit of the original, delivering the legendary rock band’s blistering arrangements and monstrous sound note-for-note at sold-out shows across the country. The thing that sets them apart, however, is the seamless and unique way LEZ ZEPPELIN inject their gender-bending performance into the original material.

9pm | $20 | Tickets

La Zona Rosa
612 W 4th St • Austin, TX 78701

Thursday | July 5

The Sickest F***ing Stories I Ever Heard 

The Sickest F***ing Stories I Ever Heard

We’re sponsoring The Sickest F***ing Stories I Ever Heard, and we couldn’t be more excited about it: Charlie Hodge, Owen Egerton, Leah Moss, Tim Murphy, and Asaf Ronen will be playing poker with real money, drinking real beer, and telling real sick stories onstage. It’s an exercise in comedy vérité that’s as bluntly provocative as its title.

More info

9pm | $10 (includes drink ticket)

512-Go!» The Hideout
617 Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78701

Friday | July 6

Opening reception for Jessica Russell at the Opera House

A new store behind End of an Ear on South 1st Street, The Opera House has treasures, gifts, clothing, and a space for events. Tonight, come celebrate the opening of Jessica Russell’s month-long art exhibition at the Opera House, which features her new 2-D and 3-D works that were made using embroidery, collage, found objects, and other natural materials. There will also be live music from Silver Pines, Super Cougar, and more.

7pm | FREE

The Opera House
2209 S 1st St • 440-1401

Saturday | July 7

The Constant Wife 

The Constant Wife

Theater production company Different Stages Inc is dedicated to performing lesser-known theatrical works in addition to the greats like Bill Shakespeare. They perform all around town, and their latest play, The Constant Wife by famed novelist W. Somerset Maugham, is almost their 80th production since the company was founded in 1980.

8pm | $10–$30 (pay what you can)

The Vortex
2307 Manor Road • 478-5282

Sunday | July 8

photo / ninnet 

Austin Symphony Concerts in the Park

Every Sunday, the Austin Symphony puts on free concerts in Wooldridge Park. A different ensemble performs every Sunday under the gazebo. Today’s ensemble is the Big Band, and they’ll be playing audience requests. A very romantic first date idea.

From austinsymphony.org:

The music and atmosphere will reflect a simpler time when performances of familiar classics in town centers were commonplace. It is the hope of the Hartman Family that these free and very informal outdoor events will provide an opportunity for Austinites to become acquainted or reacquainted with the classical music experience. As the sun begins to set, music will fill Wooldridge Park and will last about an hour. These delightful outdoor park concerts are a perfect opportunity for Austinites to enjoy some of the Capitol City’s greatest pleasures - the Austin Symphony’s fine music and one of Austin’s beautiful historic parks.

Tip: Take a picnic basket (no alcohol or glass containers) and a blanket (don’t forget bug repellent).

7:30pm | FREE

Wooldridge Square Park
9th and Guadalupe • Austin, TX 78701
