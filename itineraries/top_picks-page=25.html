Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: Feb 11 - 17

11 February 2008 | That Other Paper

Monday | February 11

T-Bird and Breaks

This local 10 piece brings together guitar, bass, drums, trombone, trumpet, two saxes, and three vocalists – most notably Tim Crane (whose hair is as slick as his suits) – to lay down some funky, dynamic R&B. The perfect cure for the Monday night blues.

7pm

Antone’s
213 5th St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Film on the rocks (King Kong) at Club DeVille | 9:45pm
  • Dr. Arjun Makhijani – Dinner talk regarding nuclear energy at The Clay Pit | 7pm

Tuesday | February 12

Jess: To and From the Printed Page

courtesy Harry Ransom Center 

Today opens the traveling exhibition Jess: To and From the Printed Page, which showcases works by the artist known professionally by the single name Jess. Born Burgess Collins, Jess is a chemist-turned-artist who abandoned his scientific career because of the development of atomic weapons and instead dedicated his life to art. This theme is prominent in his work, as are alchemy, the occult, and male beauty. His art is also full of literary references, an homage to his experiences in the literary world. Read more about Jess’s life and art at the Harry Ransom Center website. You can take a guided tour at 2.

10am–5pm | FREE

Harry Ransom Center
21st and Guadalupe • Austin, TX 78712

Other options:

  • John Leake, author of Entering Hades at BookPeople | 7pm
  • Austin Film Society Essential Screening of High Noon at Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz | 7pm

Wednesday | February 13

PowerPoint Slam

courtesy Donald Pitschel 

Once again it’s time for PowerPoint Slam. For those of you who don’t know, PowerPoint Slam, conceived by our friend Donald Pitschel, is an event that allows you to create and present a PowerPoint presentation about whatever you want – taking the PowerPoint Slam out of the office setting and to the people. The event continues to get bigger each month, and it’s always a good time. “I encourage anyone who has any unusual ideas or odd juxtapositions,” says Pitschel. “If something doesn’t belong on a slideshow, and you want to put on a slide show – do it.”

If you think you want to participate, you can send a five-minute presentation to submissions@powerpointslam.com.

8pm | $5

Salvage Vanguard Theater
2803 Manor Rd • Austin, TX 78722

Other options:

  • Molotov at La Zona Rosa | 7pm
  • Girl in a Coma at Emo’s | 10pm

Thursday | February 14

Stool Pigeon

Whether you’re single, in a relationship, or you hate Valentine’s Day, you’ll have a good time at ColdTowne Theater’s special Valentine’s Day Stool Pigeon tonight, an event we’re proud to be sponsoring. While Stool Pigeon usually involves a celebrity guest telling a few monologues that then inspire the improvisers, tonight it’s a bit different – everyone is the celebrity guest. The all-star cast of improvisers will be improvising scenes based on audience members’ “nightmare scenarios of love gone wrong.”

10pm | $7–10

ColdTowne Theater
4803 Airport Blvd • Austin, TX 78751

Other options:

  • Valentinos at United States Art Authority | 6:30pm
  • Dan Dyer free in-store performance at Waterloo Records | 5pm

Friday | February 15

Big Star Burlesque One-Year Anniversary Party

courtesy Big Star Burlesque (all rights reserved) 

It’s hard to believe that it’s only been one year since the ladies of Big Star Burlesque got together. Both sexy and funny, the shows are a blast – each Big Star girl brings something different, unique, and beautiful to the stage. Tonight’s party features not only a performance from Big Star Burlesque, but live music and more burlesque from other local troupes. TOP is proud to be sponsoring this party.

9pm | $10 in advance; $12 at the door

Emo’s
603 Red River St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Punchline at ColdTowne Theater | 10pm
  • Lions and Transmography at Victory Grill | 9pm

Saturday | February 16

Rodrigo y Gabriella

Rodrigo Sanchez and Gabriela Quintero both grew up in Mexico City. After their thrash metal band, Tierra Acida, didn’t pan out – they decided to travel to Dublin. Their plan was to play acoustic music in hotels. But things in Dublin don’t work like they do in Mexico City – you can’t just walk into a hotel with your guitars and get a gig. So they played on the streets until they earned a name for themselves. Rodrigo y Gabriella play in a traditional Mexican style – but they do it fast, like thrash metal.

7pm | $30

La Zona Rosa
612 W 4th St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • A Lovers Feast by Ararat at Hot Mama’s Espresso | 6:30pm
  • Buckner/Chappell/Ohlerking Paintings, Drawings, Whatever at Austin Figurative Gallery | 7pm

Sunday | February 17

Rock On: An Office Power Ballad 

Dan Kennedy – Rock On: An Office Power Ballad

America can’t get enough of year-or-so-in-the-life-of- someone-in-the-industry books. The Devil Wears Prada described the nitty gritty of the fashion world and The Nanny Diaries chronicled Upper-East Side New York nannying. Now we have a book about another glamorous industry, the music industry. Author Dan Kennedy got a job in music right before the industry collapsed, and his recounting of his experiences has made this book the next semi-autobiographical big thing. Don’t miss Kennedy at BookPeople – it’s sure to be entertaining.

3pm | FREE

BookPeople
603 N Lamar • Austin, TX 78703

Other options:

  • Marvin’s Room at The Wimberley Playhouse | 2:30pm
  • Gospel brunch at Stubb’s | 11am or 1pm (Call 512-480-8341 ext. 306 for reservations)
