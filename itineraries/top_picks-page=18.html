Itineraries: Stuff to do in Austin

TOP picks: What we recommend

TOP picks: March 31 - April 6

31 March 2008 | That Other Paper

Monday | March 31

Logo Pint Night at The Ginger Man

photo / leduardo  

Every Monday, The Ginger Man pub, which boasts over 80 beers on tap, has its Logo Pint Night. You buy a beer, you keep the glass. It’s first-come first-serve, so make sure you get there a little early so you can get your free mug.

6pm | FREE with a pint of beer

The Ginger Man
304 W 4th St • Austin, TX 78701

Tuesday | April 1

Austin Documentary: The Unforeseen

Award-winning documentary The Unforeseen follows Gary Bradley, who was born a Texas farm boy, moved to Austin, and became a real estate mogul. His plans to destroy Barton Springs incensed Austinites and jump-started hard-core environmentalism in the city.

From the Alamo Drafthouse:

THE UNFORESEEN is a meditation on the destruction of the natural world and the American Dream as it falls victim to the cannibalizing forces of unchecked development. It is an intricate tale of personal hopes, victories, and failures, and debates over land, economics, property rights, and the public good. In a time when development and property values have skyrocketed in nearly every major city, Dunn makes a plea for our development-oriented society to consider restructuring the relationship between our values and the environment that sustains us.

Buy your tickets here.

7:15pm, 9:45pm | $8.25

Alamo Drafthouse South Lamar
1120 South Lamar Blvd • Austin, TX 78704

Wednesday | April 2

image courtesy Eisley 

Eisley, The Myriad, Melee, and The Envy Corps

Tyler band Eisley is back in Austin. They played at Waterloo Records back in August; now don’t miss them at Antone’s. Get tickets here.

7pm | $15–$17

Antone’s
213 W 5th St • Austin, TX 78701

Thursday | April 3

Jacqueline DeCarlo Fair Trade Lecture

Author Jacqueline DeCarlo, author of Fair Trade: A Beginner’s Guide, has been all over the world and witnessed firsthand the benefits of Fair Trade practices. Her travels include Africa, Latin America, and Europe, and she currently works as a Fair Trade Program Advisor to Catholic Relief Services. Hear her speak about her experiences tonight at BookWoman.

7pm–9pm | Free

BookWoman
5501 N Lamar • Austin, TX 78751

Friday | April 4

The Rude Mechanicals Present The Method Gun

image courtesy The Rude Mechanicals 

Austin acting company The Rude Mechanicals present their latest performance, The Method Gun, written by their own Kirk Lynn and directed by their own Shawn Sides. The play is about acting – it “explores the life, ethos, and techniques of actor-training guru, Stella Burden, as recounted through the eyes of her students… in an effort to infuse a sense of depth, purpose, and urgency into even the smallest of roles.” The play has received support from Creative Capital Foundation, Rockefeller MAP Fund, The Orchard Project, The National Endowment for the Arts, The Humanities Institute and The Harry Ransom Center. Read more about the Rude Mechs and their shows at their website. Buy tickets here.

7pm | $28

Debra and Kevin Rollins Theatre, The Long Center
701 W Riverside Dr • Austin, TX 78704

Saturday | April 5

Dance Party at the Enchanted Forest

photo / Chad Hanna Waterwheel installation at the Enchanted Forest 

This is going to be one of the largest dance parties of the year. And the Enchanted Forest is a good place to get lost – acres and acres of weirdness all right in the middle of the city. DJ Manny and Sage the Firedancer will provide the entertainment for the evening. The event will be BYOB; your $10 cover will go toward maintaining the forest. Costumes are encouraged.

7pm | $10

The Enchanted Forest
1401 W Oltorf • Austin, TX 78704

Sunday | April 6

Texas Rollergirls

The Texas Rollergirls roll tonight at Playland Skate Park. First it’s the Hotrod Honeys v The Hustlers. Though the Hotrod Honeys have been doing well lately, the Hustlers are some hard-ass dames. For the second round The Tornado Alley Rollergirls face off against the Honky Tonk Heartbreakers.

6:30pm | $12

Playland Skate Center
8822 McCann Dr • Austin, TX 78757
