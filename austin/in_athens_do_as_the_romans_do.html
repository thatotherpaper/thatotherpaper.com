In Athens, do as the Romans do

29 November 2006 | Adam Avramescu
photo / Chris Yetter 

Open up Austin in your mental Photoshop. Loaded yet? OK, now crop all the buildings to five stories and surround the immediate downtown/campus area with, say, Pflugerville. Or something. What you’re left with is Athens, Georgia.

It’s the home of the University of Georgia, which encompasses nearly twice the land area of UT-Austin and one-eighth of the racial diversity. It’s also home to R.E.M., the B-52s, Of Montreal, and most of the Elephant Six collective, putting it, with Austin, on the short-list for Hippest in the South. The city is so hip, in fact, that the graffiti inside the men’s bathroom stall at the local Gyro Wrap touts, “Mike Mills has crabs.”

Oh, what’s that? You don’t know who Mike Mills is? Neither did we. He’s the guy from R.E.M. without a cool name like “Stipe” or “Buck.”

I spent the week in Athens with my band, which was playing at indie label Happy Happy Birthday to Me’s annual Indie Pop Festival. The festival is an occasion both to bring yet more high-profile bands (this year: Deerhoof, The Mountain Goats, The Apples in Stereo) to the already-legendary 40-Watt Club and to provide a venue for, essentially, a 60-band circle jerk.

I don’t imagine that the festival, which only employs two venues, overturns the town in the same way as our little-known conference does every March. Still, the influx of musicians and pop fans seems not to have slipped under the locals’ radars. While standing in the parking lot of Little Kings bar with a friend’s band from Seattle, a bulldozer of a man wearing overalls lurched toward us with an eerie glint in his eye: “Y’all ain’t from around here, huh?” What gave it away, the Washington plates?

You can also tell the Athenians from the invaders. It’s like how during South by Southwest, the Austinites are the ones breezing head-down through downtown. We grumble about the increased wait times at our favorite restaurants as the guy from the Brian Jonestown Massacre stands on the street corner waiting to be recognized. Likewise, the Athenians are the ones who don’t give the allegedly crabbed Mills a second glance as he prowls the UGA dorms. They don’t seem to think it’s a big deal that Michael Stipe owns the best vegan restaurant in town as well as the entire surrounding block. It’s old hat to them that Wuxtry Records (and the adjoining Bizarro Wuxtry Comics) is staffed entirely with contributing members to the Olivia Tremor Control, one of the greatest psychedelic bands to operate out of the ’90s.

Most significantly, the Athenians know what’s hidden underneath the glamorous facade: It kind of sucks to live there. Locals say that aside from the school, there’s really no job opportunities in the city. And to make matters worse, Athens suffers the fate of any erstwhile musical Mecca – there’s an extremely steep curve for any upstart band, because everyone in town is already in a band. Supply completely outstrips demand.

photo / Chris Yetter 

And that’s when things started to sound familiar. The “Live Music Capital of the World” title is great for music lovers but a complete curse to musicians trying to work in the town that bears the title, much in the way that an Auto Repair Capital of the World might start to suck once everybody’s cars are running smoothly all the time. We aren’t so different, Austin and Athens. There are so many venues and so many acts playing out that the average showgoer will exhaust himself long before your crappy band’s career does.

Athens is the Old South’s only other working experiment in per-capita hipness. Which I think might say something about the “Let’s Build a College Campus in the Middle of Nowhere” model of urban planning. Sure, it’s seen better days, but unlike some of its counterparts (I’m looking at you, Olympia), it at least seems like it might achieve escape velocity from the ’90s. And it’s pretty fun to walk down the street, mentally noting, He was in Neutral Milk Hotel and She played on an Of Montreal record. A caveat to the ladies, though: Come to Athens with a clean bill of health. You know, just in case the bathroom stall is right.
