Dating advice from Jimmy Suede

28 December 2006 | Jimmy Suede
Jimmy Suede 

Dear Jimmy Suede,

Boom, Jimmy Suede!

I just moved to Austin, and I’m having a really hard time meeting women. You argue that online dating is a viable alternative to going out to bars in your level 36 eBook, Digital Dating: Seducing Women Online with Jimmy Suede, but I have no idea how to get started. Can you help me create a really attractive profile for an online dating website?

Ed from Dripping Springs

BOOM, Ed!

I’m really happy that you asked this question.

The biggest frustration I encountered after reaching player status was the limited time I had to seduce women. Sure, I was getting numbers by the truckload every night at bars, clubs, and AA meetings, but I felt like the rest of the day was being wasted. I wasn’t seducing women in the car, I wasn’t seducing women in my sleep, and I wasn’t seducing women at work (until I discovered the power of workplace romances – you can read more about this in my level 28 eBook, Cock in the Henhouse: Seducing Your Co-Workers with Jimmy Suede). I felt like I was fighting the Abstinence Ogre with one hand tied behind my back.

Until I discovered Online Dating. Online Dating allows you to seduce women constantly – while you’re shaving, while you’re doing laundry, even while you’re seducing other women. The internet finally proved itself useful for something other than allowing Prince Hattah of Zambia to scam me out of $200.

But don’t get the idea that Online Dating is a cakewalk – or even an easy way to get dates. No, Ed: Picking up women online is surprisingly difficult. I was met with cold shoulders and frowny-face emoticons for my first few weeks of online dating. But I didn’t give up. If online dating was anything like real dating, I concluded, it would take hundreds of hours of community service over restraining order violations until I finally achieved success.

Fortunately, success came before any more embarrassing run-ins with the law. I devised a strategy for creating online dating profiles that made them practically irresistible to women, and I plastered them across every dating website on the internet. My inbox was soon filled with emails and “e-Winks” from women who were dying to go on dates with me. Much like the fake personality I assume in real life, my fake online dating personality was attracting women like moths to a flame.

My strategy for crafting an online dating profile centers around what I call the Three Es: Exaggeration, Euphemism, and Reversible Expectations.

photo / Kristin Hillery 

Exaggeration:
Your online dating profile is basically your dating resume, and everyone lies on their resume. You have to embellish your interests and achievements to make yourself seem more interesting than you really are. Have you ever been to a museum? Establish yourself as a sophisticated art critic. Can you read? You’re a voracious consumer of literature. Enjoy the occasional pick-up game of basketball at the neighborhood court? Indicate that you are a professional athlete. Remember – you want to put your best foot forward, even if that foot isn’t yours.

Euphemism:
One thing that online dating allows you to do that real world dating doesn’t is to present your character flaws in a positive light. You know the saying, “Every cloud has a silver lining”? Keep it in mind when crafting your profile – but avoid using it as the caption of your photograph. I learned that lesson the hard way.

Make your flaws work for you. Instead of writing that you live with your parents at the age of 45, write that you’re very family-oriented. If you’re currently in prison, express an interest in stability and consistency. If you were ticketed for trespassing after trying to take pictures of your ex-girlfriend while she slept, claim to be passionate and dedicated in your relationships. The point is, your weaknesses don’t have to keep you out of the dating pool. After all, isn’t the word “flaw” just another way of saying “imperfection”?

Reversible Expectations:
Your profile picture needs to convey the characteristics that women find attractive: wealth, virility, and a clean criminal record. You may not have all or even one of these things, but there’s no reason not to imply that you do in your picture. Remember: implying isn’t lying. My profile picture is of me standing next to a Porsche in the parking lot of an exclusive country club. Now, I don’t own a Porsche – I don’t even own a pair of matching shoes. But I’m not lying because I never claimed to. I can’t help it if someone looks at that picture and gets the wrong impression.

It’s also not lying because I’m confident that I will one day be extraordinarily wealthy. The fact that I borrowed money to pay for a coffee this morning doesn’t make my profile picture a lie, just a chronological inconsistency. My picture will one day reflect reality – until then, it’s just a snapshot of the future. That’s not called lying; it’s called “Thinking Ahead.”

There you have it, Ed. I hope your experience with online dating is as rewarding as mine has been. Just remember: Many men claim to be women online, so request multiple pictures from a variety of angles. Also, stay away from sites that run a background check.

BOOM!

About the author Jimmy Suede has authored 49 eBooks on dating and relationships. Through years of meticulous trial-and-error, Jimmy has developed a keen insight into the dynamics of dating that he dispenses in his column and at workshops and seminars across the country.
