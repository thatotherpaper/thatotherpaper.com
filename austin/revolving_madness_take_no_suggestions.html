Revolving Madness: Take no suggestions

1 September 2007 | Luna
Revolving Madness is: (From L to R) Andreas, Lauren, Michael, and Christina

Improv troupe Revolving Madness hails from San Francisco to perform at the Out of Bounds Improv Festival tonight, September 1, at 10pm at the Hideout Theater. That Other Paper Associate Editor Christine Acker talked with member Lauren Pizzi.

That Other Paper What’s the story behind how your troupe formed?

Lauren Pizzi We were formed as part of a graduate student’s thesis on improv on film. We were in our senior year in the BFA Acting Program at UC Santa Barbara.

TOP What makes your troupe stand out? What’s your shtick?

LP Our shtick is that we have no shtick. We are shtickless. We don’t take suggestions and we love working with each other. We’ve been close friends for years and that enables us to relax, have fun and show the audience a good time.

TOP How’d you get involved in Out of Bounds?

LP We’ve been traveling to festivals a lot this year (Chicago, San Francisco, New York) and we’ve heard that Out of Bounds is one of the best in the country. Some friends of ours are in Shiner and they also told us great things.

TOP What’s the comedy scene like in your home town?

LP San Francisco has a thriving scene with lots of stand-up, gay theatre, and experimental theatre, which creates a supportive environment for our work. We love to travel.

TOP What’s the story behind your troupe name?

LP All of us sitting in a kitchen… trapped… without a name… for days… no food… no water…

Madness doesn’t mean insanity – it means complete commitment and envelopment into the world you create, so that it is truly believed by yourself and, in turn, the audience. We strive for that in every show, and to connect it in a sane way. Revolving it to make a revolution in one hour, to make something come around and be completely new again.

photo courtesy of Revolving Madness 

TOP What’s your worst or most awkward improv moment?

LP Barprov. [Improv performed in bars among patrons who couldn’t care less. –Ed.] And corporate whoring.

TOP What school of improv do you practice?

LP We’ve kinda studied it all and put it all together in our own way. We do long-form, tell a story, create rich characters. Sometimes they’ll be short vignette scenes or a monologue thrown in the middle of a set, and when those things aren’t planned and work, it’s magical.

TOP What have you learned from improv and applied to your life?

LP Be spontaneous, take risks, say yes to the world and splash your color on it. The world needs that and so do we.

TOP What do you do outside of improv? How has improv affected that?

LP The four of us are all involved in lots of different things but here’s a very basic breakdown. Lauren teaches and directs. Mike teaches yoga, is a stagehand for the San Francisco opera, and loves his cat. Christy waits tables, drinks wine, and loves to love. Dre plays soccer and poker and generally is goofy.

TOP What do you like best about improv?

LP When it’s good, it’s really good. It’s like a bomb pizza: even when you’re about to burst, you want more.

Revolving Madness is Andreas Riter, Christy Daly, Mike Michalske, and Lauren Pizzi.
