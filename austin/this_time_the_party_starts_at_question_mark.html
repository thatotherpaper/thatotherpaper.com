This time, the party starts at question mark

7 March 2007 | Charlie Perlman
photo / Elizabeth Brenna 

Hey, Joey, are you coming to my 25th birthday party tonight? Yeah, it’s at my place – didn’t you get the Evite? What? Evite’s really easy to use. All you do is click on the link in the e-mail and then put your name and your RSVP. I get them all the time – no, not from my mom.

Joey, I’m telling you, this is probably the best party you’ll go to all year. I mean, this one’s gonna start at question mark.

Well, it doesn’t matter, because I made enough guacamole for everyone. You’re going to love it. If you notice something a little special about it, that’s the splash of grapefruit juice. Zing! Oh, and I hope you’re not lactose intolerant, because rumor has it there’s going to be some ice cream cake. But you didn’t hear it from me.

No, I don’t have a DJ, but I did make this awesome mixtape. It’s got five different versions of “Macarena” – isn’t that song the best? It always gets everyone dancing. Y’know, one-and-a-two-and-a-three-a-Macarena. Heeeey, Macarena. Well, duh, we’re in public. So what?

Hey, you like magic, don’t you? Well, this is going to be off the chain – are you ready? I hired the best magician in town, Bortellini the Great. He’s amazing. He’ll pull things out of your ear that you didn’t even know you had in there!

What? Sure, go ahead. Show me a magic trick. Oh, I guess you disappeared, huh?

Hey, Amy, over here! Are you coming to my party tonight? I know you marked “maybe” on your Evite, but I was really hoping, y’know, that you’d come. I really like spending time with you.

Well, you can tape Grey’s Anatomy, can’t you? I mean, I was really – really – hoping you’d come. Maybe, uh, as my – uh –

Really? You’ll come? That’s way awesome! Once the party really gets hopping, we’re going to watch the second season of Lost and look for hidden connections between the characters. Isn’t that exciting?

Oh? You just remembered what? Well, I’ll save a goodie bag for you. Those pencils I custom-engraved aren’t going to sharpen themselves.

No! You know what? You’ll be sorry, and when we get that rousing game of Duck-Duck-Goose going, guess what? You’re not invited! And when we bust out a mad pinata, guess who’s not getting a turn? And – you know what – I’m not even letting you step one foot into the petting zoo! Let’s see those little lambsies eat out of your hand from five feet away.

Whatever, Amy. Mom says you’re bad news, anyway – that I should hang out with my real friends, like Spencer and Joey and Brandon. They’re taking a math test right now, but once they get out for recess I’m telling them that this party just got one person smaller and a million billion times cooler.

And that goodie bag I promised you? Goodie luck!
