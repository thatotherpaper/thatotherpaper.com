Weapons of Mass Reduction with Avalanche Jones

22 November 2006 | Avalanche Jones
photo / Kim Heerschop 

It’s 6:56, you pathetic planets of flesh. Four minutes. I’ve had the week from hell, and I’m about to rain all of my frustrations down upon your heads in four minutes. You hear that, Jackson? I see you sweating over there. Sweating before you’ve even mounted the bike. I like that. Tonight is going to be fun. I love Thursday night spinning class.

That’s right, Ms. Adams: Hydrate up. I don’t want you to get overheated. Not after last week’s little episode with Schwartzman in the corner. What’s up, Jay? You feeling better this week, buddy? Good. I’m glad you could join us again. What’s that? You think you had a cold last week? I sure hope you’re over it, pal. I really do. For your sake.

Thirty seconds! Mount your bikes. John, play my tape. The one that says “New Iroc Z Mix.” I’m bringing an old favorite back tonight, folks. This mix really makes me want to ride. I’m ready to crest some peaks tonight, ladies and gentlemen. Ten seconds. John, fast forward through this – everyone’s heard the intro to Stairway to Heaven.

START PEDALING! Here we go, you pear-shaped corporate wastes. It’s too late to fix your seat height, Miller. You should have taken care of that while Pilates was letting out.

We’re climbing – resistance 8. I really love the way Robert Plant’s voice peaks in this chorus. You know what? Fuck it – resistance 9. I want to see those cankles ripple, McAlister. This is my time. I hope you brought your A-game tonight, Ted, because I’d hate for that pretty wife of yours to see you cry. It’s cute that you guys come to the gym together – I’ve got a great view of her in the yoga class right now. Wow, she’s nimble. She’s probably not too fond of seeing her husband weep, though. She might even divorce you over something like that. Shape up or ship out, you flabby disgrace.

Now we’re coasting; pull it back. Just a little, though – I don’t want anyone getting comfortable. Life’s not comfortable, folks, especially when your two useless kids have softball practice three goddamn nights a week. We’re at a 6.

What the hell, Fennerly? Do I come to your workplace and give you half of my ass? I want to see those love handles glisten tonight, bud. You think we, as a society, like seeing your bloated, inactive frame try to squeeze into airplane bathrooms or movie theater seats? If you put as much effort into toning that out-of-control gut as you did finding the ugliest headband on Earth to wear tonight, I probably wouldn’t have heard your son call your ex-wife’s gardener “Daddy” at the mall last weekend. How long have you guys been split up? Two years next month? Step it up, asshole.

Winger’s on, and you know what that means: We’re going mountain climbing. Resistance 10. I’m not seeing that knob turn, Andrews. You woke up early? I swear to Christ, if you don’t turn that knob I will punch you in the throat. You can’t cheat in my realm, you saggy-necked piece of shit. Don’t bother coming back next week if you don’t puke in the water fountain tonight.

I lost my virginity to this song in high school, folks. I only lasted 30 seconds, so let’s pick up the pace. We’re at 12. When’s the last time you saw your penis, Henderson? It’s probably been a while. You’ve been morbidly obese for, what – five years? Six? I bet you didn’t think things would end up like this when you were a kid, huh? It might even be too late to pick up the pieces.

I’m warmed up, people. We’re going to the Himalayas. Resistance 15. I want to see some fucking dedication out of you sedentary calorie banks. Pretend this is buffet night at the Sizzler and move, Harris. I despise each and every one of you lethargic wastes – you know that, right? I hope you never lose weight. I want you here, in my house of pain, week after week. I want you to suffer. Resistance 18.

Did you know that I’m afraid of using urinals? I only use stalls – even to pee. What do you think of that, Daniels? Just a little tidbit for you to chew on. I’m going to make you bleed internally, you overweight computer nerd. Do you think people see a successful software company CEO when they watch you drive around town in your Porsche? I see a double chin and a spare tire. All the money in the world can’t buy you the satisfaction of not having to put extra holes in your belt. Let’s all take it up a notch for Mr. Tech Company. I want to see someone faint tonight.

Anyone else a Poison fan? Sometimes I crank up “Look What the Cat Dragged In” and huff chlorine in my garage. I’m so pumped up right now that I can’t remember my wife’s name. John, can the boombox get any louder? No? Fuck it, I’m going to start singing along.

I skip lunch at work at least once a week and do pushups in the supply closet for an hour. It’s okay, though, because I think Anderson over there generally eats enough for the two of us. Do you know what moderation means, Sam? I’ll give you a hint: It doesn’t involve eating meals in acts. Lunch isn’t supposed to have an intermission. I think we’re all getting bored – resistance 26.

Is that the end of the tape? Fine. Hughes, you’re bleeding from your mouth. If anyone has anything left, I’m sticking around for a while. My kid won some award at school and my wife wants to celebrate. I think I’m going to hit the weights for a few hours; I really want to punish my arms tonight. See you worthless slobs next week.
