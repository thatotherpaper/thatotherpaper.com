TOP picks: April 28 - May 4

28 April 2008 | That Other Paper

Monday | April 28

Grand opening of the United States Art Authority

The United States Art Authority, a mixed media alternative space hosting everything from dance parties to art exhibitions to sketch comedy to screenings, has held several events since its inception – yet they haven’t had an official grand opening. Until today. There will be drink specials and the art of Courtney Miller and Steve Bernal to enjoy.

8pm | FREE

United States Art Authority
510 W 29th • Austin, TX 78705

Tuesday | April 29

Mum w/ Tacks, the Boy Disaster

Tuesday can be boring unless you go see this Icelandic electronic group. Their grasp of blips and beeps combined with the level of creativity you only have as a child come together to make some of the best electronic music to date. Whether you are stressed because of work or upcoming finals, Mum should reconnect you with your childhood dreams of distant worlds and all their possibilities.

8pm | $18

The Parish
214 E 6th St • Austin, TX

Wednesday | April 30

photo / bluerasis Kanye West 

Kanye West w/ Rihanna, N.E.R.D, and Lupe Fiasco

Once again, Kanye shows his genius by being one of the only hip-hop artists to surround himself with artists more talented than himself. Even though his ego is huge, it isn’t too big to let other stars share the stage with him. This is one of the reasons he’s so damn hott right now. Sure, it’s a chunk of change, but you’ll be talking about it for a while.

7pm | $42–$104

Frank Erwin Center
MLK at I-35 • Austin, TX

Thursday | May 1

This American Life host Ira Glass 

This American Life: LIVE!

This American Life is easily the best radio show ever produced. Its stories, which blend documentary with fiction and performance with interview, can be hilarious, touching, and horrifying. In recent years, host Ira Glass has experimented with transforming TAL’s narrative to other media and production techniques: print, live performance, and the incredible This American Life television series on Showtime. But this Thursday, May 1, he’s going to try something completely new: broadcast a live episode via satellite across the United States. Austin is lucky enough to have three participating theaters.

Want more info? Check out the trailer. But buy your tickets soon – they’re already selling out at some locations.

7pm | $20 | Buy tickets online

Participating Austin-area theaters: Regal Metropolitan Stadium 14, Cinemark Southpark Meadows, Cinemark Tinseltown USA Pflugerville

Friday | May 2

The Boxing Lesson, The Unbearables, Alexander’s Dark Band, and Mount Righteous

Yeah, there’s other stuff to do tonight, but you owe it to yourself to go to this show. The Boxing Lesson is Austin’s own synthy psych-poppers, but what seals the deal is the Unbearables – power-pop songs in 5/4 with choir voices and Sufjan orchestration, mostly about zombies. If you’ve never seen them, do it NOW. Mount Righteous is a bunch of kids from Colleyville, TX (home of Fishboy and The Rocket Summer!) who decided to leave Buon Giorno Coffee Shop on Hall-Johnston Rd and start playing Danielson-style gypsy pop (or whatever the hot term for gypsy pop is these days). Also they have a tuba. No idea what Alexander’s Dark Band is but, like, with a name like that – right? To seal the deal, there might be cake served.

10pm | $5

Club de Ville
900 Red River St • Austin, TX 78701

Saturday | May 3

Master Pancake Theater: Planet of the Apes

Damn dirty apes, Owen Egerton, and the Drafthouse combine to make the thing to do tonight. The Drafthouse sums it up best:

This spring, Master Pancake reaches back to the 1960s to mock the Charlton Heston classic Planet of the Apes (1968). Guest comedian Owen Egerton rejoins the cast to help mock the movie in which Charlton Heston plays against type: as a macho, chiseled-face everyman angrily fighting for survival in a world gone mad. Heston plays wayward astronaut George Taylor, who lands on a mystery planet (!?) where apes are in charge and humans are their slaves. Roddy McDowall and Kim Hunter are superb as the simian scientists who help out our hero. Jerry Goldsmith’s ram’s horn score is brilliant and The Twilight Zone’s Rod Serling wrote the script, as evidenced by his signature surprise ending in which Taylor laments the destruction of patriotic statuary and suddenly realizes he’s actually been in New York City the whole time. Featuring Owen Egerton, a Charlton Heston nude scene (!), and more classic quotes involving “damn” than you can shake a monkey stick at, you won’t want to miss Master Pancake’s skewering of this schlocky-but entertaining sci-fi classic!

7; 10:15pm | $25$12.50 | Get tickets

Alamo Drafthouse Ritz
320 E 6th St • Austin, TX

Sunday | May 4

Folded, Torn, Cut, Woven, and Pulled

Today is the last day you can see the Folded, Torn, Cut, Woven, and Pulled exhibition at the Blanton Museum of Art. The art in this exhibition eschew the traditional uses of paper – printing and drawing – and turn paper into a sculptural medium.

From the Blanton Museum website:

With the rise of Minimalism in the 1960s and 1970s, and its focus on both process and materiality, this spirit of experimentation was extended to the paper itself. Folded, torn, cut, woven, and pulled, paper was and continues to be worked as a sculptural medium in its own right.

1pm–5pm| $7 adults, $3 student with ID, free for UT students

Blanton Museum of Art
200 East Martin Luther King, Jr. Blvd. • Austin, TX 78701
