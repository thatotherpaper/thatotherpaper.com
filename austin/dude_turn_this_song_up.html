Dude, turn this song up

28 December 2006 | Eric Seufert and Kyle Cray
photo / Valerie Briscoe 

I’m really cramped back here, man. Can you move your seat up a little? Come on, dude, you don’t need that much leg room. I could have been a dick and called shotgun, but I let you have it because you’re taller. We’ve got like five more hours on this roadtrip – just give me a little more room.

Alright, fine. Want some chips? I’m about to finish the bag, but you can have the last handful if you want. No? More for me, man. These barbeque chips kick ass. They’re really salty, though. Can I have a sip of your soda? Bro, come on. I just want one sip. I’ll take the lid off and sip from the cup. Dude – please. My mouth is dry as hell right now. I seriously want just enough to wet my lips. Whatever, man.

Hey, is this that one song by the band from England? I love this song. I put this on a mix CD for my ex-girlfriend right before we broke up. I think about that time we went to Six Flags when I hear it because we listened to it like 50 times on the way there. It’s almost like we’re not broken up when it’s playing.

Dude, turn this song up.

Please, man. How can you hate this song? Because they’re from England? So are the Arctic Monkeys, and they’re your favorite band. You’re being an asshole. It’s three minutes, man – do me this one favor. I’m sitting with my knees in my chest and my mouth is parched as hell, just do this for me. I let you have fucking shotgun.

Douchebag. And I want that five dollars back now. I was going to forget about it, but you won’t even turn up a song I like. The five dollars for that slice of pizza last weekend. Yeah, I did – you asked me to spot you and then you never gave me my change. Well, I want the money back now. I don’t care if you don’t remember; give me my money. It was on 6th street, dude. How the hell would I be too drunk to remember the facts? I drove down there to pick you guys up.

photo / Danielle Lupkin 

This is the bad ass guitar solo! Dude, turn this song up. I will forget about the five dollars if you turn this up right now. Bro – Bro, please. Fine. You owe me five bucks. I want it right now. Give me my money. Then I want it as soon as we stop at an ATM.

What the fuck – are you wearing my shirt? Where the hell did you get that? Of course it’s my shirt, man. Angela got it for me when she was in Minnesota with her family. How the hell would you have a Mall of America t-shirt? She got you one, too? The exact same shirt? Bullshit. Fine, I will check the tag, dumba–

Shit – mine is a medium. That’s messed up, man. Yeah, dude, it’s messed up that my girlfriend bought you a shirt. Ex-girlfriend, whatever. No, stop it; I don’t want to hear about it. I don’t care, stop telling me. She got you a sweatshirt, too? I’m done talking about this. Just turn this song up.

Please! Dude, give me the ending chorus. That’s all I’m asking for, man. I’ll give you 10 dollars if you turn this song up for the last 30 seconds. Here, take this 10– where’s my wallet? You found it on the floor of that gas station bathroom? Dude, no – Finders, Keepers doesn’t apply to wallets. You have to give me my wallet back. Look, just give me my credit cards and ID; you can keep the money if you turn this song up. You gave my ID to a homeless person? How am I going to get into the concert?

Whatever. Come on, man, give me the last few seconds. Bro. Dude. Bro. I’ll drive the whole way back if you turn this song up. Seriously, you guys can sleep the entire five hours home if you just crank it up for these last few seconds. What – you gave away my seat for the ride back? How am I supposed to get back? A fucking bus? I don’t have any money on me, man. Dude, let me ride with you guys. I’ll squeeze into the trunk. What the hell. That’s not cool.

Don’t even bother turning it up now – the song is over. You’re turning it up if it comes on again. And I want that five dollars.

My mouth is really burning up, man. My tongue feels like a rock. Can I please have some of your soda?

Dude!
