Speed traps for June 11-15

10 June 2007 | Todd Ross Nienkerk

The following is a list of speed traps (“traffic enforcement locations”) around Austin for the week of June 11–15, 2007.

The Austin Police Department sez: Traffic safety is a major focus of the Austin Police Department, and a substantial amount of resources is committed to traffic safety improvements and traffic enforcement. Traffic enforcement is a primary function of APD motor units and one of the main functions of patrol officers. In addition, APD schedules officers each week on overtime, funded by grants, to enforce traffic laws. The goal of traffic enforcement is to increase citizens’ voluntary compliance with traffic laws.

Monday, June 11

  • Morning: 2400 to 2500 blocks of Red River Street
  • Morning: 3000 block of East 12th Street
  • Morning: Oltorf Street
  • Afternoon: 7900 block of San Felipe Boulevard
  • Afternoon: 4600 block of East William Cannon Drive

Tuesday, June 12

  • Morning: 2600 block of East Martin Luther King Boulevard
  • Morning: 12300 block of Riata Trace Parkway
  • Morning: 1700 block of West Cesar Chavez Street
  • Morning: 7200 block of South First Street
  • Afternoon: 8000 to 9500 blocks of Highway 290 East

Wednesday, June 13

  • Morning: Barton Springs Road
  • Morning: 7500 block of Hart Lane
  • Morning: 100 to 5100 blocks of North I-35
  • Afternoon: Cameron Road and Briarcliff Boulevard
  • Afternoon: 700 block of West Stassney Lane

Thursday, June 14

  • Morning: 5900 to 7500 blocks of Cameron Road
  • Morning: East Martin Luther King Boulevard from I-35 to Ed Bluestein Boulevard
  • Afternoon: 7000 to 13200 blocks of South I-35
  • Afternoon: West Gate Boulevard
  • Afternoon: 7300 block of FM 2222

Friday, June 15

  • Morning: 1800 block of West 5th Street
  • Morning: 7200 block of South First Street
  • Morning: 100 to 1000 blocks of East Riverside Drive
  • Afternoon: 6800 to 7200 blocks of Manor Road
  • Afternoon: Southwest Parkway

Traffic tip from the APD: Stop for flashing red lights on a school bus, regardless of which direction you are driving. Continue your trip once the bus has moved, the flashing lights stop flashing or the bus driver signals that it is okay to pass. Violations can lead to a $1,000 fine.

Courtesy Austin Police Department. Released June 8, 2007. Photo by JimmJamm77.

 

APD offers FREE VIN etching!

The Austin Police Department will etch your vehicle identification number (VIN) on for FREE at various locations around Austin through June and July. Here’s the schedule:

  • June 23 Target at 11220 FM 2222, 10am to 1pm
  • July 11 Wal-Mart at 9300 S. I-35, 10am to 1pm
  • July 21 Dolores Catholic Church (1111 Montopolis Drive), 10am to 2pm
  • July 26 Target at 5621 N. I-35, 10am to 1pm

More details can be found on Austin’s ONLY Blog.
