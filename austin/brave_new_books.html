The truth is in here

8 June 2007 | Michael Joseph
photo / Veronica Hansen Brave New Books 

In August 2006, a new kind of bookstore opened for business on Guadalupe. Literally and figuratively underground, the space that is now Brave New Books used to serve as Kinky Friedman’s campaign headquarters. To enter, you walk down a flight of stairs from the street. It’s a fitting location for the brainchild of Harlan Dietrich.

Inside the bookstore, you notice first the sheer volume of material crammed inside the small space. Bookshelves circle the entire room from floor to ceiling. The books cover a wide array of subjects: The 9/11 attacks are featured perhaps most prominently, but other conspiracy theories involving secret societies, the medical industry, and even JFK can be found.

Several flat screen monitors throughout the store continuously play documentaries – customers are encouraged to watch as long as they want. To Dietrich, Brave New Books isn’t a business venture designed to make money. He considers it a mission to spread suppressed information and expose people to what he believes are government lies.

When most people hear the name Osama bin Laden, they think of the world’s most infamous terrorist, Al Qaeda, and 9/11. But Dietrich is on a mission to change that. He’s trying to convince people that 9/11 was actually part of an intricate and sinister plan orchestrated by our own government, conceived to scare the American public in order to help create a police state that engages in perpetual and unnecessary war.

Dietrich’s involvement in the 9/11 Truth Movement began years ago. He started studying the official story of 9/11 almost immediately following the attacks when he saw local filmmaker and premier Truth Movement advocate Alex Jones on cable access. Over the next few years, Dietrich met more and more like-minded people, eventually attending the first 9/11 Truth Conference in Chicago. There he had the idea of opening a store to disseminate information on the movement.

“We’re in business to go out of business,” explains Dietrich. “Our goal is to popularize this information to such a point that it eliminates the need to have a bookstore specializing in suppressed information.”

Thus, Brave New Books focuses on educating customers as much as selling them products. Everyone who walks into the store for the first time is likely to walk out with a free DVD of some kind and as many informational pamphlets as they can carry.

The store also holds special screenings several times a week. Customers watch the documentaries in a large screening room in the back of the store, and a discussion session typically follows. They feature programs discussing 9/11, civil liberties, deception in school textbooks, the genetic engineering of animals, vaccine education, and much more.

“It really does crack the egg,” explains Dietrich. “When people realize what has been told to them is a complete fiction, a complete myth, that sort of destroys all preconceived notions that they have about everything else.”

While the store does carry a lot of books detailing shocking and outlandish conspiracy theories, there are some authors that Dietrich won’t endorse.

photo / Veronica Hansen Brave New Books 

“We try to vet information. We try to put things on the shelf that are the most trustworthy,” says Dietrich. “As you walk through the store, you can see we have no UFO books. We try and stay away from anything that involves a Jewish conspiracy or a Catholic Jesuit conspiracy.”

They reject some books on 9/11 as well, including one proposing that energy beams from space destroyed the World Trade Center.

Despite Brave New Books’ initial success, Dietrich realizes widespread acceptance of the 9/11 Truth Movement is not something that will happen in the immediate future. However, he does remain optimistic.

“You don’t have to move the rock, you just have to push it. That is what we are doing. We’re pushing the rock.”
