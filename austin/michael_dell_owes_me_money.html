Michael Dell owes me money

11 October 2007 | Dr. Dan Jerbrowski
photo / yngrich 

Michael Dell owes me some money.

Let me explain: Last Wednesday my day got off to a rough start. First my debit card bounced in front of a line full of people at the Dairy Queen, and the employee decided to further degrade me by nearly tossing my delicious treat into the garbage right in front of me. Then my dog refused to eat the can of green beans I poured into his bowl when I got home (since I was out of dog food), and then he pissed on the kitchen floor to tell me what he thought about my decision. Yeah, that hurt too.

In spite of all that, I had one thing going for me: I could still steal Internet from my neighbor across the way. Let’s just say I was beyond excited when the connection actually went through. Things were really picking up, I decided. Then I saw the top story on Yahoo.

Now, I hope I’m not alone here in wanting to go out and choke a politician after reading the 25th installment of the Forbes 400, which innocently joked that $1 billion “is no longer enough.” I’ll go on the record as saying that I’m on the opposite side of statements like that. Hell, $14 is enough if I can get drunk on it. But apparently there are 400 people out there that have 1.3 billion, or more, dollars.

photo / edans 

Now, okay – I can deal with that, I guess. It isn’t too much of a surprise. I’m not naive or anything like that. But for reasons I’ll get to in a moment I should mention that up to this point I’ve owned a Dell computer. I say up to this point, because I threw that fucking piece of shit against the wall last Wednesday, but I’ll get to that too if you’ll just hold your goddamn horses. And I should also mention that I think Dell makes really, really shitty computers to begin with, which you already know if you own one.

So I’m reading the list, sort of pissed off and weepy all at the same time, you know, because it sucks but it also hurts my feelings when I get to number eight on the list. Guess whose picture I saw there, smiling all smug between his big-ass ears, looking like he just had his balls licked or something?

Michael Dell himself, the eighth richest man in America. Let me get into a few details, in case any of you haven’t keeled over and puked on yourselves yet. Michael Dell has $17.2 billion in assets.

It gets worse. Right at the pinnacle moment of my anger, I mean when I was ready to strip down naked and jump up and down screaming, “Fuck! Fuck! Fuck!” or “Cocksucker!” or something like that, because just looking at that scoundrel infuriates me so much, my computer just DIED.

I’m not talking about accidentally turned off, or that it had an error that caused it to reboot, or that it just sort of farted for a minute. I’m talking about high-pitched whines and weird mechanical choking, a sound like the Game Over sound on Nintendo, smoke coming out of the intake vent. It was like a prairie dog trying to shit out a softball. I’m talking about dead like my neighbor’s dog under my back tire last week in the parking lot.

Maybe throwing the computer at the wall wasn’t the smartest thing I’ve ever done. Actually, definitely not – it put a dent in my wall that the property manager’s gonna be pissed about, trust me, and now I don’t have a computer. But it felt good. And it got me thinking. That motherfucker owes me. I’m sitting there, drinking screwdrivers mixed with Skol at two in the afternoon, definitely not as worried about paying my rent as I should be, and this guy’s out showing off like everything’s wonderful, smiling like the joker because it’s practically a fucking joke it’s so lame how much money he has. Well, not for me, Mr. Dell. I don’t have $17 billion. My life’s really pretty shitty when you get down to it.

photo / Charlie Brewer 

I actually pictured Michael Dell sitting in his Antichrist-sized mansion, dancing in circles, laughing at what a sucker I am. It’s not enough that I get dealt cards like lazy, does not work well with others, and has no talent at anything. I play them pretty well, all things considered. But Michael Dell gets to make billions of dollars by putting computers together with construction paper and Elmer’s glue, and then selling them to mental midgets everywhere (myself included). That really pisses me off.

Which is what led to my decision to personally write a letter to each of the 400 richest people in America. Right now I’ve only gotten to, like, number four or so, and even that isn’t much progress since I skipped Mark Cuban (I can’t help it, I just like that guy). But it’ll happen, I think.

I was thinking about sending the Walton heirs matching sets of animal carcasses, along with a few for the subprime lending guys who’ve slithered up there to the top, but we all know that probably won’t happen. Not because I’m unwilling to choke out a cat or two for justice, but mostly because it’s just too much work. But here’s the point – actually, no. I take that back. I don’t really have a point. I’m just really, really full of hate. It’s a problem, I admit it, so get off my back. Who’s gonna help me anyway? Michael Dell? That bastard hasn’t even returned my phone calls yet.
