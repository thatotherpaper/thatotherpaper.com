Jo Barrett: Stallflower

10 April 2007 | Kristin Hillery
512-Go!» Book People
Related links
  • Jo Barrett
  • Jo Barrett’s blog

Some women go to the bathroom to gossip, some go in to cry after getting broken up with in public, others just go in to touch up their make-up. And author Jo Barrett writes about all of it in her new book The Men’s Guide to the Women’s Bathroom. A University of Texas at Austin grad and former lawyer, Barrett will be at BookPeople this Wednesday, April 11 at 7pm.

That Other Paper How’d you get the idea for the The Men’s Guide to the Women’s Bathroom, and what actually motivated you to do the research, write it, and get it published?

courtesy Jo Barrett 

Jo Barrett Well, I’m from Texas originally, but when I moved back here from New York City, I started going out with a bunch of women to enjoy Austin. We’d go out to restaurants, see live music – and I realized that a lot of our conversations and a lot of action that was happening got redirected into the women’s restroom and that there was a lot of bonding and community and fun in the women’s bathroom. It was almost like another party was happening in there. And I thought, men are always asking the question, you know, “What are women doing in the bathroom? Why do they always go in pairs? What takes us so long in there?” And I think a lot of it is that the women’s bathroom is sort of this place – it’s like our salon, our chit-chat place, it’s like a secret confessional. So I think it provides a quick retreat, like a fast sanctuary for women to get together and quickly bond, you know? So I just started thinking about women’s bathrooms, and then I started interviewing a bunch of women in Austin and then in Texas and then I did it internationally as well to see if women were all having the same experiences in bathrooms. It seems like predominantly it’s true that women feel a sense of community in the bathroom, that they share a lot of pearls of wisdom and things like that. And the book is really popular I guess in Los Angeles – I sold it to CBS – because every network is looking for the next Sex and the City. They said, “Well, it seems like women’s bathrooms are this place where women go, and it’s never been talked about.” It’s an entirely different experience, and of course what men experience is that it’s not a “clubby” atmosphere there – there’s no furniture, and they go individually.

TOP Since the book’s release last month, have you felt like you’re some kind of pro every time you’ve used a public restroom?

JB I was in some public restrooms on the day the book came out, and I even want to sign books in the bathroom – I want girls to come in the bathroom to get their book signed at the book signings. It’s just really funny – my friends keep saying, “You have a law degree from Georgetown and you have a PhD in bathrooms, so you’re like a resident bathroomologist.”

TOP Speaking of: You were a lawyer for a while, and then you moved to New York to become a writer. I was wondering, was law school something you did because you actually wanted to be a lawyer, or was it one of those “What am I gonna do? I guess I’ll go to law school…” decisions?

courtesy Jo Barrett Jo Barrett 

JB I went to law school because I got a job on Capitol Hill in D.C., so I was working for a member of Congress. I thought it’d be great to go to law school and be a part of this. I did an evening program at Georgetown while I was working during the day on the Hill – which was tough, but I got through it. After I left the Hill, I just decided to start writing books. So I’m really happy about this novel. This book is really a love letter to Austin; it’s a love letter to Texas. I name over 56 local places in Austin in the novel. I’m just glad – Austin’s becoming so big right now. Everyone’s so interested in our city, and I’m glad the book features the city.

TOP Is it still going to take place in Austin when it gets picked up? I guess it’s being made into a movie or a TV show?

JB Yeah, they’re looking at both TV and movies. They were thinking it would make a great TV show, because they could see it doing something each week where it ended up in the women’s bathroom. I’ve even had people interview me and they’ve said, “The first time I ever had a one-night stand was in a public bathroom in a youth hostel in Barcelona.” I thought that was kind of funny, because so many women have different bathroom stories. For example, every woman I know has either been in a bathroom crying after someone broke up with her in a public place, or after she saw an ex show up somewhere with a new girl or something like that.

TOP Awkward. Well, what’s the worst women’s bathroom situation: No mirror or no toilet paper?

JB I think most women would say no toilet paper, with no mirror to be the follow-up second. But definitely no toilet paper. And especially if you don’t discover it until after you’re already… you know.

TOP I found this quote on your website, and I thought it was funny. I was wondering if you could explain: “If Andrew Lloyd Webber had set foot in a women’s bathroom, we wouldn’t have had to endure two decades of Cats.”

JB I know! That’s because there’s a lot of drama in the women’s bathroom sometimes. I mean, you’ve probably been out to bathrooms in different clubs or something and you go in and there’s some kind of drama scene going on. It’s great for eavesdropping too, and just hearing all these stories. The stories write themselves. Real life is the best story of all. So that’s what I meant – Andrew Lloyd Webber could have done a whole Broadway show with women in a bathroom at a busy club or something.

courtesy Jo Barrett Jo Barrett 

TOP Okay, I need to know the best overall bathroom, the best bathroom for gossip, and the dirtiest bathroom – out of all the bathrooms in Austin, of course.

JB The dirtiest bathroom in Austin? I’m going to get slammed for this.

TOP I would say Emo’s.

JB I think Emo’s is definitely up there. You mean as far as physically dirtiest? I think Little Woodrow’s can get lots of wet toilet paper on the floor – you need a hazmat team. As far as prettiest, well, the Four Seasons has a really nice bathroom. The bathroom at Mars is pretty cool looking, too.

 
See Jo Barrett at BookPeople Jo Barrett will be at BookPeople (603 N Lamar Blvd) this Wednesday, April 11 at 7pm to talk about The Men’s Guide to the Women’s Bathroom and to sign copies of the novel.
