This lane is intended for 10 items or less

13 December 2006 | Eric Seufert and John Atley
photo / Redjar 

Wow, you’re buying an awful lot of organic tomatoes! It’s great to see a fellow shopper who loves tomatoes as much as I do. Truth be told, I’m kind of a tomato nut. In fact, I’m planning on working on my tomato garden as soon as I get home.

You’ve got quite a few jars of dried apricots there, too. And I notice you’ve got six cartons of free-range chicken eggs in your cart. Actually, you have a lot of things in your cart – organic asparagus, maize grown on an Indian reservation, hand-flattened tortillas. I appreciate you supporting this locally-owned co-op, but I think you may be using the wrong checkout lane.

You see, I’m not trying to be rude, but this is the 10 Items or Less line.

Oh, don’t worry about it. I was just pointing that out. I’m in no hurry – this is a leisurely Saturday at the co-op for me. My only plans for the rest of the day are to tend to my garden and read the Iraq Study Group report. Man, what a mess that situation has become.

What is that – eight bushels of carrots harvested from a federal land grant in Appalachia? Not that I’m really bothered by it, but you’ve already had 22 items scanned and your cart’s only half empty. The 10 Items or Less sign is pretty big; I’m not quite sure how you missed it. No, I’m not asking you to leave the line – I just wouldn’t want you to make the same mistake again.

How about this weather? I may have to take a rain check on those tomatoes and head out to Zilker this afternoon! I love tossing the ol’ disc around with friends while tipping back a few Sierra Nevadas. If I ever get out of here, that is.

What? Oh, don’t mind me; I’m just having a laugh. I really don’t mind that you’ve already rung up – what’s the register say? Is that a two? – 32 items. Like I said, this is just a relaxed Saturday for me. I’m in no rush at all.

photo / Redjar 

There was only one car in the parking lot – it must have been yours. Now, is that a Range Rover or a Land Rover? A Range Rover? What’s the difference? Ah, I see – that’s quite a bit more expensive. Plus, you know, all the money you must spend on gas.

I rode my bike here. Yeah, I put my groceries in this hemp sack and just stick it in the basket on the back. You don’t bring your own sacks? I suppose the store does provide paper bags for customers. And that way you don’t even have to wash or store them, you can just throw them away. How convenient.

Who is this? Your wife? Oh, sure, I’m completely fine with her throwing a few more things into your cart. Pile ’em on there. I mean, a trip to the grocery store isn’t successful unless you come home with eight pounds of oysters that were shucked by asylum-seekers from Sudan. I can wait a few more minutes in line for the sake of making a statement against the situation in Darfur. No, don’t apologize – I should be thanking you. I came to this co-op to buy a granola bar and a bottle of milk, and here I am condemning crimes against humanity. I feel like George Clooney.

Look at that: The sun is setting. Nothing beats a Central Texas sunset. I love the way it settles behind the skyline and seems to hang there for a few minutes, like it doesn’t want to say goodbye. It’s like the moon is contending for its chance to watch over Texa–

Okay, what the fuck. Are you seriously writing a check? You just rang up 57 items, and now I have to wait for your inconsiderate ass to fill out a goddamn Snoopy check? You’re a real piece of work. Nice handwriting, too. I like those big curves on your Ds. Are you going to dot your Is with hearts?

Yeah, don’t forget to fill out what this check is for. Good move. I’d be confused, too, if I saw a $158 check for 10 or less grocery items. I tell you what: Why don’t I come watch you get your oil changed or your water heater replaced? I mean, my time is worthless, right?

You have got to be fucking kidding me. No, no, I don’t mind – run out to your car to get your driver’s license. I have absolutely no problem with that. Take your time. I was actually just telling my wife how I wish my two great loves, riding my bike on Lamar in the dark and being held up by yuppie assholes at the grocery store, could be combined into one festival of shit. I guess today is my lucky day.

Take long? No, absolutely not. That wasn’t long at all. I’m sure whatever phone call you took on the way back inside was life-or-death. Your niece won third place at the swim meet, you say? Well, I guess you have a reason to open that bottle of wine produced at a vineyard employing reintegrated ex-convicts tonight. Any reason to celebrate, right?

Hey, don’t worry about it. I enjoyed watching your endless stream of groceries pass over the infrared scanner. I love hearing the sound that machine makes when it catches a UPC label – I’ve been trying to find it as a ringtone. And I think that 10 item rule is more of a guideline than a hard-and-fast limit. Enjoy that wine.

Great. It’s starting to rain.
