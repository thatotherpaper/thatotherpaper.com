TOP picks: August 13-19

13 August 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | August 13

The Way Things Always Happen Here 

Kevin Stewart and John Hennessy

Having both lived in Austin at different times in their lives, it makes sense that Kevin Stewart and John Hennessy chose Book People as the forum to read from and discuss their new works tonight. Stewart won the Texas Review Novella Prize in 1999 for his novella Margot, and has been compared to such big hitters as Larry McMurtry, Andre Dubus III, and Breece D’J Pancake. Hennessy just recently published his first book of poems, Bridge and Tunnel, to great critical acclaim. Stewart likewise has a new collection out, The Way Things Happen Here, a book of short stories chronicling various characters and their struggle with the places they come from.

7pm | FREE

Bookpeople
603 N Lamar Blvd • 78703

Tuesday | August 14

photo / The Anti-ZIM 

SegCity Tour

You’ve seen Segways around town (you know, those weird scooter things with two wheels). They’re not just for tourists – take a Segway tour and see Austin through new eyes. The weather’s been great lately, so there’s no better time to do it.

Call 402-9299 to make a reservation | $65

SegCity
621 E Sixth St • 402-9299

Wednesday | August 15

photo / themelkerina 

Eisley at Waterloo Records

You saw Spoon for free at Waterloo last month – now go see the Tyler-based indie-poppers Eisley. Best part: Free.

5pm | FREE

Waterloo Records
600 N Lamar Blvd • Austin, TX 78703

Thursday | August 16

The Sickest F****ing Stories I Ever Heard

Full disclosure: We’re proudly sponsoring this show. The title really says it all. You have a table populated by drinking, poker-playing comedians telling the most disturbing, funny stories they know. Not for the faint of heart, but nothing exhilarating in life ever is. Your ticket affords you one free drink. This show features John Rabon (Funniest Person in Austin finalist), Tiffany Janssen (burlesque performer with The Jigglewatts), Matthew Odam (writer/columnist), and Chris Trew (improviser/nerdcore rapper). Asaf Ronen hosts and deals the cards.

More info

9pm | $10 (includes drink ticket)

512-Go!» The Hideout
617 Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78701

Friday | August 17

photo / joshua 

Victory Grill’s 60th anniversary

To celebrate 60 years of Victory in Austin, Victory Grill will host a dance party featuring five DJs (Little Danny, Greg Most, Dr. Rhythm, Brett “Pointman” Koshkin, and Emcee Obatallah) throughout the night. They’ll be spinning the rarest, most hardcore funk, soul, and boogaloo they can find. Come prepared to get deep in the pocket.

9pm | $5

Victory Grill
1104 E 11th St • 512-5125

Saturday | August 18

photo / Samuel Eichner 

First Annual Austin Ice Cream Festival

The little kid in you should be absolutely flipping out about this. It’s a whole festival dedicated to ice cream, and not only is this event delicious, it’s helpful, too – it’ll help raise money for the Austin Children’s Shelter, La Leche, and Habibs Hutch. With live music, contests, games, and plenty to eat, this summer festival should have something for everyone.

More info

10am–7pm | FREE admission. Ice cream eating contest is free for the first 100 people who are 18 years old and older. Ice cream making contest is $50 per contestant. Popsicle stick building contest is free for all ages.

Waterloo Park
1301 Trinity St • Austin, TX

Sunday | August 19

photo / oskay 

City Wide Garage Sale

For 28 years Austin has held a city wide garage sale, and this year is no different. There’s no telling what treasures you’ll find among the thousands of antiques, collectibles, and vintage items for sale. Note that this event runs on both the 18th and the 19th.

11am–5pm | FREE for kids under 12; $5 everyone else

Palmer Events Center
900 Barton Springs Rd • Austin, TX
