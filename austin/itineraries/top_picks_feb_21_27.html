TOP picks: Feb 21-27

20 February 2007 | That Other Paper

Wednesday | February 21

photo / Brett L. 

Quiz Night

B.D. Riley’s Irish Pub has been running pop-trivia contests each Wednesday since they opened a few years ago. Individuals can compete, but most people form teams (of any size) to play in two rounds of 25 questions on a wide variety of topics like sports, movies, history, and local news. The grand prize winner gets a $100 B.D. Riley’s gift certificate.

Quiz Night has a strong following, so tables become scarce by 7:30. There’s no cover, and you can park free at Littlefield Garage if you get your ticket validated at B.D. Riley’s.

8pm | FREE

512-Go!» B.D. Riley’s Irish Pub
204 E Sixth St • 512.494.1335

Thursday | February 22

image courtesy of marcmaron.com 

Marc Maron, with Lucas Molandez

Marc Maron is set to bring his personal brand of angry, neurotic comedy to Cap City for five nights this week. As heard on Air America Radio and seen on Short Attention Span Theater and Tough Crowd with Colin Quinn, Marc’s biting, hard-edged honesty is sharp enough to make you laugh at life’s harshest realities.

Feb 20–24 | $9 | see times and buy tix here

Check out That Other Paper’s interview with Marc Maron.

512-Go!» Cap City Comedy Club
8120 Research Blvd • 512.467.2333

Friday | February 23

comic courtesy of davidhuntsberger.com 

David Huntsberger, with Howard Beecher and Matt Kordelski

He’s a cartoonist, a former substitute teacher, and a comedian, and tonight he’s headlining at The Velveeta Room. You may know David Huntsberger as one of the founders of The Hooligan Show, or maybe you’ve seen him on Comedy Central’s Premium Blend. Regardless, with Howard Beecher and Matt Kordelski opening, this show’s bound to be a good time.

9:30pm and 11:30pm | $5

512-Go!» The Velveeta Room
521 E Sixth St • 512.469.9116

Saturday | February 24

photo / Asylum Street Spankers’ website 

Asylum Street Spankers album release party

Family pick “God’s Favorite Band” will be celebrating the release of their latest album Mommy Says No! not once, but twice this Saturday. According to KUT, their latest release is “a family-style set of songs that takes a look at childhood from both kid and adult perspectives.” Though these are intended to be family-friendly performances, Asylum Street Spankers shows are always worthwhile – especially at Ruta Maya. (Hopefully the kids won’t unwittingly wander across the parking lot and into the Exposé Men’s Club.)

Asylum Street Spankers’ official website

4pm (kids) 8:30pm (all ages) | $4

512-Go!» Ruta Maya Coffee House
3601 S Congress Ave • 512.707.9637

photo / myspace.com/jigglewatts 

A Pocketful of Murder: A Burlesque Noir

The lives of an heiress, a songbird who hides her past, a speed demoness, and a kleptomaniac converge thanks to a mysterious man with sinister plans for them all. Song, dance, daring, and darkness! Make sure you don’t miss your pocketful of The Jigglewatts Risque Cabaret’s burlesque noir.

Jigglewatts on MySpace

9pm | $15

512-Go!» The Hideout Theatre
617 Congress Ave • 512.443.3688

Sunday | February 25

photo / Joe Shlabotnik 

Alamo Drafthouse’s 9th Annual Red Carpet Bash!

Never ones to not fix it if it isn’t broken, the Alamo Drafthouse is expanding and further extravaganza-ing its annual Academy Awards viewing event this year. Not only will they have prizes and drink specials, they’ll also have special events during those tedious commercial breaks. Hosted by Drafthouse staple Owen Egerton and his equally hilarious wife Jodi, this event is sure to have you rolling on the red carpet.

6pm | FREE, but you can reserve your seat with a $12 voucher that buys you champagne, a copy of the Razzie award winners, and an Alamo Academy Award raffle ticket

512-Go!» Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
409 Colorado St • 512.476.1320

Monday | February 26

It’s magical… 

Wonderwall, in celebration of George Harrison’s birthday

Director Joe Massot singled out George Harrison to create the soundtrack for his 1968 film experiment. Harrison responded that he had never done a soundtrack before and didn’t know how. Massot insisted that whatever he came up with would work. The movie is about a man who becomes obsessed with his striking model neighbor, Penny Lane. Harrison’s end product was a trippy instrumental score for a psychedelic, voyeuristic fantasy. Here’s a bit of Beatle’s trivia, the soundtrack, entitled Wonderwall Music is also the first Beatles solo album.

Wonderwall on IMDB.com

9:45pm | $2; $1 with student ID

512-Go!» Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
409 Colorado St • 512.476.1320

Tuesday | February 27

photo / Scion Live Metro Tour 

Scion Live Metro Tour, featuring Big Daddy Kane

“Hey, I’m Scion, and I am trying as hard as I can to appeal to my target demographic!” You know what, Scion? That’s totally cool with us. Just keep setting up these awesome free monthly shows, and we won’t complain. Promise. So this month’s event features Big Daddy Kane, Connie Price and The Keystones, and a DJ set by J.Period. All this entertainment for the low price of $0 means you’d better show up early to guarantee a spot inside – and don’t forget to RSVP.

9pm | FREE | RSVP here

512-Go!» austin/cityguide/the_parish_room
214 E Sixth St • 512.479.0474
