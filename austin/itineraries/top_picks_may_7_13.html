TOP picks: May 7-13

6 May 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | May 7

Photo / Ed Schipul 

Texas 42 Night at Ginny’s Little Longhorn Saloon

Grab a beer and a partner and play some Texas 42, a game that was created by two kids in Texas over 100 years ago. Their conservative Southern Baptist sect forbade them from playing cards, so they totally stuck it to the man and created this domino game, which is sometimes called “the national game of Texas.”

Ginny’s website

7pm | FREE

Ginny’s Little Longhorn Saloon
5434 Burnet Rd • 458-1813

Tuesday | May 8

Photo courtesy of myspace.com/gamesanddames 

Games & Dames

Redrum and Varietease Burlesque Co. put on this monthly event, which pairs two classic forms of entertainment: burlesque and gambling. This week’s version features visiting burlesque talent from as far away as Hollywood and a variety of acts from contortionists to striptease to something called The Banana Dance. There’ll also be live music from Heather Rea and the Moonshine Boys, plus poker and blackjack until 2am. Everybody gets $100 in gaming chips just for showing up. It’s pretty much all the fun of the old West, except you also get indoor plumbing and A/C.

8pm | FREE

Redrum
401 Sabine St • 78701

Wednesday | May 9

Photo / Nika 

Bat cruise on The Little Star

Sure, bat-watching is a big tourist attraction, but it’s still pretty cool – especially when you’re watching from a boat on Town Lake. For under $10 a person, this one-hour cruise is the ultimate way to watch those little guys. Cruisers over 21 are welcome to bring their drink(s) of choice.

30 min. before sunsetpm | $8 adults, $5 kids under 12, $6 seniors

For reservations, call 327-1388. Visit their website for info on how to get there and where to park.

Thursday | May 10

photo courtesy austinparks.org 

Movies in the Park: Election

This classic, starring Matthew Broderick as a high school history teacher going through a mid-life crisis, will be playing in Republic Square Park tonight. Picnics, chairs, and pets are welcome – but no booze is permitted. Bring cash if you want to buy snacks and drinks from Dog Almighty, Ben and Jerry’s, or Maine Root Root Beer.

Movies in the Park website

7:30pm | FREE

Republic Square Park
5th & Guadalupe • Austin, TX

Friday | May 11

George Clinton and the Parliament Funkadelic

The King of Interplanetary Funksmanship jams on the outdoor stage at Stubb’s tonight.

Doors 7pm | $25 | Buy tickets

Stubb’s Bar-B-Q (outdoor stage)
8th & Red River • 78701

Saturday | May 12

Supaidaman, 1978 

Japanese Spiderman w/ foley artist Fred Newman

Foleyvision has returned to the Alamo Drafthouse! How it works: Sounds made live by a team of people in the theater replace the movie’s soundtrack, which will be muted. And this show’s not only special because it’s the Japanese Spiderman (Supaidaman, who has a spaceship that turns into a robot!), but also because sound effects master Fred Newman will be lending his talents to the event. Newman’s credits include sound work on the Prairie Home Companion radio show, Men in Black, and Gremlins.

7 & 9:45pm | $8.50 (students); $10 (adults) | Buy tickets

512-Go!» Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
409 Colorado St. • 476-1320

Sunday | May 13

Photo / goforchris 

Mother’s Day Concert in the Park

Mom in town for Mother’s Day? Pack a lunch and a blanket and take her out for a great picnic in Zilker Park. The Austin Symphonic Band will be playing a free concert at the Zilker Hillside Theater.

Austin Symphonic Band’s website

7pm | Free

Zilker Hillside Theater, Zilker Park
2206 William Barton Drive • 78704
