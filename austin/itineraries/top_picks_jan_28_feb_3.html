TOP picks: Jan 28 - Feb 3

28 January 2008 | That Other Paper

Monday | January 28

City of Austin’s People’s Gallery 2007

photo / Todd Ross Nienkerk Detail from “Austin” by Mark Prellop 

Every year, the city of Austin issues a call to the public for works of art. The result is the People’s Gallery, a collection of hundreds of pieces of art created by your fellow Austinites. The purpose of the People’s Gallery is “to showcase regional artistic endeavors and to encourage public dialogue, understanding, and enjoyment of visual art,” or, in other words, to show off how talented Austin is. You only have a few more days to check out the 2007 gallery – it’s closing on January 31st to make way for the 2008 gallery, which opens February 22.

8am–5pm | FREE

Austin City Hall
301 W 2nd St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Monday Blues at TC’s Lounge | 9:30pm
  • Chris Gage at Donn’s Depot | 9pm

Tuesday | January 29

Air Guitar Championships

Competing in the Air Guitar Championships is actually quite an opportunity. Between January and May – on the last Tuesday of each month – the Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz will be having preliminary Air Guitar Competition rounds. The three best air shredders will have the option of competing in the Austin Air Guitar Finals. The winner of that competition wins $100 and goes to New York where the National Air Guitar Competition will take place. The winner of that competition will be flown to Finland to represent the United States in the Air Guitar World Championship.

Participants also receive four free Alamo Drafthouse tickets. If you want to participate bring a CD with your 60 second air guitar track the night of the competition, or email the track in advance to brad@originalalamo.com. Wear a costume. Something CRAZY.

You’ll compete for 60 seconds and then be judged on a scale of 4.0–6.0 – like in figure skating.

9:45pm | $5

Alamo Ritz
320 E 6th St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • This Will Destroy You in-store performance (free beer) at Waterloo Records | 5pm
  • Dale Watson and His Lone Stars at The Belmont | 7pm

Wednesday | January 30

i google myself

i google myself 

Everyone’s done it – Googled their own name, that is. i google myself is the story of three very different men with the same name brought together by narcissistic Google searches of themselves. One’s a stalker, one’s a porn star, and one’s a stoner mechanic, and “what starts as a curiosity turns into an obsession that makes these three men’s worlds violently collide.”

9:15pm

Blue Theater
916 Springdale Rd • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Whiskey Wednesday at The Mohawk | 5pm
  • Jeff Klein at Stubb’s | 9pm

Thursday | January 31

Garden District

Garden District is an awesome two-for-one deal – the production consists of two one-act plays by Tennessee Williams. There’s Suddenly Last Summer, which “concerns the mysterious death of Sebastian Venable, the sordid truth that could destroy his reputation, and the lengths to which his mother will go to keep it secret forever,” and Something Unspoken, the companion piece to Suddenly Last Summer that “reveals the deeply buried feelings behind the facades of a wealthy Southern grand dame and the mousy widow who has served as her secretary for 15 years.” The cast of Garden District is top notch, featuring five Austin Circle of Theaters award winning actresses.

8pm | $15–$30

Austin Playhouse at Penn Field
3601 S Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • The Bangles at La Zona Rosa | 8pm
  • Maximalist at Else Madsen Gallery | 10am–5pm

Friday | February 1

Dudley and Bob’s Pleasurefest

With a $100 cash prize giveaway, porn stars, lingerie modeling, booths selling unconventional products, performances by the JiggleWatts, Kitty Kitty Bang Bang, and The Southern Sirens (voted best in show at the 2007 Texas Burlesque Festival) – this will probably be an interesting evening. Sex, money, drinks, and humor.

Doors at 7pm | $5

La Zona Rosa
612 W 4 St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • AM Syndicate at The Mohawk | 10pm
  • The Refugee All Stars at Flamingo Cantina | 10pm

Saturday | February 2

Super Furry Animals w/ Holy Fuck

These are two very interesting bands. Super Furry Animals is a Welsh band that’s been around since 1994. They play rock that leans toward psychedelia and electronic experimentation. They’ve come out with eight studio albums and have a solid following. Their companion band, Holy Fuck, is a super evil band out of Toronto. They do lo-fi improvisation that emulates electronic music without using any of the basic techniques common to the electronica genre – like sampling, looping, programing and computers. Rather, they use keyboards, bass, drums, and found instruments like toy ray-guns to achieve their unique sound. You can get your tickets to see both bands for under $20.

8pm | $13

Emo’s
603 Red River • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • 31st annual Carnival Brasileiro at Palmer Event Center | 8pm
  • Groundwork Music Orchestra at Ruta Maya World HQ | 4pm

Sunday | February 3

Spike and Mike’s Sick and Twisted Festival of Animation

It’s that time of year again – time for the annual animation festival that’s become an Austin tradition. Animated shorts and toons at Spike and Mike’s are never a letdown for audience members who’ve come to be grossed out, shocked, and made to laugh. From the promoter:

The 2008 collection is filled with so much that is so wrong that you’ll be bleeding out of your eyes by the end of the show, and you’ll be laughing so hard that your intestines will likely pop up out of your mouth and fly into the air, lassoing an unsuspecting audience member four rows down who will mistake your guts for a prop that’s supposed to be used in the show and will start gleefully playing with them. And you’ll still be laughing so hard at the sickness on the screen that you won’t even notice and care. Seriously, that’s how good this year’s show is.

7:15, 9:45pm | $8.75 | Get tickets

Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
320 E 6th St • Austin, TX
