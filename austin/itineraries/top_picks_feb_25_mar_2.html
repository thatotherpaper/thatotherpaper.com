TOP picks: Feb 25 - Mar 2

25 February 2008 | That Other Paper

Monday | February 25

Rock and Roll Karaoke

photo / jenn tx  

Beerland has one of the best, if not the best karaoke night in town, with over 1,000 songs to choose from. No cover and beer specials just add to the fun – bring your friends, embarrass yourself with a passionate rendition of “Pour Some Sugar On Me,” and leave happy.

9pm | FREE

Beerland
711 Red River • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Love and a 45 Record Player Party at Rio Rita | 7pm
  • Funktronica at Flamingo Cantina | 10pm
  • Stage adaptation of the 1971 cult classic Harold and Maude at Mercury Hall | 7:30pm

Tuesday | February 26

Johnny Cash Bash

courtesy KOOP 

This is a benefit for KOOP 91.7 to help rebuild the place after yet another fire. This is going to be a rowdy night at the Continental Club. On the bill for the evening they have: Death and Texas, Dale Watson, Bob King, Roger Wallace, Rosie Flores, and The Band in Black. Make sure you wear some black – you’ll get a discount at the door.

10pm | $10 if you wear black

Continental Club
1315 S Congress • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Maceo Parker at Antones | 8pm
  • Daniel Johnston tribute night and music video premier at The Parish Room | 8pm
  • Final performance: Stage adaptation of the 1971 cult classic Harold and Maude at Mercury Hall | 7:30pm

Wednesday | February 27

Oscar nominated film shorts and animation

Ever watched The Oscars and seen the awards given for live action and animation and wished there was an easy way to watch them? Well it’s Christmas time, and the Alamo Drafthouse is Kris Kringle. Each category is a separate showing with five wonderful films.

4:30pm, 7:40pm, 10pm | $8.25/6.25

Alamo Drafthouse
320 E 6th St • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • Worst Bands in Austin Showcase with Career Criminal, Comanche Abortion, Destroyed for Comfort vs. Subnatural, and comedy by Trey Galyon at Beerland | 6pm
  • Free brisket and tortillas at Freddie’s Place | 6pm until they run out

Thursday | February 28

courtesy Pump Project / Fred Tabares 

Rhymes With Orange: New work from the Painterly Handsomes

Rhymes With Orange, Pump Project’s current exhibition, features collaborative work composed directly on the gallery walls by Troy Allen, Chris Holloway, Cody Scrogum, and Frederick Tabares, who make up the Painterly Handsomes.

1–6pm

Pump Project Art Complex
702 Shady Ln • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Austin Collins CD Release Party at Stubb’s Bar-B-Q | 8:30pm
  • The Notorious Open Mic at The Velveeta Room | 10pm

Friday | February 29

Ghostland Observatory CD Release Show

Ah, how to spend your leap day? Say what you will about this band – we’ve all probably said something terrible at one time or another – but for the time, they are Austin music. And their new record, Robotique Majestique will probably either shuttle them off to international glory a la Daft Punk or leave them in the same dumpster where Sound Team wonders what happened to them. Either way, say you were there, and get a copy of the CD (included in the ticket price) so you can spoil it for your friends before they pick one up at Waterloo.

9pm | $30 advance / $32 day-of-show (includes CD)

Austin Music Hall
208 Nueces St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Punchline at ColdTowne Theater | 10pm
  • Damn Yankees presented by TexArts at Paramount Theater | 8pm

Saturday | March 1

Marilyn Manson with Ours

Marilyn Manson needs no introduction. The controversial artist born Brian Hugh Warner (no joke!) is known for hard music, gender-bending, and being the ex-Mr.-Dita-von-Tesse. He’ll hit Austin on tour tonight with his friends Ours.

Trivia: Did you know that Marilyn Manson tried to launch his own brand of absinthe? And make sure you check out his website – it’s pretty trippy.

8pm | $39

Austin Music Hall
208 Nueces St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Citizen Cope at Stubb’s | 7pm
  • Texas State Arts Festival at Bob Bullock Texas State History Museum | 10am–6pm

Sunday | March 2

Betty’s Summer Vacation

Betty’s Summer Vacation is produced by Austin Community College’s drama department, and today is the last day to see the production. It’s the story of a vacation that winds up being more than the title character asked for:

From the promoter: Betty is looking forward to her summer share at the ocean. But Trudy, whom she knows only slightly, chatters incessantly; and then there are the other housemates – sexy lout Buck, who’s pathologically on the make with women all the time, and sweet, withdrawn Keith who carries a shovel and a mysterious hatbox and just may be a serial killer. Then the emotionally anarchic landlady, Mrs. Siezmagraff, moves in too; and she invites a crazy derelict to dinner, and, well, the vacation becomes more and more of a strain for poor Betty. Not to mention there seems to be a laugh track coming from the ceiling that no one seems able to shut up. Death, destruction, mayhem – Betty finds it all in her seaside retreat.

2pm | Call 223-3000 for tickets

Austin Community College (Rio Grande Campus)
1212 Rio Grande • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • Gospel Brunch at Stubb’s Bar-B-Q | 11am
  • Beauty and the Beast at Scottish Rite Theatre | 2pm
