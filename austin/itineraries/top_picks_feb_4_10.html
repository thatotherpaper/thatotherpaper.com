TOP picks: Feb 4 - 10

4 February 2008 | That Other Paper

Monday | February 4

Register to vote

Today is the deadline to register to vote in the impending March 4 primary election. Your application has to be postmarked by today. You can print your application at www.sos.state.tx.us. Alternatively, you can go to any public library, or the Travis County Courthouse to pick up your application. It’s possible that your post office will have applications, but not every single post office does.

Once registered, www.sos.state.tx.us allows you to keep tabs on your status and polling locations.

All day | FREE

Travis County Courthouse
1000 Guadalupe • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Soundcheck Residency and KOOP Benefit w/ Ladyfingers, Sorne, and Team Fabrication at Beauty Bar | 9pm
  • Love and a 45 record player party at Rio Rita | 7pm

Tuesday | February 5

The Bravery with The Switches

If you’re not collecting beads in New Orleans for Mardi Gras, check out New York-born The Bravery, who have been compared to Depeche Mode and The Cure. Music critic Mike Affholder says, “The Bravery masks conventional music norms behind mathematical synth beats and vocals that Morrissey has practically copyrighted. New-wave is coming back in a way that recollects the ’80s in their prime, and The Bravery has taken everything good about the decade’s music and blended it into one accessible new wave smoothie.” Check out The Bravery’s MySpace and website to hear their music before heading to the show. This concert’s going to be huge, so don’t miss it.

10pm

603 Red River • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Austin Blues Society Blues Jam at Antone’s | 7pm

Wednesday | February 6

On the Road with the Beats

February 5 through August 3, The Harry Ransom Center will be exhibiting all their Beat effects – manuscripts, photos, books, and visual arts – to trace the extensive travels and literary revelations of Jack Kerouac, Allen Ginsburg, William S. Burroughs and others. On March 7, the Ransom Center will display Kerouac’s famed scroll manuscript of On the Road.

Standing across from these authentic documents gives you a tingly feeling.

Tue 10am–5pm, Wed 10am–5pm, Thurs 10am–7pm, Friday 10am–5pm, Sat Noon–5pm, Sun Noon–5pm | FREE

Harry Ransom Center
300 W 21st St • Austin, TX 78712

Thursday | February 7

The Velvet Rut Theatre’s ’90s grunge take on Romeo and Juliet

There have been countless takes on Shakespeare’s Romeo and Juliet – to satisfy the short attention span of modern audiences, directors and casts have gotten creative with their productions. Musicals, operas, ballets, Leonardo DiCaprio, and now a ’90s grunge take on one of the most famous works ever. From the promoter:

The Velvet Rut Theatre’s ’90s grunge take on Shakespeare’s Romeo and Juliet, with lighting by Steven H. Shirley (A Thought In Three Parts). Kelli Jones and Casey Allen as R&J, Japhy Fernandes as Friar, John Baltisberger as Tibalt, Christopher Harris as Paris, Aron Lawhon as Benvoilio, and featuring Julio Mella as Mercutio. Kevin “encyclopedia” Cox chews scenery as Prince.

Bonus: The Velvet Rut Theatre is BYOB.

8pm | Pay what you can

Saturday | February 9

courtesy of the Vortex Theatre 

Beirut

Tonight is the last night to see Beirut at the Vortex Theatre. Beirut was written by Alan Bowne, who died of AIDS in 1989. His play Beirut is an allegory that analyzes the American AIDS problem. The play, set in the future, depicts a virulent planet where people are quickly being infected by a deadly sexually transmitted virus that has ravaged civilization as we know it – consequently, the concept of love has been abolished. Those infected are being rounded up and quarantined in New York’s lower east side, known as Beirut, to be kept away from the general population. Torch, the male character, has been infected and quarantined. Blue, his one-time lover, makes her way illegally into Beirut and to his cell. Will they or won’t they do it?

There will be nudity and adult situations.

8pm | $10–$30

Vortex Theater
2307 Manor • Austin, TX 78722

The Shape of Things

courtesy of the City Theatre 

Tonight’s the last night to catch the dark comedy The Shape of Things by Neil Labute. Billing itself as “a thought-provoking dark comedy that shakes up the lives of a trio of friends and explores modern love, jealousy, manipulation and…the shape of things,” this play is sure to entertain. Labute’s play has also been made into a film starring Paul Rudd and Rachel Weisz, so you know it’s good.
