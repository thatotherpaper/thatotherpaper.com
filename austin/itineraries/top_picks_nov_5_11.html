TOP picks: Nov 5 - 11

5 November 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | November 5

Jim Lehrer: The Journalism Revolution

Native Texan Jim Lehrer is the executive editor and anchor of PBS’s influential show The News Hour with Jim Lehrer. Highly respected in his field – having numerous awards and honors – Lehrer will be speaking about the history of journalism and the ways our understanding and consumption of media continues to evolve. The discussion will pay particular attention to issues surrounding the conflict between print and online media.

6:30pm | FREE

UT Hogg Auditorium
24th St and Whitis • Austin, TX 78705

Other options:

  • Grounded in Music fundraiser at Antone’s | 9pm

Tuesday | November 6

EXTRA-ORDINARY: The Everyday Object in American Art

courtesy AMOA 

Everyday objects are beautiful. That’s the theme of the current exhibition at the Austin Museum of Art. In this exhibition, life informs art, not the other way around, with sculptures, photographs, paintings, and prints spanning more than 40 years. Check out what artists like Andy Warhol and Alexander Calder found beautiful in their day-to-day existences.

10am–6pm | FREE for kids under 12; $4 students and seniors; $5 adults

Austin Museum of Art
823 Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Gram Parsons Hoot Night benefiting The Sims Foundation at Threadgill’s World Headquarters | 7pm
  • iPod Party at Club 115 | 8pm

Wednesday | November 7

UCB Touring Company 

The Upright Citizens Brigade Touring Company

Although this is not the original cast you saw on TV a few years back, the Upright Citizens Brigade Touring Company has gone through intense training to be able to call themselves part of the UCB. This should be a hilarious evening.

7 and 9:45pm | $15

Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
320 E 6th St. • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Polyphonic Spree, Rooney, Redwalls at Emo’s | 10pm
  • Trube Farell and Sniz at Continental Club | 9pm

Thursday | November 8

Mute Math with Eisley

Mute Math are on their way up. They’ve performed on Late Night with Conan O’Brien, The Late Show with David Letterman, The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson, and TRL. They also recorded the Transformers theme! They’ll be at Waterloo Records before the show at 5pm. Get tickets to the show here.

La Zona Rosa
612 W 4th St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Black Joe Lewis, Mr. Lewis and the Funeral Five, The Peculiar Pretzelmen, and Miss Vermilion at Room 710 | 7pm
  • Two-Step and Country Western Swing Dance Lessons at Broken Spoke | 8pm

Friday | November 9

Yawn 

That Other Paper’s One-Year Anniversary Party

Dayum! We’re going all out for our one-year bash – performances by The Jolly Garogers, Fishboy, The Fred and Joel Show, John Wesley Coleman, and Bryan Gutmann, the Funniest Person in Austin. And DJ Manny will be spinning and emceeing all night. My, how fancy. Kick it somewhere comfy out on the Scoot’s patio, or challenge a mysterious stranger to a game of Halo 3. Other options:

Saturday | November 10

photo / cleong Wurstfest or The Worst? 

Wurstfest

Wurstfest is a 10-day festival that starts every year on the Friday before the first Monday in November. And let’s face it: Most of us don’t get over to Germany enough. Despite their occasional attempts to conquer the entire world – and the violence and complicated identity issues associated with such attempts – Germans are fucking cool. And if you’ve never been out to Wurstfest to stuff your face with bratwurst and beer, you really should. Just make sure you have a designated driver if you plan on heading back to Austin the same night. Our public transportation is coming along, but it’s not as if you can just get hammered in New Braunfels and jump on a train for Austin – then get in a heated verbal conflict with a Turkish guy, puke, pass out, and end up in Galveston.

All day after 11am | $8 (12 and under FREE)

Wurstfest Grounds
835 Fredericksburg Rd • New Braunfels, TX 78130

Other options:

  • Stitch Fashion Show at Austin Convention Center | 4:30pm
  • Technical Farm Tour Workshop at Tecolote Farm | 8am–12pm

4pm | NO COVER

Ginny’s Little Longhorn Saloon
5434 Burnet Rd • Austin, TX 78756

Other options:
