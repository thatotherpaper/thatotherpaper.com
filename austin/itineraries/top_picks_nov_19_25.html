TOP picks: Nov 19 - 25

19 November 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | November 19

Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles pizza party

photo / plasticrevolver OH SHI 

The kid in us is about to go nuts. Tonight at the Drafthouse Ritz, they’re not only showing the original Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles, but they’re making it a full-on pizza party – as it should be any time the flick is shown anywhere. Now, what exactly do they mean by pizza party? you might be wondering. All. You. Can. Eat. Believe it. And come dressed as your favorite turtle – there will be a costume contest. Note: This is an all-ages show, so it’s cool to bring your 10-year-old best friend or something.

The world’s most beloved reptiles are in full effect in the greatest ’80s movie of the ’90s! Four ordinary turtles are exposed to The Ooze (aka toxic waste!) and, with the help of enormous Buddhist rat Master Splinter, grow into an unstoppable force of martial arts justice! When Splinter is kidnapped by his loathsome nemesis The Shredder, the turtles enlist the aid of TV reporter April O’Neil and wise-cracking vigilante maniac Casey Jones to rescue their number one rodent from the venomous karate clan. And you’d better believe that Leonardo, Donatello, Michaelangelo and Raphael are ready to kick as much shell as necessary in the process.

7pm | $14 | Get tickets

Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
320 E 6th St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • La Putain Avec Les Fleurs at Salvage Vanguard | 8pm
  • Circle Takes the Square, Consider the Source, BPIV, Fight Pretty, Faithhealer at Red 7 | 8pm

Tuesday | November 20

photo / Schram Alejandro Escovedo 

Alejandro Escovedo

Longtime central Texas resident Alejandro Escovedo has been in the rock ’n’ roll game for over 30 years. During his early career Escovedo made a name for himself with his bands Nuns, Rank and File, and True Believers. He’s been touring solo for the past 14 years. And he seems to just keep getting better. His latest album, The Boxing Mirror, (which he cut after he was back on his feet after overcoming health complications) was hailed by critics and sold very well. The fact that after all this time he keeps his ticket prices under $10 proves that he’s the real deal.

10pm | $7

Continental Club
1315 South Congress • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Punk’s Not Dead Alamo Ritz | 10pm
  • Lydia Lunch at Emo’s | 8pm

Wednesday | November 21

La Putain Avec Les Fleurs: A Junk Melodrama

The most famous clown in 1940s France, the legendary Baptiste, goes on a quest to find true happiness as a real man. This production will be performed at different venues all over Austin this month, and tonight it’s at The Parish. From the promoter:

A troupe of vagabond performers, known as “Theatre Des Funambules,” along with their live acoustic avant-garde band, Bric-a-Brac, present the story of Baptiste, the world’s greatest clown, and his journey to become a real man. An explosion of song, dance, pantomime, clowning, puppetry, and a Big French Bear, La Putain truly has something for everyone!

8pm and 11pm | $10

The Parish
214 E 6th St, • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Bubba and Babe’s Backwoods Wedding dinner theater at Patsy’s Cowgirl Cafe | 7:30pm
  • Ringo Deathstarr, Cavedweller, She, Sir at Emo’s | 10pm

It’s Thanksgiving, the day where eating everything in sight and sitting around is just what you’re supposed to do. If you’re not having a traditional dinner at home with family and friends, check out James Bickham’s All You Can Eat column for some ideas on where to eat.

Friday | November 23

photo / Chad Hanna Chris Trew overeats sushi – again! 

ColdTowne Theater’s Biggest Shopping Day of the Year All Stars

Not only are our friends over at ColdTowne hysterically funny, they’re in the giving spirit tonight. Between rent and bills and now all the spending associated with the holidays, you’re broke and so are they. But rather than getting all Scrooge about it, they’ll be presenting their All Star show totally, absolutely, 100 percent free – no strings attached. Seats are limited, so get there early.

8pm | FREE

ColdTowne Theater
4803 Airport Blvd • Austin, TX 78751

Other options:

  • Marky Ramone and Friends, The Queers, Superzero, The Sweethearts at Emo’s | 10pm
  • Alamo Drafthouse: UT Football on the Big Screen at Alamo Drafthouse Village | 2:30pm

Saturday | November 24

The Santaland Diaries

photo / jnd90745 David Sedaris 

People really took note of David Sedaris on a national scale when he read The Santaland Diaries on NPR’s Morning Edition in 1992. A hilarious personal essay chronicling his misadventures as an elf at Macy’s in New York, The Santaland Diaries’ sardonic humor appeals to everybody. Sedaris has won the Thurber Prize of American Humor more than once. Critics have also said that he’s one of the sharpest satirists we’ve had since Nathanael West. And when people say that, you’ve made it. The play, staged at Zachary Scott Theater, has become a perennial holiday favorite. Note: The humor is very adult and would probably make your children cry.

8pm | $33–$44

Zach Scott Theater
1510 Toomey Rd • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Ghostface Killah, Rhythm Roots All-Stars, DJ Mel at The Parish | 9:30pm
  • Table Manners at Plush | 10pm

Sunday | November 25

photo / susan_w On a mission from God 

Moonlight Cinema: Blues Brothers

Hosted by the multi-talented Jerm Pollet, this free dinner/movie event happens each Sunday at The Belmont (the bar that brought you the $1 million, six-song Prince show). The movies are always classics in the vein of Blazing Saddles, Office Space, and so on. Tonight, they’ll be showing The Blues Brothers. Just so you know, the events are interactive, so it’s not like you’ll just be sitting there watching a movie. There will be some live music, witty repartee, and good times. So go there like you’re on a mission from God.

8pm | FREE

Belmont
305 W 6th St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • City-Wide Garage Sale at Palmer Event Center | 11am
  • The Australian Pink Floyd at The Paramount Theatre | 8pm
