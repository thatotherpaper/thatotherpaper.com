TOP picks: August 20-26

20 August 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | August 20

Bob Schneider and Jay Thomas

photo / dhfloyd 

The son of an opera singer, Schneider began learning piano and guitar at a very early age. And all that practice has paid off, as Schneider has moved more albums through Waterloo Records than any other Austin musician, Willie Nelson included. He co-founded and played with such well-known acts as The Scabs and The Ugly Americans. The sets he plays on Monday nights at The Saxon Pub are sit-down affairs.

8:30pm | $10

The Saxon Pub
1320 S Lamar • 448-2552

Other Options:

  • Fallen Idol at The Paramount Theater | 7pm
  • One Man Exhibition: Contemporary Works by Steven Tugwell at El Taller Gallery (last day!) | 10am–6pm
  • Party Garbage, Delay (OH), Johnny Bodacious & the Bad Attitudes, and The Unloveables at The Parlor | 8pm

Tuesday | August 21

Open-mic night at B.D. Riley’s

Sign up to perform or just go drink some mead, listen to local artists before they make it big, and pretend you’re Irish.

Sign-up at 7:30pm; performances at 8pm | FREE

512-Go!» B.D. Riley’s Irish Pub
204 E Sixth St • 512.494.1335

Other Options:

  • Church of the Friendly Ghost at Salvage Vanguard Theater | 8pm
  • ATX Beat Battle at Flamingo Cantina | 8:30pm

Wednesday | August 22

Tabletop Sculpture

courtesy Art Palace 

This show has been running since July 14, but tonight is your last chance to catch it at Art Palace. Curated by Amanda Douberley, the exhibit features artists Richie Budd, Hunter Cross, Bill Davenport, Katalin Hausel, Mark Schatz, and Jared Steffensen. Tabletop Sculpture works off an interesting premise: Use small models to project a contemplative state or a big idea, fully realized by the subject or not. To show off these small sculptures, the gallery space has been transformed into a giant installation, designed by Erin Curtis, of a mid-20th-century living room.

You can also check out the newly built Side Gallery, which is displaying the works of fine wood worker and Austinite Alod Valdes Bohm. His stylish, handcrafted furniture echoes and reinterprets modernist sculpture and architecture.

7pm–9pm | FREE

Art Palace
2109 Cesar Chavez • 496-0687

Other Options:

  • Screen Door Films presents eight short films at Salvage Vanguard Theater | 7:30pm
  • Karaoke Apocalypse at Emo’s | 9pm
  • Community Night puppet show at the Austin Children’s Museum | 6pm

Thursday | August 26

Neo-Soul Lounge and Poetry Reading

courtesy Club Illusion 

Every Thursday at Club Illusion, South Flavas Entertainment hosts a themed open-mic night. Acts can include anything from spoken word to singing and monologues, with neo-soul music sliced in between. This week’s theme is genocide. The featured poet will be Taalam Acey from Baltimore, Maryland. Sign up is open to anyone and starts at 8. Dress code is enforced, so look fly.

8pm | 21 and up | $6

Club Illusion
2700 W Pecan St • Pflugerville, TX 78660

Other Options:

  • Hot and Cold: The Best of Canadian Contemporary Art at Art on 5th | 10am–8pm
  • Shannfab at Red Eyed Fly | 10pm

Friday | August 24

Honky

photo / basurablancaphoto Honky 

Austinite Jeff Pinkus, of Butthole Surfers fame, also plays bass in the band Honky, a straight-ahead rock ’n’ roll band. So don’t go expecting a psychedelic showdown. Rather, this is three gentlemen from Austin who are ready to ROCK your ass. Pinkus says two things about his band: “We’re not trying to be groundbreaking; this is the most timeless music there is,” but he also says, “When we have 300 people at our shows, they tend to drink like they’re 600.” The Washingtons will start at 10pm, then it’s Grady at 11pm; Honky comes on at 12:30am.

12:30am | $8

The Continental Club
1315 S Congress Ave • 441-2444

Other Options:

  • TRON at the Paramount Theatre | 7:30pm and 9:40pm
  • Ladies of the ’80s Singalong at Alamo Drafthouse Village | 11:59pm
  • The Applicators, Young Heart Attack, Human Being Lawnmower, The Steps at Emo’s | 10pm

Saturday | August 25

photo / j. lindsay Flugtag 

Flugtag

Unless you’ve been living under a rock that’s under a boulder that’s under a dead giant’s asshole, you know that today is the day Flugtag takes over Auditorium Shores. Get there early, find a good spot, and cheer for your favorite Flugtag team as they’re judged on showmanship, creativity, and distance. Whose homemade craft will fly the farthest, and will anyone break the current US record of 155 feet? The anticipation is killing us.

Gates open at 5pm; first flight at 6:30pm | FREE

Auditorium Shores
900 West Riverside Dr • Austin, TX 78704

Other Options:

  • David de Lara’s Broken Dolls at Pump Project Arts Complex | 7pm
  • ROMP: Back to School Special at the Vortex | 3pm
  • David Huntsberger w/ Daniel Jackson and Todd Merriman at the Velveeta Room | 9pm and 11:30pm
