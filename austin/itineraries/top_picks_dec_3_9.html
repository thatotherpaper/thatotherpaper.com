TOP picks: Dec 3 - 9

3 December 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | December 3

Improv 101: ColdTowne Open House

photo / Kristin Hillery 

If you’ve never defined your relationship to improv comedy, our friends at ColdTowne Theater are giving you the chance to do just that – for free. The environment is no pressure, and experienced teachers will be there to address your curiosity. Improv skills aren’t just for people who want to be on stage or TV. They can help you in your real-life interactions with family, friends, and strangers – especially in situations where the three overlap. Full disclosure: We’re good friends with ColdTowne, but we couldn’t let you miss out on this.

7pm | FREE

ColdTowne Theater
4803 Airport Blvd • Austin, TX 78751

Other options:

  • Cave, UME, Megazilla, We’ll Go Machete at Emo’s | 10pm
  • Gibson Robot Guitar demonstrations at Guitar Center | 3pm

Tuesday | December 4

photo / phil schatz The Jackalope

10:30pm | No cover

The Jackalope
404 E Sixth • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Todd Rundgren at Antones | 8pm
  • DJ Scorpio at Beauty Bar | 12am

With a ton of amazing stuff – paintings, sculptures, jewelry, toys, and more – from over 80 local and regional artists, Blue Genie is the place to get all your holiday shopping done. Today is the opening day of the bazaar, and it will run until December 24.

Other options:

Thursday | December 6

Checking It Twice

This St. Idiot Collective production is a naturalistic, adult take on the world of Santa and the nature of Christmas. The play is broken up into three sections that all have a quirky and unusual spin on the traditional Christmas story. Proceeds from the show will go to LifeWorks Austin.

Checking It Twice includes scenes by some of Austinâ€™s leading playwrights, such as Ken Webster of Hyde Park Theater and Kirk Lynn of the Rude Mechs. Local comedian Chuck Watkins moonlights as the humble narrator, a Ghost of Christmas Past style cocaine street hustler named Frosty the Blowman.

If you can’t go tonight, the show will also run on Friday and Saturday nights at the same time and place.

8pm | $8 at the door | Get tickets

The Hideout
617 Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • The Creative Art of Editing at Austin Film Society | 7pm
  • The Nutcracker at The Paramount | 7:30pm

Friday | December 7

SuperHouse

Local foursome SuperHouse play at The Parlor tonight, so get in there, grab a slice of pizza, and dance your ass off – they wouldn’t have it any other way.

From SuperHouse’s MySpace page:

Seeing SuperHouse perform live is like believing in miracles. They are ruthlessly fresh, relentlessly inventive, and ridiculously energetic. They dance, in the hopes that you will dance, they flail in the hopes that you will flail. They enjoy life, not merely for the sake of enjoyment, but in an effort to teach others how. Watch and learn with us.

9pm | FREE

The Parlor
43rd and Guadalupe Sts • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • Punchline at ColdTowne Theater | 10pm
  • Luminations at Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Center | 6pm

Saturday | December 8

Collaboration from Conception

courtesy Sodalitas 

Collaboration from Conception results from the efforts of three different collaborative artist groups: Sodalitas, comprised of Shea Little, Jana Swec, and Joseph Phillips (the organizers of E.A.S.T.); Katalin Hausel, Leanne Zacharias and Jacyln Pryor; and Art Silva and Stephanie Bonham. The works are diverse, as are the artists – they vary widely in age, artistic experience, and method. In order to inform the viewer, you will be provided with the creative history of many of the works of art – including a raft built by the artists. The works will be displayed at Creative Research Laboratory, a contemporary art/design space furthering art education in Austin by encouraging collaborations between students, teachers, artists, alumni, and the community at large.

Noon–5pm | FREE

Creative Research Laboratory
2832 E MLK • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Clap Clap, Haunting Oboe Music at Emo’s | 9pm
  • Gourds Holidaze Show at Ruta Maya | 9pm

Sunday | December 9

The Southern Sirens Burlesque Show

Made up of four dancers and two singers, Southern Sirens were founded this year and aim to be “the most sizzling show in Texas.” The ladies will perform their full-length show The Soul of a Woman tonight at The Parish.

Their intricate choreography is modern, raw and high energy. The alluring singers add the live element that Austin, the live music capital is known for. These sultry women have mastered the art of tease in this naughty yet refined performance. We guarantee their Southern charm will leave you begging for more.

Get a table by emailing booking@theparishroom.com. Front tables are $60 and include up to three admissions; rear tables are $50 and also include up to three admissions.

8pm | $50–$60

The Parish
214 E Sixth St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Cherrywood Art Fair at Maplewood Elementary | 10am
  • Half price bottles of wine at Woodland | All day
