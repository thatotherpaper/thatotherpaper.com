TOP picks: Oct 15 - 21

15 October 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | October 15

Rock and Roll Karaoke

photo / jenn tx 

With over 1,000 songs to choose from you’re bound to find a song you both know and like at Rock and Roll Karaoke. Just don’t try to do an Axl Rose or Elvis… unless you’re really, really good. No one enjoys that awkward, shaky rendition of “Welcome to the Jungle.”

9pm | FREE

Beerland
711.5 Red River • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Lyle Lovett and his Large Band at Stubb’s | 7pm
  • Car Stereo (Wars) at Emo’s | 10pm

Tuesday | October 16

Fugiya and Miyagi

courtesy Fugiya and Miyagi 

Three people actually make up this band: Steve Lewis (Fujiya) plays synths and takes care of backing vocals; David Best (Miyagi) sings and plays guitar; and Matt Hainsby (Ampersand), who joined the band in 2005, plays bass guitar and sings backing vocals. The band formed in Brighton, UK in 2000. They are influenced by Krautrock bands such as Neu!, Can, and Kraftwerk as well as early ’90s electronica such as Aphex Twin. Their music reflects their influences and yet makes a whole new sound – one that’s good to dance to.

8pm | $10

Club DeVille
900 Red River St • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Sunset Rubdown, Johnny and the Moon, and Magic Weapon at Emo’s | 10pm
  • 3D African Adventure at Bob Bullock Museum | see website for times

Wednesday | October 17

photo / ghoulmann 

The Melvins

Without The Melvins there would be no Nirvana… or grunge rock for that matter. Their influences range from the heavy Black Flag and Black Sabbath to slower rock acts like Flipper and Swans – so The Melvins like to take it slow and heavy. And they keep it real: Despite having been in the business so long, they still charge well under $20 to see their show. Here’s what their label, ipecac recordings, has to say about them:

The Melvins are family entertainment on another level. Some people say The Melvins are the wave of the future. Some people think The Melvins have already influenced a huge wave of music on a global level but most people expect The Melvins to totally fuck up. Most people think they should be called “The Fucks” because they’re fucked.

10pm | $12

Emo’s
603 Red River • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Guitar Auction (all those guitars around town are being sold) at GSD&M | 6:30pm
  • Jesu, USSA, Weedeater, Wolves in the Throne Room at The Mohawk | 10pm

Thursday | October 18

photo / Roberta Roth 

The Donnas

This all-girl hard rock band made it big in 2002 with their major hit “Take It Off.” Since then The Donnas’ music has been featured in various movies, TV shows, and even video games. Just this September, they released their latest album, Bitchin’. The Donnas will be performing with Donita Sparks and American Bang tonight at the Parish.

8pm | $15

The Parish
214B E 6th St • Austin, TX 78704

Other options:

  • Thursday Night Awesome at Coldtowne Theater | 10pm
  • Film Fantastique at Volitant Gallery | 6pm

Friday | October 19

courtesy Vortex Theater 

IO: A Myth About You

From the Vortex:

When money and fame guarantee you worship, where do the real gods go? The god of the streets captures a savior with a secret and a mistress with no memory – but who’s the real prisoner here? Part myth, part mockery, part modern-day morality play, Io will remind you of the distinct joys of being mortal.

Shrewd Productions is thrilled to announce the world premiere of Io: a myth about you, a play with music written by Co-Artistic Director, Monika Bustamante, and directed by NYC-based (by way of Austin) director, Elena Araoz. This exciting new foray into heroic tales, vengeance, decadence, salvation, forgiveness, and rock & roll opens at the VORTEX on October 19th and runs Thursday, Friday, and Saturday nights at 8pm through November 10th.

8pm | $15–$25

The Vortex
2307 Manor Road, Austin, TX 78722

Other options:

  • Minus the Bear with The Helio Sequence and Tiny Vipers at Emo’s | 10pm
  • DJ Skot Kees at One 2 One | 10pm

Saturday | October 20

Tour de Fat

photo / Salim Virji Tour de Fat 

New Belgium Brewing Company is behind Tour de Fat, a free festival happening at Fiesta Gardens today that’s all about bikes, beer, and crazy costumes. Dress up and enjoy performances by The Reals, The Asylum Street Spankers, March Fourth Marching Band, and The Handsome Little Devils. Proceeds from beer sales will go to Austin’s Yellow Bike Project, the Austin Cycling Association, and the Austin Ridge Riders.

11am–noon (ride time); noon–6pm | FREE

Fiesta Gardens
2100 Bergman Ave • Austin, TX 78702

Other options:

  • Doug Mellard at The Velveeta Room | 9:30 and 11:30pm
  • Elliott Yamin at The Parish | 9pm

Sunday | October 21

photo / Erik Charlton 

Maker Faire

Enjoy this two-day family-friendly event at the Travis County Fairgrounds that exhibits and teaches homemade arts and crafts, engineering, science projects, and do-it-yourself projects. Events include Swap-O-Rama-Rama, the King of Fling Catapult Competition, and Edible Austin’s DIY Food.

10am–5pm | $25

Travis County Fairgrounds
7311 Decker Lane • Austin, TX 78724

Other options:

  • The Austin Pops at Ruta Maya | 8pm
  • Earth’s Birthday at BookPeople | 1pm

Man, is this the right time for Dino to come back or what??? It’s been over 20 years since Dino Lee (aka Mr. Fabulous) clanged shut the lid on the White Trash Revue, the wildest showband Austin ever saw. They played a CD release party last Friday that proves they were one of Austin’s most eclectic recording acts, mixing funk, punk, shock rock and Tex Mex.

Dino Lee & the White Trash Revue are getting together one more time on Friday, October 26 at Antone’s. Dino Lee performs with Texas accordion king Ponty Bone (Joe Ely, the Southsiders), Hector Muñoz (True Believers, Hellapeño, Alejandro Escovedo), Chris Gates (Big Boys, Gatesville), Mike Navarro (Joe King Carrasco, 3 Balls of Fire), Troy Dillinger (Austin Swim, Band From Hell), Frankie Camaro (Big Guitars from Texas), Evl_E (Gahdzilla Motor Company, Gods Among Men), David Beeson (Broken Teeth), John Mills (David Byrne), and Mark Wilson (Burning Spear). Original Jam & Jelly Girls Margaret Moser, Kim Miller, and Alice Berry are playing too.

Here’s what Classic Rock Magazine UK had to say about Dino Lee in their September 2007 issue: “While most of his brethren in the 80’s Austin punk scene were content to thrash out noisy odes to teenage angst in grubby jeans and greasy hair, Lee sported an 18-inch pompadour, dressed like a Roman centurion and concocted stage shows that crossed Caligula with a Wild West Rodeo. His 1985 album The King of White Trash, a wild mix of Tex Mex, soul, swing, punk and high-octane rock’n’roll, was his definitive album.”

“As the self-proclaimed (and unchallenged) King of White Trash, Dino Lee staged spectacular tributes to Caligula, pioneered the merger of rock ‘n’ roll and pro wrestling and pretty much stole the show when MTV’s Cutting Edge devoted an entire one hour program to Austin in 1985,” wrote Michael Corcoran in the Austin American-Statesman.

The newly released King of White Trash Anthology, Volume I (PolyFab Records) is at Waterloo, and it collects the best of Dino Lee’s vinyl records from his two New Rose albums: King of White Trash and The New Las Vegan on CD, with favorites such as “Beer Party,” “Stud Pony,” “Baa Baa Black Sheep,” “Sex Change.” and “Everybody Get Some.” This release is the first time it has been available in America since its original vinyl offering.

3 Balls of Fire will work their twang whammy at 10:30pm. Tix are on sale at Antone’s and moving fast. THIS is going to be one hot show!
