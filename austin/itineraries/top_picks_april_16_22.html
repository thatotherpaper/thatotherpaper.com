TOP picks: April 16 - 22

16 April 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | April 16

David Sedaris

David Sedaris is back in Austin again to talk about – well, it doesn’t really matter what he talks about, because regardless of the subject, it’ll be interesting and entertaining. The only tickets left are “obstructed view” seats, and the only way to get them is to call the Paramount Theatre’s box office at 472-5470. This event has been rescheduled for Monday, April 23

8pm | $45

Paramount Theatre
713 Congress Ave • 472-5470

Tuesday | April 17

Aqua Teen Hunger Force Colon Movie Film For Theaters 

Aqua Teen Hunger Force Colon Movie Film For Theaters

Everybody’s favorite edible trio, Aqua Teen Hunger Force, takes it to the big screen for Aqua Teen Hunger Force Colon Movie Film For Theaters, a film that’s been getting some excellent reviews. The 10-minute ATHF toons have been one of the best parts of Adult Swim for the past few years, and there’s nothing better than getting to see Meatwad, Frylock, and Master Shake star in their very own movie film for theaters.

From originalalamo.com: An action-adventure epic that tackles the mysterious circumstances that brought Meatwad, Frylock and Master Shake together. An immortal piece of exercise equipment threatens the balance of galactic peace, and it is up to the Aqua Teen Hunger Force to run away from it. Complicating matters, the Plutonians team up with the Cybernetic Ghost of Christmas Past for ultimate control of the deadly device.

Various showtimes | $6–$8 | Get tickets

512-Go!» Alamo Drafthouse South Lamar
4th & Colorado St • Austin, TX 78701

Wednesday | April 18

Two-for-one at Emo’s

One cover gets you both stages tonight at Emo’s. Live tunes from Ponys, Deerhunter, and Lomita (outside stage), and Brightblack Morning Light, The White Lodge, Silver Pines (inside stage).

10pm | $8

512-Go!» Emo’s
6th & Red River • 477-3667

Thursday | April 19

The Paper Sculpture Show

Image courtesy of amoa.org 

From amoa.org: The Paper Sculpture Show brings the artist and visitor together by providing a hands-on art making experience. This interactive exhibition is composed of works by 29 artists, drawn on flat sheets of paper and stacked in the gallery space. The Museum invites visitors to choose projects and assemble them into a three-dimensional sculptures. It invites visitors to reconsider the relationship between two-dimensional images and three dimensional sculptural objects, originality, mass production and the handmade as they become the artists in this interactive exhibition.

10am–8pm | $5 (adults), $4 (students and seniors), free for kids under 12

512-Go!» AMoA Downtown
823 Congress Ave • 495-9224

Friday | April 20

Easy Star All-Stars perform Radiodread

Courtesy of Easy Star Records 

This isn’t your typical reggae cover band. The Easy Star All-Stars are a collaboration of artists from the New York City label Easy Star Records. The All-Stars are best known for their 2003 release Dub Side of the Moon, a surprising and well-fit reggae adaptation of Pink Floyd’s classic, Dark Side of the Moon. Two years ago, they performed the entire album at Flamingo Cantina to a sold-out crowd. They’re sure to work their magic again as they transform Radiohead’s OK Computer into reggae gold.

Easy Star Records website

Listen: Karma Police

Doors at 8:30pm | $15

512-Go!» Flamingo Cantina
6th & Neches • Austin, TX

Saturday | April 21

Art + Erotica = ArtErotica Art Auction and Party

The Octopus Club’s 5th annual Erotic Art Auction & Dance Party benefiting Austin AIDS Emergency Fund is rescheduled and rejuvenated for this weekend. Buy a ticket online – it gets you cocktails, wine, beer, and erotic sweets a go-go, live DJs, and a silent auction for over 200 works of erotic art from local hotties– err, we mean, artists.

9pm–midnight | $35 advance, $50 at the door | Get tickets

Tillery Street Theater
7th and Tillery • Austin, TX

Austin Earth Day Living Green Festival

 

From austinearthday.com: Earth Day is the only event celebrated simultaneously around the globe by people of all backgrounds, faiths and nationalities, raising cultural awareness of the environmental movement worldwide. AGA’s eARTh Day event is a fun, all-ages event designed to educate individuals on how to apply this new awareness and minimize their personal environmental impact.

Events include the Green Scene Scavenger Hunt, live music, art made from natural and recycled materials, the Austin Farmer’s Market, and more. Costumes encouraged!

austinearthday.com

9am-9pm | Most events free; Scavenger Hunt $10

Sunday | April 22

Lettice and Lovage

From vortexrep.org:

Lettice Douffet, an expert on medieval cuisine and weaponry, is a tour guide of Fustian House, the most boring stately home in Britain – until she begins to embellish her tours, which become more interesting as they stray further from the facts. Charlotte Schoen, sent by the Preservation Trust to investigate, is not amused and fires her. But Charlotte’s passion for history draws her to Lettice’s romantic world-view, and the two women forge an unlikely friendship in the face of their dreary modern lives. This charming and hilarious British comedy by Peter Shaffer (who also penned Equus) won two Tony awards in 1990.

For tickets call the Vortex box office, 512-478-LAVA (5282)

7pm | You pick the price: $30, $25, $20, $15

The Vortex
2307 Manor • 512-478-LAVA
