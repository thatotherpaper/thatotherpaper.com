TOP picks: June 25 - July 1

24 June 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | June 25

photo / kingsnake Guy Clark 

Guy Clark

Legendary songwriter Guy Clark plays tonight at the Union Ballroom. His body of work is pretty much untouchable in terms of emotional honesty and attention to craft. Those who have covered his songs include Johnny Cash, Vince Gill, Ricky Skaggs, Steve Wariner, and Rodney Crowell. Among his friends you’ll find all the Highwaymen, Towns Van Zandt, and Emmylou Harris. Not bad company to be in. The Union Ballroom is a beautiful old ballroom with wonderful acoustics, an intimate sit-down venue. So get there early.

7:30pm | $28

Texas Union Ballroom
24th and Guadalupe • 475-6515

Tuesday | June 26

photo / betsyjean79 

Accordion Roundup w/ Debra Peters and the Love Saints

Debra Peters, AKA the “Zydecowgirl” and the “Squeezebox Chanteuse of the Lone Star State,” headlines tonight’s monthly show at the Broken Spoke, and she’s bringing along some of her pals: the Love Saints. They invite anybody who has an accordion to bring it and play along with them. If that doesn’t sound like a party, we don’t know what does. Even better: Happy hour will be going on at the same time, so enjoy some cheap drinks while you’re there.

6pm–9pm | FREE

The Broken Spoke
3201 S Lamar Blvd • Austin, TX 78704

Wednesday | June 27

photo / Christopher Shea 

Mud

This will be your last chance to see the play Mud, by Maria Irene Fornes. Fornes is a Cuban-born playwright who has won many awards and distinctions during her career, she’s also one of Salvage Vanguard Theater’s biggest influences. So they put a lot of passion into performing Mud. The premise of the play goes like this: Mae and Loyd live in a shack at the edge of a promontory. Mae thirsts for knowledge, while Loyd is a simpleton suffering from an undiagnosed illness. But then another person shows up: Henry, also barely literate. Conflict obviously ensues.

8pm | $12–$35| Tickets

Salvage Vanguard Theater
2803 Manor • Austin, TX 78722

Thursday | June 28

But, I Could Do That

Yellow Tape Construction Company is pure fun. A group of hilarious and talented folks, these guys always put on a good show. But, I Could Do That will surely be just as impressive as their previous work.

From Yellow Tape:

A shockingly-joyous, blindingly-comical performance extravaganza featuring an original score by Cari Palazzolo – lead singer of Austin’s indie-pop sensations, Belaire. Surely this will be the most exciting Yellow Tape extravaganza to date.

8pm | $15 | Tickets

The Off Center
2211 Hidalgo St • 476-7833

Friday | June 29

The Katie O Combo

Enjoy coffee (roaster in-house) and beautiful scenery at Mozart’s Coffee as you listen to smooth jazz from The Katie O Combo. Definitely a great place to go if you’re looking for a romantic, cheap, casual date idea. From The Katie O Combo’s MySpace page:

Vocalist Katie O sings everyone’s favorite standards in her own unique style, honed from years on stage in musical theater, nightclubs and gospel choir. Statuesque describes her, tall and sensuous with a voice that floats like an evening mist. Striding away from Velour Fantasia, her Exotica Lounge Sextet, Katie graces any venue with a unique presence that is all her own.

Listen to some of The Katie O Combo’s songs on their MySpace page. (We recommend “Lullaby of Birdland.”)

8:30pm | FREE with purchase of coffee or dessert

Mozart’s Coffee Roasters
3825 Lake Austin Blvd • 477-2900

Saturday | June 30

photo / Chad Hanna 

FrankenBike

We wrote about FrankenBike last month, and we were so impressed by it that we couldn’t help making this month’s FrankenBike a TOP pick. Though it’s a free swap meet that happens every month at a different location, this one is going to be full of greatness and is definitely worth checking out – they’re going all out, with food from Wahoo’s, a quarter-pipe, bands, beer, and a ton of bikes. Bring bike parts you’re looking to trade or sell, or just come and enjoy the scene.

11am–6pm | FREE

Red 7
611 E Seventh St • 476-8100

Sunday | July 1

Sunday Under the Stars w/ DJ Abe the Assassin

Opened to honor the brave souls who ran speakeasies during Prohibition, Speakeasy is a great place to enjoy live music and hang out with friends. On Sunday nights, they host Sunday Under the Stars – only the famous terrace, Terrace 59 (named after the 59 steps you have to climb to reach it; they promise it’s worth it), is open. Tonight DJ Abe the Assassin is spinning his stuff. While you’re enjoying the beautiful view of Austin, make sure to look for the ghosts.

9pm–2am

Speakeasy
412D Congress Ave • 476-8086
