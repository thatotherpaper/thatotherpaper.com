TOP picks: Jan 7 - 13

6 January 2008 | That Other Paper

Monday | January 7

photo / ponchosqueal  

Emo’s Free Week: Day Three

Emo’s Free Week kicked off last Saturday, January 5, and it continues until Thursday, January 10. Tonight you can check out a ton of different bands playing inside, outside, and at Emo’s Lounge. Pick one or all – it’s all free. Playing inside: Scorpion Child, Big Black Smoke, Consider the Source, Panther Zora, and Cobretti. Playing outside: At All Cost, Rory and the Artificial Heart, Radioland Murders, AUX, and Monuments. Playing at the Emo’s Lounge: Pleasant Grove, Hollywood Gossip, The Easies, Many Birthdays, and History at Our Disposal.

Doors at 8pm | FREE

Emo’s
603 Red River St • Austin, TX 78701

Tuesday | January 8

A Tuna Christmas

“Austin’s Favorite Holiday Comedy Returns!” says the promotional materials. If you haven’t yet seen A Tuna Christmas, the second of three shows about the fictional Texas town of Tuna, it’s a serving of Southern-fried stagecraft cooked up by three men and performed entirely by two of them. Did somebody say, “Costume change”? This is the first night it’s playing on its current run, which lasts until the 13th. So… y’know… just in case you weren’t tired of Christmas yet.

8pm | $20–$40 (based on seating)

Paramount Theatre
713 Congress Ave • Austin, TX 78701

Wednesday | January 9

Jon Gries Night

Jon Gries of Uncle Rico and Lost fame is hosting a Jon Gries bad film fest at Alamo Downtown. Napoleon Dynamite, Real Genius Quote-Along, and TerrorVision will be screened followed by an audience Q&A. All three will set you back $60, but you can choose to attend just one of the screenings.

7pm | $60

Alamo Downtown
320 E 6th St • Austin, TX 78701

Thursday | January 10

River City Pops presents Now and Then

From Do512.com: River City Pops (RCP), Austin’s popular song and dance ensemble, is pleased to announce their upcoming production. Now and Then will take audiences on a musical journey that will lift spirits of all ages with high-energetic performances that include the music styles of Latin, Jazz, Pop, Swing and Rock ‘n roll. This group’s eclectic music selection is sure to please all kinds of music lovers no matter how finicky they are. So come celebrate with us in the New Year and bring your family, that special loved one or date.

7pm | $15/General Admission, $10/Students, seniors, and groups of 10 or more, $7/Children under 12

Reagan High School
7105 Berkman Dr • Austin, TX 78752

Friday | January 11

Pink Floyd Laser Spectacular

You like lasers and you like Pink Floyd, so it stands to reason that you’ll love the Pink Floyd Laser Spectacular. In 1986, a San Antonio laserist (how cool would it be to be a laserist?) approached Paramount Organization producer Steve Monistere about putting together a laser show. The result is the longest-running theater show in history (18 years). You can watch a preview of the show here and you can get your tickets here.

8pm | $25

Austin Music Hall
208 Nueces • Austin, TX 78701

Saturday | January 12

BCP Hike and Lecture

Located just north of Lake Travis above Cypress Creek, you’ll find the Balcones Canyonlands Preserve. The preserve protects endangered species such as the Golden-cheeked Warbler – as well as clear canyon streams, and rare indigenous plants. They schedule events through out the year, but this is first on of 2008. From 8am–11am, the preserve offers a guided winter hike of Crossings Preserve for free. There are only 25 spots, so you will want to reserve yourself a spot early. The lecture will be given by conservation and land use experts and will inform you about the area and the preserves system in general. As far as additional recommendations, “Sturdy footgear is recommended. Drinking water and other concessions are not available so please plan to bring your own. Sun block and hats are recommended. Please feel free to bring binoculars and/or photographic cameras. No pets are permitted within the preserve.”

If you’re interested, contact Gail McGlamery at gail.mcglamery@ci.austin.tx.us or 263-6437.

8am | Free, but you have to register

Balcones Canyonlands Preserve
The Crossing 13500 FM 2769 • Austin, TX 78726

Sunday | January 13

courtesy Fishboy Fishboy 

Peel w/ Poison Control Center and Fishboy

That Other Paper writer Adam Avramescu leads a not-so-secret life as a member of the Denton, TX quartet Fishboy. Along with Ames, Iowa’s excellent Poison Control Center, they will be opening up for Austin’s own Peel, who’s about to embark on a tour. Wish them a fond farewell, and see one of That Other Paper’s own make a complete ass of himself on stage. All ages welcome!

10pm | Tickets sold at the door

Mohawk
912 Red River St. • Austin, TX 78701
