Tuesday | July 31

GG Allin Premier w/ The Good Looks and a GG Allin lookalike contest

From the promoter:
Rarely performing for more than 10-20 minutes before clubs shut him down, Allin usually took the stage in a jockstrap and wound up nude; he beat himself bloody with broken bottles, torn cans, and microphones (when he wasn’t trying to shove the latter up his own ass); he attacked and was attacked by his own audience; he urinated on the stage, on his band, on the audience; he frequently took laxatives before shows in order to defecate on the stage, after which he generally ate his own feces or threw them at the audience; and yet, somehow, he still found audience members willing to perform oral sex on him on-stage. Needless to say, he also ingested enormous amounts of booze and drugs, served several prison terms, and even promised to commit suicide on-stage on Halloween (he died of an overdose in 1993 before he could follow through). His songs – such as they were – were comically over-the-top hell-raisers about substance abuse, casual violence, and sexual conduct that was at best degrading and at worst criminal. Yet no matter how vile he was, Allin had his supporters, whether they belonged to the outermost fringes of society, admired his absolute freedom of expression, or were simply fascinated by the spectacle of a man venting all the ugliest, most primal impulses of the human id.

7pm | NO COVER

Beerland
711 Red River St • 479-7625

Sunday | August 5

photo / secretlyironic 

A Skull in Connemara

Irishman Martin McDonagh is an accomplished playwright: He’s the only playwright on earth to have had four plays running simultaneously in London since Willy Shakes himself, and he’s also won several Tony Awards. A Skull in Connemara constitutes the second part of McDonagh’s Leenane Trilogy. This blackly humorous play follows grave digger Mick Dowd, who has the unsavory job of exhuming seven-year-old corpses to make room for fresh bodies. One of the old graves he exhumes belongs to his long dead wife. The exhumation gets the town talking again and speculating on Dowd’s role in her death. This is your last night to see the play, so get tickets in advance.

6pm | $6

Dougherty Arts Center
1110 Barton Springs Rd • Austin, TX 78704
