TOP picks: Oct 29 - Nov 4

29 October 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | October 29

Robert De Niro Film Series

photo / Daquella Manera 

As a part of the De Niro Film Series, The Harry Ransom Center will show Kenneth Branagh’s 1994 adaptation of Mary Shelly’s Frankenstein. This is the perfect way to get geared up for Halloween. The best part about the event is that it’s free, though seating is limited. So get there early.

7pm | FREE

Harry Ransom Center
21 and Guadalupe • Austin, TX 78705

Other options:

  • Life of Brian at Cafe Mundi | 8:00pm
  • The Swells Wave at Lucky Lounge | 10pm

Tuesday | October 30

photo / jonsterm 

Everybody Loves a Clown Tour: Atmosphere with special guests Mac Lethal, Grayskul, and Luckyiam

Atmosphere has been making hip-hop music since 1997. And he hasn’t let up on his quality control. Hailing from Minneapolis, Atmosphere defies the East coast/West coast hip-hop paradigm, edifying listeners with lyrics that are thoughtful, clearly expressed, and full of deft rhymes – not to mention a sense of humor.

10pm | $15

Emo’s
603 Red River • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Austin Ghost Tour at Moonshine | 8 and 9pm
  • Chicken Strut at Continental Club | 10pm

Wednesday | October 31

photo / brynlanyon 

Halloween with the Dandy Warhols

It’s Halloween! And you have as many options of what to do as there will be crazy costumes on 6th Street. If you’re not heading downtown to participate in the craziness on the street or going to a house party, check out the Dandy Warhols Costume Party at La Zona Rosa. There will be music, a costume contest, and $3 Buds for those who dress up. Buy your tickets ahead of time since they’re likely to sell out.

9pm | $25

La Zona Rosa
612 W 4th St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Rocky Horror Picture Show at Alamo Village | 10:15pm
  • Austin Haunted Forest Halloween Show Extravaganza austinhauntedforest.com | 9pm
  • Galactic’s Galabratory: A Multimedia Halloween Experience ( Galactic ft. Chali 2na, Mr. Lif, and Lifesavas) at Stubb’s | $24

Thursday | November 1

courtesy of Art on 5th 

First Thursday at the West End Galleries

Recently, several fine art galleries decided to dedicate the first Thursday of every month to public art viewing. Encouraging patrons with free wine, appetizers, and conversation, the galleries see their First Thursday as a more mature alternative to South Congress. The following galleries are all within walking distance and usually have free parking:

  • Art on 5th | 1501 W Fifth St; 481-1111
  • Stephen L. Clark Gallery | 1101 W Sixth St; 477-0828
  • F8 Fine Art Gallery | 1137 W Sixth St; 480-0242
  • Wally Workman Gallery | 1202 W Sixth St; 472-7428
  • Artworks | 1214 W Sixth St; 472-1550
  • Austin Galleries | 1219 W Sixth St; 495-9363

6–8pm | FREE

West End Galleries
Downtown • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • Peter Himmelman at The Texas Union | 830pm
  • Tuna Does Vegas at The Paramount | 8pm

Friday | November 2

photo / matthewfugel 

The Smashing Pumpkins

Tonight, tonight the Smashing Pumpkins are playing at The Backyard. Sorry for the terrible joke, but there it is. They’re back together and better than ever. Tickets are expensive but totally worth it. I mean, dude – it’s the Pumpkins. Buy your tickets here.

8pm | $49.50

The Backyard
13101 Highway 71 W • Austin, TX 78738

Other options:

  • Robbers on High Street, Great Northern, Til We’re Blue or Destroy, Goodbye Girl at Club de Ville | 9pm
  • B Scene at Blanton Museum of Art | 6pm

Saturday | November 3

Fun Fun Fun Fest

photo / pelcinary 

Last year Fun Fun Fun Fest went smashingly well and everyone had a good time. You can tell because this year’s line-up is phenomenal. The acts range from indie rock to punk to hip-hop to DJs, so there’s a little bit of everything for everyone. Set up at Waterloo Park the acts will play on multiple stages, ensuring fans a more singular experience while allowing them to hear a wide range of music.

1pm | $30 for one day; $54 for two days

Waterloo Park
403 E 15th St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • Rocky Horror Picture Show at Alamo Village | 11:55pm
  • Dirty Dozen Brass Band at Antone’s | 9pm

Sunday | November 4

Master Pancake Theater: Pump Up the Volume

Join the Pancake gang as they dig their satirical claws into the ’90s classic Pump Up the Volume, in which Christian Slater as a pirate radio DJ who begins to enthrall his friends and infuriate his high school principal when he speaks his mind.

7pm | $12.50

Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
320 E 6th St • Austin, TX 78701

Other options:

  • $1.75 domestic pints at The Draught House Pub | All Day
  • Gospel Brunch with the Bells of Joy at Stubb’s | 11am, 1pm
