TOP picks: Sept 10-16

10 September 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | September 10

Elizabeth Edwards at BookPeople

Elizabeth Edwards 

Elizabeth Edwards, wife of politician John Edwards, will be discussing and signing copies of her new book Saving Graces tonight. The book details her battle with breast cancer, her experiences growing up in the military, the struggles of being a politician’s wife, and the tragic death of her 16-year-old son.

8pm–10pm | FREE

BookPeople
603 N Lamar Blvd • 78703

Other options:

  • Plutonium Farmers at Emo’s | 10pm
  • Aaron Beaumont at Ruta Maya | 7:30pm
  • Natalie Zoe at Opal Divine’s | 7pm

Tuesday | September 11

Iraqnophobia

Today, as no one needs reminding, is the sixth anniversary of the September 11 attacks. To commemorate the attacks, the Salvage Vanguard Theater is performing Iraqnophobia, a jazz orchestra suite by Alex Coke.

Iraqnophobia 

From the Creative Opportunity Orchestra site:

Originally recorded and released on the VoxLox label in 2005, Iraqnophobia “[is a) deep meditation on human rights, peace, and social justice. For all (its) sensuous aesthetic, this composition is also a critical works of musical citizenship. Like Alan Pogue’s stunning photographs (included in the CD package), it directly confronts the brutal circumstances of life in war zones. (It) causes us to ask about the pain and misery of these places, and in doing so advocate(s) for compassion and human dignity,” says Steven Feld, Professor of Anthropology and Music at the University of New Mexico.

8pm | $10

Salvage Vanguard Theater
2803 Manor Road • 447-7825

Other options:

  • Hilary York and Aimee Bobruk at the Scoot Inn | 8pm
  • Transactions at the Blanton Museum of Art | 10am–5pm

Wednesday | September 12

Monty Python and the Holy Grail Quote-Along

photo / antikris 

Anyone who’s into Monty Python no doubt has their own particular lines they relish quoting. And now it’s perfectly okay – you don’t have to mouth the words in quiet agony for fear of catching evil glances from fellow moviegoers. They’ll be passing out various props: coconut shells and rabbits’ feet. The show will start with a Walk the Bridge of Death game. So what’s your favorite color? Red. No, blue – ahhhhhhhhhhh.

9:45pm | $8

512-Go!» Alamo Drafthouse Village
2700 W Anderson Ln • 476-1320

Other options:

  • Screen Door Film screening of Four Eyed Monster at Salvage Vanguard Theater | 7:30pm
  • Dougherty Art exhibit: Serious Fun | 9am–9pm
  • DJ Melee live at Beauty Bar | 10pm

Thursday | September 13

Pirate Radio USA screening

photo / alfaltendorf 

Screen Door Films has been screening short films, feature length independent films, and documentaries in a variety of venues in Austin since 2003. The ongoing screening series are curated so that only the highest quality films from Austin and film festivals are shown. These screenings are a good chance to catch rarely seen films. Regarding Pirate Radio USA, here’s what Screen Door has to say:

Pirate Radio USA is a feature-length digital documentary about the underground world of illegal radio in America, where people play what they want and say what they want—unless the FCC catches them…

DJs Him and Her, from their live Pirate Radio USA Studio, take you on a rock ’n’ roll journey inside rogue radio stations across the country to see why Americans defy Federal Law to free the radio airwaves.

See the battle to free the airwaves live – and find out the real price of freedom. It’s not about Left vs. Right, it’s about Big vs. Small.

7:30pm | $6

Salvage Vanguard Theater
2803 Manor Road • 447-7825

Other options:

  • Best Little Whorehouse in Texas 25th anniversary screening at the Paramount Theater | 7pm
  • Spoon, Sound Team, Alright Tonight at La Zona Rosa | 9pm
  • Roald Dahl’s 90th Birthday Bash at Book People | 6pm

Friday | September 14

Austin City Limits Music Festival

You all knew it was coming, and now it’s here. You’ll want to check out the schedule so you don’t miss out on any of your favorite Friday acts. You can also browse our ACL Previews to read interviews with some of the bands. Three-day passes are sold out, so your best bet might be browsing the Tickets section on Craigslist.

photo / Victoria Welch 

You ARE allowed to bring the following items into the festival:

Blankets, umbrellas (small hand-held style), soft-sided coolers, baby Strollers, portable/collapsible chair (individual units only; no “double-wide” connecting chairs), regular-size/unframed backpacks, two factory sealed water bottles up to 1 liter each, empty Camelbak or other empty plastic container (ie, Nalgene bottle), ice in soft-sided cooler or Camelbak, binoculars, personal camera equipment (i.e. small digital cameras or point-and-click 35mm cameras). Cameras with any type of detachable lenses are prohibited.

You ARE NOT allowed to bring the following items in to the festival:

Weapons of any kind, illegal substances (including narcotics) or drug paraphernalia, framed or large backpacks, alcohol (alcohol will be sold at the Festival), glass containers, food or beverages (except for factory-sealed water bottles), skateboards, scooters, or personal motorized vehicles Bicycles inside Festival grounds (bike racks are available near each entrance), carts, large chains or spiked jewelry, fireworks, explosives, large umbrellas, non-collapsible chairs, hard-sided coolers, tents, pets (except service dogs), video equipment, professional still camera equipment (no detachable lenses, no tripods, big zooms, or commercial use rigs), audio recording equipment, no illegal vending is permitted. No unauthorized/unlicensed vendors allowed.

11am–10pm

Zilker Park
2100 Barton Springs Rd • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • Former President Bill Clinton speaks about his new book Giving at Book People | 11am
  • MIA after-party at Beauty Bar | 10pm
  • Screen Door Films screening of The Outdoorsman: blood, sweat, and beers at Salvage Vanguard Theater | 7:30pm

Saturday | September 15

Enchanted Forest’s ACL after party

Groovin’ on Your Grave 

It’s September, and that means we’re just weeks away from Halloween. And that means the the Haunted Forest, one of the most fun things there is to do in late October (besides destroying your child’s pumpkin), is about to take over the Enchanted Forest. To help raise money for the Haunted Forest, the Enchanted Forest is holding an ACL after party tonight – come dance to music from DJ Manny, The Electric Mudd, Nine Dollar Word, and more.

9pm–3am | $6

The Enchanted Forest
1412 Oltorf St • Austin, TX

Other options:

  • The Derailers at the Broken Spoke | 7pm
  • Start Trekkin’ at the Hideout | 8pm
  • Gary Hood w/ Trey Galyon at the Velveeta Room | 9:30pm and 11:30pm

Sunday | September 16

photo / Bill Wilcox  

Gore Gore Girls

On their website, Gore Gore Girls are described as “a meeting of the Stooges and The Ronettes.” This garage-rock foursome from Detroit might be the cure for your Sunday night sadness if you’re without tickets to the White Stripes show at Stubb’s or if you didn’t have an ACL pass.

10:30pm | FREE

512-Go!» Beauty Bar
617 E 7th • 391-1943

Other options:

  • ACL after party at Red Seven | 9pm
  • Jeff Helmer Trio | 7pm
  • Silver Lining flat lining: Michael Wutz at Volitant Gallery | Noon–6pm
