TOP picks: March 19-25

18 March 2007 | That Other Paper

Monday | March 19

photo / Michael Fajardo 

Logo Pint Night

No need to bring a purse (or man-bag) big enough to carry out that sweet glass — tonight at Ginger Man, everyone who buys a beer is welcome to take the glass home with them.

6pm until glasses run out

Ginger Man Pub
214 W 4th St • Austin, TX 78701

Tuesday | March 20

Transformers: The Movie

The new Transformers movie isn’t out until this summer. But tonight at the Drafthouse they’ll be playing the original flick from 1986… which takes place in 2005. Expect evil robots, a buttload of explosions, and a hilariously futuristic depiction of 2005.

7pm | $5.50–$7 | Tickets

512-Go!» Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
409 Colorado St. • 476-1320

Wednesday | March 21

photo / Mark Setchell 

Dare to Fly

Peter Gold, self-described “fear transcender,” wants to teach you how to fly trapeze. It doesn’t matter if you’ve never done it before or if you’re the Bearded Lady – just put on clean socks, tight-fitting clothes, and don’t wear any hand lotion or jewelry. Kids under six can even take the class so long as they’re with an adult at all times.

3pm–5pm | $75 or purchase a Frequent Flyer Card that’s good for five classes ($300) | Call ahead for registration: 877.844.3003

The Crossings
13500 FM 2769 • 512.258.7243 ext.4101

Thursday | March 22

photo / courtesy Vehicular 

Vehicular

Jeff, Patrick, and Gary make up local hip-hop trio Vehicular, and they’re hosting their weekly show tonight at Red Fez. Their sound is a little old skool, with no shortage of witty and sometimes hilarious lyrics – and, yeah, these white rappers have been told they’re just like the Beastie Boys at least a jillion times. Stick around after Vehicular’s set for Nuthin’ New, where DJs spin everything from En Vogue to Ice Cube in hope you’ll get on the dance floor.

9pm | NO COVER

Red Fez
209 W 5th St • 78701

Friday | March 23

photo / Val Brown 

The Gathering

Courtney DiSabato from the Austin Fine Arts Alliance and Sean Gaulager from Cantanker Magazine and Volitant Gallery present The Gathering, a collaborative effort between local artists and urban developments.

From the promoter: This exhibition focuses on the relationship of artists to one another and to the broadening Austin community. The artists’ collectives in this show work together seamlessly, inspired by the multiplicity of ideas that come from collaboration, while other artists challenge traditional ideas about media by creating works that use a range of novel materials. Collectively considered and highly regarded, for these artists, convergence and communion is the ultimate goal.

Anything you see at The Gathering – including the entire building – is for sale.

6pm | FREE

Duplex Town Home by Skyline CityHomes
5412-B McCandless • 78756

Saturday | March 24

The Boys Next Door

The City Theatre is relatively new, but they’ve been off to a great start so far – The Boys Next Door is the latest reason to stop by the intimate East Side theater. Boys is Tom Griffin’s comedy about four mentally handicapped men and Jack, their social worker.

8pm | $15; $10 for students

The City Theatre
3823 Airport Blvd, Suite D • 512.524.2870

Sunday | March 25

photo / jonathan 

Where The Wild Things Are

From the promoter: Juror Randy Jewart, noted Austin-based sculptor and founder/director of Austin Green Arts, has selected 19 Austin artists to display 29 works that explore the unique artistic perspectives of native Texas habitats, ecosystems, and wildflowers.

12pm–5pm | $7; $6 for students and seniors

Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Center
4801 La Crosse Ave, Suite D • Austin, TX 78739
