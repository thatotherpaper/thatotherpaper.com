Margaritas on me, guys

11 January 2007 | Chuck McDoddleson
photo / Ana Ulin 

Are you chumps ready? Seriously, tell me if you won’t be able to hang tonight. We’re going hard, and we’re going long – this is going to be a drinking marathon. I won’t look down on you if you leave the bar now. This night is going to separate the men from the boys.

Margaritas on me, guys.

Here you go, Zach. Can you handle it? You’re going to hear about it for months if you puke. Timmy-dog, take your glass. Need me to pour some of it out? I just don’t want to waste any. Let me know if I have to finish the last half for you. Adam Bomb! Here it is, brother – the start of a great evening. Adam is the only one who can handle my pace. Seriously, dude, you’re a machine. Remember South Padre two years ago? Man! That was some intense drinking. Cheers to that, buddy.

Timbo! You stepped up to the plate, homeboy. Don’t burn out too quickly – we’ve got at least four hours of boozing ahead of us. I’m not carrying you home. Take a page out of Zach’s book – slow and steady wins the race. But not too slow; I’m buying round two when I finish this. Just a few more sips to go.

Nice work, Adam! You killed that drink like a champ. You and Zach are tied for second. I’ll finish mine on the way to the bar. Get ready for numero dos, fellas.

Did someone say Cape Cods? Grab ’em while they’re hot, boys.

Tequila shots? Nah, man – this is way stronger. Yeah, the bartender here knows me. She hooks me up – these are like half vodka. And not well vodka, either. This is top-shelf stuff. But that doesn’t mean we’re going to sip them. Let’s go, guys. Bottoms up. I want to hit thirdsies before midnight.

Dude, thirdsies is not a chick word. Shut up, Adam. You weren’t complaining when we were going thirdsies in Padre two years ago. Just finish your drink. I’ve got next round. I’m going to put some hair on your pre-pubescent chests.

Welcome to the next level, amigos – grab your Cuba Libres. Girl drink? You wouldn’t be saying that if you saw how much rum went into these. We are going to be drunk when these glasses are empty, my friends.

You ever been drunk, Tim? You should try it sometime. It’s a cool feeling. You have to actually finish a drink to experience it, though.

Dude – I did finish that last one. There was like one mouthful left in the glass when I put it on the bar. It was mostly backwash. I didn’t want to completely blow you guys out. Whatever, Zach. You’re just jealous of all the attention the hot bartender has been giving me.

photo / Doug Bowman 

Remember that one bartender in Padre, Adam? The one I was talking to all night? No, it wasn’t just because I was buying drinks. She said she liked my smile. Well, I would’ve gotten her number if I hadn’t been so wasted. Speaking of wasted – who’s up for round four?

Wait – why is the bartender walking over here with shots? Did one of you order a round while I was at the bar? What the hell, Zach? I mean, yeah, I’ll drink it. It’s got alcohol in it, right? I just thought this was going to be a Major League night. Whiskey shots are for pee-wee games. Is your dad going to hold the shot glass for you? I think I see your grandpa cheering from the bleachers.

Alright, alright – let’s get this over with. One, two, three: Shot! That was rough. That really burns. You look like you just bit into a lemon, Tim. You okay, Zach? Adam is just getting started. Remember the tequila shots we did on the beach in Padre, Adam? You know – when I puked on that girl’s beach towel and then fell onto it? Man. That was a great week.

Dude, I did not pour my shot out. I don’t know what the puddle on the floor is. It’s probably blood from all the drinks I’ve killed tonight. Why would I dump whiskey on the ground? Whatever – enough of these children’s games. I’m about to give you all a diploma from the University of Chugging Liquor. I hope you brought your caps and gowns. We’re going to graduate magna cum boozed tonight. I’ll be right back.

Onward and upward, comrades: I come bearing Fuzzy Navels.

Dude, this is not a chick drink. These are basically all alcohol. I told you, man – this bartender is hooking me up. She seriously poured like half a bottle of Schnapps into these drinks. I’m getting drunk just looking at mine. Fine, Zach, don’t drink it. But you owe me $9. What the hell, Tim? This is the last time Adam and I invite you guys out. I knew you would flake out in the clutch.

This place is dead, Adam. Let’s finish our drinks and find a new bar. I’ll tell you what – at the next place, the first round of Flirtinis are on me.
