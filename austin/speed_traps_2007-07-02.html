Speed traps for July 2-6

1 July 2007 | Todd Ross Nienkerk

The following is a list of speed traps (“traffic enforcement locations”) around Austin for the week of July 2–6, 2007.

The Austin Police Department sez: Traffic safety is a major focus of the Austin Police Department, and a substantial amount of resources is committed to traffic safety improvements and traffic enforcement. Traffic enforcement is a primary function of APD motor units and one of the main functions of patrol officers. In addition, APD schedules officers each week on overtime, funded by grants, to enforce traffic laws. The goal of traffic enforcement is to increase citizens’ voluntary compliance with traffic laws.

Monday, July 2

  • Morning: 900 block of Springdale Road
  • Morning: Manchaca Road
  • Morning: 1500 block of Rio Grande Street
  • Morning: 3900 block of Spicewood Springs Road
  • Afternoon: 5300 block of Emerald Forest Drive

Tuesday, July 3

  • Morning: 8500 block of North Capital of Texas Highway
  • Morning: 800 to 1000 blocks of West Cesar Chavez Street
  • Morning: 2600 block of East Martin Luther King Boulevard
  • Afternoon: Slaughter Lane
  • Afternoon: 2100 to 2500 blocks of South Pleasant Valley Road

Wednesday, July 4

  • Afternoon: 4400 to 5800 blocks of East Riverside Drive
  • Afternoon: 700 block of West Stassney Lane
  • Morning: 13000 block of Highway 183 North
  • Morning: 2400 to 2500 blocks of Red River Street
  • Morning: 400 block of Pleasant Valley Road

Thursday, July 5

  • Morning: 1800 block of West 5th Street
  • Morning: Riverside Drive
  • Morning: 100 to 4500 blocks of Airport Boulevard
  • Afternoon: Convict Hill Road
  • Afternoon: 1400 to 2800 blocks of South I-35

Friday, July 6

  • Morning: South First Street
  • Morning: 7400 block of Loyola Lane
  • Morning: 800 block of West Slaughter Lane
  • Morning: 1700 block of West Cesar Chavez Street
  • Afternoon: 5300 block of West Parmer Lane

Traffic tip from the APD: What should you do when you are stuck in traffic and late for an appointment or work? Just be late. Being late for an appointment or work is better than endangering your life or the life of someone else. Arriving late is much better than not arriving at all.

Courtesy Austin Police Department. Released June 29, 2007. Photo by JimmJamm77.

 

APD offers FREE VIN etching

The Austin Police Department will etch your vehicle identification number (VIN) on for FREE at various locations around Austin through June and July. Here’s the schedule:

  • July 11 Wal-Mart at 9300 S. I-35, 10am to 1pm
  • July 21 Dolores Catholic Church (1111 Montopolis Drive), 10am to 2pm
  • July 24 The University of Texas, Denius Field, 2200 Red River Street (Lot 41), 10am to 1pm
  • July 26 Target at 5621 N. I-35, 10am to 1pm
  • July 28 Target Greatland, 5300 South MoPac, 8am to 12pm

More details can be found on Austin’s ONLY Blog.
