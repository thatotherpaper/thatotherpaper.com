Dating advice from Jimmy Suede

13 December 2006 | Jimmy Suede
Boom! 

Dear Jimmy,

BOOM!

You mention in your level eight eBook, What’s Your Sign: The Art of the Pickup Line with Jimmy Suede, that an opening line should convey brute masculinity and an insatiable appetite for lust. There’s a woman at work that I’m really attracted to, but I’m afraid of coming on too strong and becoming known as a “player” around the office. My second-worst fear is being fired because my boss is intimidated by my overwhelming sexual appeal. My first is spiders. Should I water down my approach to avoid getting a reputation around the office?

Tim from Westlake

Boom, Tim!

You raise a good point, and your question inspired me to make some changes in my life. I traded my brand-new Porsche Carrera for a 1984 Dodge Defender with a spider-web crack across the entire windshield so that I can conceal my wild success as a seduction coach. I also marred my face with a hammer and gained 60 pounds so no one will catch on to the fact that I’m incredibly good looking. Finally, I transformed all of my expensive jeans into Capri pants and gave myself a perm. I’ll be mortified if someone actually mistakes me for a man.

In case you can’t tell, Tim, I’m being facetious.

Your masculinity is your greatest asset. Do you think being an Alpha Male is only useful when you’re in a seedy, degenerate-filled liquor barn? Do only morally flexible women respond well to a man who takes charge and calls the shots? Here’s a news flash, Tim: Being top dog is a lifestyle strategy, not a dating trick. You need to step up and celebrate your desires.

Wear your libido like a badge of honor. Toning your virility down to attract a woman is like amputating one of your legs to run a marathon. You’ve been presented with a golden opportunity here, Tim: Not only can you utilize your manhood to attract a woman, but you can also establish dominance amongst your co-workers and leverage their fear of you for a promotion.

I say you broadcast your masculinity like a natural disaster warning. Instead of asking this woman out on a date, rip your shirt off and bark maniacally. Jump from desk to desk in your office, screaming non-sequiturs and catapulting computer monitors in every direction. Scare and confuse your co-workers; keep them on their toes, constantly dreading an unpredictable outburst of violence and rage.

The world of seduction is a lot like the world of competitive drag racing, Tim: It’s fast-paced, dangerous, and the biggest scores are made in burnt-out industrial districts. Make your intentions clear and never apologize for them – that’s what separates us from the beasts. BOOM!

You are a GOD, Jimmy! Boom!

I broke up with my girlfriend recently, and I can’t seem to get over it. I know you advise immediately ending a relationship once it hits the three-month milestone in your level sixteen eBook, Cut and Run: Keeping Your Game Sharp with Jimmy Suede, but I dated my ex for almost nine months and became really attached. How can I get over her and become interested in other women?

Art from Pflugerville

BOOM, Art!

I’ve got bad news: You’re afflicted with a terrible case of what I call One-itis. Being obsessed over one woman, you’ve forfeited your right to ever consider yourself a player or brag about your skill with women. I am obligated by the seduction code to help you out of your abject predicament, but don’t think for a second that I wish you well or want you to get over your ex. In fact, I am utterly disgusted with you and hope desperately that you fail.

That said, overcoming One-itis is surprisingly simple. Think of your ex-girlfriend as a car – she may get great gas mileage and even have ice-cold A/C, but you can find something you’ll like better if you open yourself up to new features. Perhaps you would have enjoyed a moonroof more than your ex’s automatic seatbelts? What if a navigation system would have been more convenient than a six-disc CD changer?

Have you ever sat on heated seats? Picture this: You awake to a below-freezing temperature with a negative windchill on an icy January morning. You eat breakfast and stand in your doorway, bracing yourself for the frozen air you’re about to step into. You briskly jog to your car, start the engine, and are engulfed in the fantastic warmth emanating from your sophisticated captain’s chair.

That, Art, is what I call marriage material.

The point is, you’re only hung up on your ex because you have yet to experience another combination of amenities that you’ll appreciate on your daily commute. It’s pretty closed-minded to think that you could only be happy driving one specific car. Why do you think I lease?

BOOM!

About the author Jimmy Suede has authored 42 eBooks on dating and relationships. Through years of meticulous trial-and-error, Jimmy has developed a keen insight into the dynamics of dating that he dispenses in his column and at workshops and seminars across the country.
