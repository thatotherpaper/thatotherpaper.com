Been there, seen that: Part 3

20 March 2007 | Bradley Jackson
Armed with a platinum badge and a cinematic prowess that would impress both Ebert and Roeper, That Other Paper writer Bradley Jackson offers you his opinion on the best (and worst) films that SXSW had to offer

Reign Over Me

courtesy Sony Pictures Reign Over Me 

Probably the highest profile film of the entire festival, Reign Over Me stars Adam Sandler and Don Cheadle. This funny yet heartbreaking drama is about grief, friendship, and forgiveness. Featuring winning performances by both actors (especially Sandler – this is his toughest role to date), the film is able to maintain a healthy balance of comedy and drama while never really getting cheesy. There’s even a part in the film where Sandler delivers a two-page monologue about his family. Sandler cries in this scene, and it doesn’t seem forced – he nails it. I only wish writer/director Mike Binder had structured the screenplay better, seeing as how many of the scenes (especially towards the end) feel disjointed and stilted. Nonetheless, in a time where sequels, remakes, and movies where Eddie Murphy is in a fat suit rule the multiplex, it’s refreshing to see an original story with fantastic performances and compelling themes.

Reign Over Me
7 / 10

Confessions of a Superhero

photo / Penningtron This isn’t from the movie, but it sure is funny 

When it comes to documentaries, it seems there’s no point in watching one unless it involves a political agenda, an ex-vice president, or thousands of adorable penguins. Confessions of a Superhero proves to be the caveat to that rule. Following the lives of four struggling actors who make their living portraying photo-op ready superheroes in front of Grauman’s Chinese Theater, Confessions of a Superhero is a fascinating character study and a compelling look into our society’s obsession with superheroes. The filmmakers send us along with four characters (Superman, Batman, Wonder Woman, and the Incredible Hulk) as they struggle to maintain their identity and sanity while they try to live the Hollywood dream. Humorous but never mocking, Confessions of a Superhero is a documentary that makes you root for the man (or woman) behind the mask.

Confessions of a Superhero
9 / 10

Undead or Alive: A Zombedy

Zombie movies are awesome. Western movies are awesome. But Chris Kattan movies – they usually suck. These were exactly my thoughts before watching Undead or Alive: A Zombedy. However, despite the presence of the former chair-humping SNL alum, the movie is actually a decent zombie comedy. It’s by no means great, but the presence of Brian Posehn and Upright Citizens Brigade founder Matt Besser – along with some over-the-top zombie violence – help elevate this film from Corky Romano to a funny, if uneven, midnight movie. And as much as I like to rag on Chris Kattan, he’s actually pretty funny in this flick.

Undead or Alive: A Zombedy
6 / 10
