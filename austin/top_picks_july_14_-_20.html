TOP picks: July 14 - 20

14 July 2008 | That Other Paper

Monday | July 14

Karaoke Underground

You won’t find your typical karaoke hits at The Parish tonight – LCD Soundsystem, Belle and Sebastian, and Joan Jett are more the style at Karaoke Underground. You can see their songlist here.

8pm | FREE

The Parish
214 E 6th St bull; Austin, TX

Tuesday | July 15

Psycho Beach Party

courtesy Alamo Drafthouse 

This is the second Hey Homo! event the Drafthouse has done, and this time the movie is Psycho Beach Party, starring Six Feet Under’s Lauren Ambrose. From the Drafthouse:

This film is a perfect mix of 1960s surfer movies and 1980s slasher flicks, so dress accordingly or be called out by MC Rebecca Havemeyer! Now we can’t bring you a beach, but expect an Air Surfing Competition, Hawaiian Leis, special drinks, performances and a zillion awesome queer folk!

10pm | $10 | Get tickets

Alamo Drafthouse at the Ritz
320 E 6th St • Austin, TX

Wednesday | July 16

James Hunter 

James Hunter

James Hunter describes himself as “an overnight sensation that’s taken 20 years.” This native Brit, whose sound has been likened to Aretha Franklin, Los Lonely Boys, Boz Scaggs, and Van Morrison, released his first album, People Gonna Talk, in the US in 2006 after writing and performing in Britain for 20 years. The album garnered a Grammy nomination, and his second album, The Hard Way, came out last month. Get tickets here.

9pm | $15

Antone’s
213 W 5th St • Austin, TX

Thursday | July 17

Kidnapped by Craigslist

Craigslist has provided us endless entertainment over the last few years, and we don’t really know what we’d do if we couldn’t read the Missed Connections or put up our junk for sale (and subsequently have a million people flake the fuck out and never show up to buy our vintage ceramic cat garden sculpture and matching queen headboard). Oh, Craigslist. Anyway, Kidnapped by Craigslist is a comedy that “delves deep into the online phenomenon that is Craigslist.” More from the promoter:

Want to buy a couch? Find a date? Complain about your roommate? Your boss? Your sketchy neighbor that sleeps in his doghouse? Kidnapped by Craigslist is a unique new comedy that explores the carnivalesque, cult-like phenomenon that is Craigslist. Combining actual postings from the Austin website with scripted material, the show tells the true stories locked inside an online community that has shaped and inspired today’s internet generation. Adapted exclusively for Shrewd Productions and the Austin stage, Kidnapped by Craigslist had a successful 2007 New York City engagement at the acclaimed People’s Improv Theater.

8pm | $12–$25

Blue Theater
916 Springdale Rd • Austin, TX

Friday | July 18

Punchline

Punchline 

Punchline is a stand-up comedy show hosted every Friday night at ColdTowne Theater (map) and sponsored by That Other Paper. The show consists of about 10 comics who each get four minutes onstage. It’s a good time, dude.

10pm | $5 at the door

512-Go!» ColdTowne Theater | map
4803 Airport Blvd (same building as I Luv Video) • 524-2807

Saturday | July 19

The Longest Day of the Year

courtesy Art Palace 

Art Palace’s summer group exhibition features new work by Jonathan Marshall, Erin Curtis, Jules Buck Jones, and Erick Michaud.

Noon–5pm

Art Palace
2109 E Cesar Chavez St. • Austin, TX

Sunday | July 20

[adult swim] Dethklok Before and After Party

Even though we don’t really understand what Aqua Teen Hunger Force is about anymore, we can’t say no to [adult swim], especially an [adult swim] party here in Austin. They’re promising music, performances, comedy, and a pool, with $2 tallboys and $3 frozen margaritas. [sweet].

8pm | FREE

Tiniest Bar in Texas
817 W 5th Street • Austin, TX
