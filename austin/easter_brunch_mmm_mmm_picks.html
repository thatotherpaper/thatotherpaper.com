Easter brunch: Mmm mmm picks

18 March 2008 | That Other Paper
photo / Sam Pullara FYI: This photo rules 

Maybe you’re all about Jesus, maybe you just want the extreme thrill of finding some plastic eggs stuffed with candy and/or coin, maybe you’re a bit of both of those things. Or, hey, maybe you just want an excuse to stuff your face and not have to do any dishes. It’s none of our business, really. Still, we’d like to suggest a few ideas if you are in fact looking for somewhere to celebrate Easter that involves feasting on delicious eats…

photo / denver935 Brunch at Moonshine omg 

Moonshine

Ohmanohmanohman. So good. But so crowded. Get here early – as in, like, 30 minutes to an hour early – if you want the first crack at Moonshine’s amazing buffet. Waffles, migas, chicken fried steak, soups, desserts galore… this is what brunch dreams are made of.

10am–2:30pm | $14.95/person

Moonshine
303 Red River St • Austin, TX 78701

Dona Emilia’s

“Traditional south American fare and New Latin cuisine” is how Dona Emilia’s describes their food. Though they usually offer brunch plates like Steak Ranchero, Spanish Chorizo con Huevo, and Cuban French toast, they’ll have a buffet out for Easter Sunday. Expect to see blackened salmon, salads, fruit, empanadas, top sirloin, and more when you move through the line.

10:30am–4pm | $19.95/person

Dona Emilia’s
101 San Jacinto Blvd • Austin, TX

Blue Dahlia Bistro

Blue Dahlia is a European-style bistro with a relaxing atmosphere. The menu is pretty small, but they have lots of tasty baked goods, coffee, sandwiches, and their food is made with very fresh ingredients.

Brunch starts at 10am

Blue Dahlia Bistro
1115 E 11th St • Austin, TX 78702

Blue Star Cafeteria

Blue Star offers what they call the best eggs benedict in town, so try it and decide for yourself. Besides that, they have grits, eggs, fruit, French toast, and more.

10am–3pm

Blue Star Cafeteria
4800 Burnet Rd • Austin, TX

La Cocina de Consuelo

Cheap cheap cheap. That’s not the sound of your marshmallow Peeps coming alive to get revenge on you for eating their baby, that’s just our way of saying this place doesn’t cost much. Load up on Consuelo’s burritos and breakfast tacos in this cozy little restaurant on Burnet that’s easy to miss (Hint: It’s right next to Upper Crust Bakery). The tortillas are fantastic.

8am–2pm

La Cocina de Consuelo
4516 Burnet Rd • Austin, TX

photo / Kristin Hillery  

Curra’s

After the random (and heartbreaking) closings of the locations on Burnet and McNeil a few months ago, fortunately we still have the original Curra’s location on Oltorf. Take a look at their brunch menu by going here.

Brunch starts at 7am

Curra’s
614 E Oltorf St • Austin, TX

The Original Pancake House

Two words: Dutch baby. Okay, maybe you don’t want a giant puffed-out pancake smothered in butter and powdered sugar for brunch, but they have other stuff, including an apple pancake baked to perfection. Corned beef hash, omelets, crepes, and more. Another great option if you’re looking for somewhere to go that won’t cost a fortune.

7:30am–3pm

The Original Pancake House
1700 W Parmer Ln • Austin, TX

Kerbey Lane

Affordable, great for families with young children, and consistently good. They have pancakes, eggs, bacon – the usual stuff, though they do some things with a bit of a twist (like granola pancakes). It’s an ideal place if someone in your party is picky – there’s enough on the menu to please just about anyone.

Open 24 hours; they serve breakfast all day

Kerbey Lane
Various locations
